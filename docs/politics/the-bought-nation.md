# The Bought Nation

> Main canon file for the forgotten migrant human polity of the Meridian Prime Escarpment / Prime Plateau settlement sphere.

**Canon Status:** MAIN CANON, EXPANDABLE

## Core Canon

**The Bought Nation** is the forgotten migrant human polity of the Meridian Prime Escarpment sphere.

It is not a fully sovereign state.

It is a settlement people formed from migrant contracts, survival promises, labor obligations, debt instruments, World Works ownership clauses, Prime Ops survival infrastructure, NCI / contractor route labor, and generations of people who remembered themselves into political existence after Earth forgot them.

Best summary:

**The Bought Nation is the forgotten migrant people of the Escarpment, purchased into survival by World Works, maintained by Prime Ops, worked by NCI and contractor houses, and still trying to become a nation no ledger can own.**

Core wound:

**Earth forgot us. World Works counted us. We remembered ourselves.**

## Origin Wound

The Bought Nation's origin is abandonment disguised as opportunity.

Possible historical sequence:

1. Migrant populations were recruited, relocated, displaced, or economically trapped into Meridian Prime settlement programs.
2. Earth-side oversight faded.
3. World Works absorbed settlement logistics into corporate management.
4. Original migration promises became charter language, then ledger language, then archive fragments.
5. Children born on the Escarpment inherited obligations they did not sign.
6. The people realized no outside polity was coming to remember them properly.
7. They began calling themselves **The Bought Nation**.

Do not write this as simple slavery. The stronger horror is legal entanglement:

- inherited lease dependency
- contract-bound housing
- corporate fuel access
- restricted movement through safety policy
- employment tied to survival infrastructure
- debt instruments framed as opportunity
- corporate-controlled external recognition
- settlement councils permitted but limited
- citizenship without independent gate control

World Works can say:

**No one is owned. Every obligation is contractual.**

The Bought Nation hears:

**A cage with receipts is still a cage.**

## Social Groups

### Charter Families

Descendants of first migrant settlers. They keep old documents proving promises World Works never fully honored.

### Route Families

Families tied to Traverse work, NCI, repair yards, descent labor, and Dry Circuit survival. They are respected because settlements die without them.

### Leaseborn

People born into World Works housing, debt systems, or employment-dependent infrastructure. They are technically Compact citizens but practically inherit obligations.

### Free Yarders

Independent mechanics, haulers, cooks, medics, salvage crews, black-market suppliers, and informal route workers who live in the gaps.

### Paper Citizens

People legally counted as Compact members but physically trapped in remote camps, snow stations, work blocks, or lease settlements. They exist more clearly in ledgers than in politics.

### Reclaimers

A Bought Nation political faction seeking true land ownership, treaty recognition, independent route authority, and an end to corporate settlement dependency.

World Works calls them destabilizers.

Prime Ops may call them a route-security concern.

NCI families may be divided over them.

Do not canonize exact Reclaimer leadership yet.

## Relationships to Major Powers

### World Works

World Works owns or controls the settlement framework. It may celebrate Bought Nation culture publicly while preventing true sovereignty privately.

Official phrase:

**Meridian Human Settlement Compact**

Translation:

**A nation leased back to itself one generation at a time.**

### Prime Ops

Prime Ops is not purely villainous. Prime Ops personnel keep Compact people alive through emergency convoys, snowfield supply systems, road maintenance, and evacuations.

But Prime Ops also enforces the infrastructure of dependency.

Best tension:

**Prime Ops saves the Compact every season and cages it every day.**

### NCI and Contractor Houses

NCI is crucial because it gives the Bought Nation human texture.

NCI may be trusted by Route Families because its workers live among them, marry into them, repair their homes, and know old lies.

NCI is not outside the system. It profits from contracts too. But it preserves field memory World Works cannot fully buy.

Possible NCI saying:

**A contract does not know who carried the wrench.**

### Prime Plateau

The Prime Plateau is a major Bought Nation pressure point because it is descent hinge, work zone, refuge, cloudforest resource, repair corridor, local home, cultural borderland, and a place where local knowledge beats corporate maps.

World Works calls it protected.

Bought Nation people may hear:

**protected from us.**

### Hydropolis

Hydropolis may see Bought Nation identity as legally injured because corporate contracts interfere with personhood, movement, records, and recognition.

Hydropolis sympathy does not equal open support. It may recognize the wound while refusing to let corporate identity contamination enter Hydropolis systems unchecked.

### Anticlinal Craton

The Craton may see the Bought Nation as a people without a true gate.

Craton interpretation:

**The Nation Without Its Own Gate.**

To the Craton, sovereignty means controlling thresholds. If World Works owns the gates, roads, fuel, charters, and permissions, Bought Nation nationhood is wounded but not meaningless.

## Relationship to Charterhold and Ledger Falls

**Charterhold** is the political and logistical heart of the Bought Nation.

**Ledger Falls** is one of its working edges: a border logging town where cloudforest, labor, conservation, and World Works permits collide.

Charterhold says:

**We are a nation.**

Ledger Falls answers:

**Then why is the forest still leased?**
