> Focused cosmology reference for the Seven Witnesses, the seven classical planets, and the official Meridian Witness Calendar.

**Canon Status:** PROPOSED CANON / CREATOR APPROVED FOR CANON ENTRY

## Purpose

This document records the agreed Seven Witness cosmology architecture for Meridian Prime.

The Witness system should feel ancient, layered, damaged, religiously alive, politically domesticated, and cosmologically suspicious.

It should support Meridian Prime's larger themes:

- memory
- route logic
- hidden history
- inherited responsibility
- corporate erasure
- field truth
- sacred ambiguity
- machinery carrying old names forward

This document does not fully explain the Core, the gateway system, Aeonos / Àæonos, Rob Holliday's death-chain, or the larger Book One mysteries.

## Core Framing

The Seven Witnesses are the oldest known symbolic figures in Meridian Prime tradition. Their exact historical nature is unknown.

No living civilization can definitively state whether they were:

- individuals
- philosophical principles
- historical events
- Aeonos / Àæonos leaders
- divine beings
- civilizational archetypes
- something older

The Witnesses are remembered through the official Meridian calendar, planetary nomenclature, ancient records, Aeonic teachings, family customs, Traverse sayings, and old archival traditions.

The Seven Witnesses are:

1. Aioniary
2. Meridiary
3. Chronal
4. Solivaran
5. Pelagoust
6. Umbrielor
7. Hermar

The Witnesses are not confirmed objective gods in current canon. However, some Aeonic religious traditions treat them as divine beings or divine presences.

Safest canon framing:

**The Witnesses may be divine, historical, symbolic, or something older, but current Meridian Prime civilization cannot prove which interpretation is true.**

World Works did not need to ban the Witness Calendar. It domesticated the Witnesses into harmless calendar history.

Official World Works education treats the Seven Witnesses as ancient calendar figures associated with the seven classical planets.

This framing strips the Witnesses of dangerous religious, historical, and cosmological meaning without erasing the names.

Guiding principle:

**World Works does not need to destroy a tradition if it can make people forget why it mattered.**

## Four-Layer Name Architecture

Each Witness name has four overlapping meanings:

1. **Witness Layer:** the oldest symbolic or historical figure.
2. **God-Face Layer:** later religious personification or divine interpretation.
3. **Planet Layer:** the celestial world bearing the Witness name.
4. **Month Layer:** the official Meridian calendar month named after the planet / Witness.

Modern Meridian usage allows a single name to refer to the Witness, planet, month, religious figure, symbolic idea, or cultural period.

This overlap is normal.

Example:

**Umbrielor** may mean the Witness of silence, the Dark World, the month of secrecy, an old symbolic principle, or part of the name Red Umbrielor.

## Seven Planets

The ancient civilizations of Meridian Prime recognized seven major celestial worlds. Each world bears the name of one of the Witnesses.

Five are treated as ordinary or mostly ordinary visible celestial bodies in official astronomy.

Two are suspicious or uncertain.

The seven planets are:

1. **Aioniary:** The Archive World. Associated with time, age, continuity, and records. Traditionally violet and silver.
2. **Meridiary:** The Crossing World. Associated with navigation, Traverses, journeys, direction, meridians, and crossings.
3. **Chronal:** The Crystal World. Associated with memory, records, knowledge, and crystal-imprinting traditions.
4. **Solivaran:** The Beacon World. Associated with illumination, discovery, signals, and beacon symbolism.
5. **Pelagoust:** The Deep World. Associated with depth, exploration, hidden places, and oceanic imagery. Its tradition is mostly lost, and the planet itself may not be an ordinary planet.
6. **Umbrielor:** The Dark World. Associated with silence, secrecy, absence, hidden truth, and withheld records. It is one of the least understood worlds and may not be an ordinary planet.
7. **Hermar:** The Returning World. Associated with return, succession, continuity, inheritance, and renewal.

## Lost Witness Traditions

Five Witness traditions survive in partial form.

Two are mostly lost.

The two lost or flattened Witness traditions are:

- **Solivaran**
- **Pelagoust**

Solivaran survives mostly as beacon symbolism, public illumination imagery, and harmless discovery language.

Pelagoust survives mostly as oceanic, depth, exploration, and hidden-place symbolism.

Their deeper religious, historical, or cosmological meanings have been damaged, erased, or domesticated.

## Official Witness Calendar

The Witness Calendar is the official Meridian calendar.

It consists of seven months:

1. Aioniary
2. Meridiary
3. Chronal
4. Solivaran
5. Pelagoust
6. Umbrielor
7. Hermar

The calendar preserves the order:

**Memory → Crossing → Record → Illumination → Depth → Silence → Return**

The symbolic yearly journey is:

1. Remember what came before.
2. Choose the route.
3. Record the truth.
4. Light the signal.
5. Enter the deep.
6. Endure the silence.
7. Return with responsibility.

Most ordinary Meridians treat the month names as normal civic calendar terms. Religious, archival, family, Traverse, and dissident traditions may preserve deeper meanings.
