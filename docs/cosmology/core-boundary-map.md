# Core Boundary Map

> Reveal-safe cosmology planning document for the Core, Chrono-Loom, time crystals, gateway logic, Aeonos / Àæonos, and Witness symbolism.

**Canon Status:** PROPOSED CANON / CREATOR REVIEW NEEDED

---

## Purpose

This document defines what the Core, Chrono-Loom, time crystals, gateway logic, Aeonos / Àæonos, and Witness symbolism are allowed to suggest without prematurely solving Meridian Prime cosmology.

This document should support, not overwrite:

- `docs/cosmology/seven-witnesses.md`
- `docs/species-guide.md`
- `docs/traverse-system.md`
- `docs/characters/tenet-nocsid-iii.md`
- `docs/legacy/the-last-traverse.md`
- `docs/story/book-one-reveal-spine.md`
- `docs/story/book-one-novella-structure.md`
- `docs/story/book-one-novella-beat-grid.md`

Guiding principle:

**Reveal enough to make the next route necessary, not enough to solve the whole world.**

Do not fully define the Core.

Do not fully define gateway mechanics.

Do not fully explain Aeonos / Àæonos cosmology.

Do not solve Rob Holliday's death-chain.

Do not turn the Seven Witnesses into confirmed objective gods.

Do not reveal everything in Book One.

---

## Core Boundary

The Core should remain:

- real
- hidden
- powerful
- dangerous
- old
- connected to buried infrastructure
- connected to World Works secrecy
- connected to Horus Skelton's obsession
- connected to Rob Holliday's death-chain
- connected to Aeonos / Àæonos mystery
- connected to Book One's hidden formation / route / doorway clue

But the Core should not be fully explained in Book One.

---

## Core Hard Boundaries

The Core must not be:

1. **A literal confirmed god.**
2. **A simple portal.**
3. **Fully understandable by World Works.**
4. **Controllable without consequence.**
5. **Explainable in Book One.**

Optional additional guardrails:

- The Core should not be reduced to a normal power generator.
- The Core should not be reduced to an ordinary alien spaceship.
- The Core should not be fully benevolent.
- The Core should not be fully malicious.
- The Core should not erase field procedure, machinery, human choice, grief, or Traverse Ops danger.

## Core Interpretive Lanes

### World Works Interpretation

World Works treats the Core as something to classify, control, exploit, hide, and bureaucratically rename.

World Works may possess partial technical data, old reports, restricted maps, and corrupted records.

World Works does not fully understand the Core.

### Horus Skelton Interpretation

Horus believes the Core is something he was denied access to, or something he has a right to reclaim.

His obsession grows from resentment, ambition, exclusion, control, and old wounds.

Horus may understand more than most people, but he is not omniscient.

Horus's interpretation may be technically impressive and morally poisoned.

### Rob Holliday Interpretation

Rob does not know everything.

Rob knows enough to be afraid, suspicious, and careful.

Rob may understand that the Core is connected to old routes, Tenet I's legacy, The House Ten Built, Horus's obsession, and something World Works cannot safely admit.

Rob's final moves are practical, not prophetic.

### Tenet Nocsid III Interpretation

Tenet should not begin as a cosmology expert.

Tenet discovers the Core-adjacent mystery through:

- paperwork mismatch
- field procedure
- route contradiction
- old signals
- machinery behavior
- hidden formation evidence
- footprints
- Rob's Last Traverse
- Grandfather Ten's pistol
- The House Ten Built
- crew expertise
- what the land reveals against the map

Tenet should understand the Core first as a field problem, then as a family wound, then as a route toward a larger truth.

### Aeonic Religious Interpretation

Some Aeonic traditions may treat the Core as sacred, forbidden, wounded, divine-adjacent, or connected to the Seven Witnesses.

Do not confirm that the Core is divine.

Do not confirm that the Seven Witnesses created the Core.

### Scientific Interpretation

Researchers may interpret the Core through:

- time crystals
- Chrono-Loom effects
- crystal-imprinting
- buried energy systems
- ancient route infrastructure
- matter / anti-matter interaction
- signal behavior
- old planetary records

Scientific interpretations may be useful without being complete.

## Chrono-Loom Boundary

The Chrono-Loom is a dusk phenomenon where Meridian Prime appears to remember ancient mountains, forgotten coastlines, erased cities, old horizons, or buried history.

Do not reduce the Chrono-Loom to one confirmed explanation yet.

Maintain competing interpretations.

### Field Interpretation

Traverse crews and field people may say Meridian Prime remembers.

The Chrono-Loom may be treated as beautiful, eerie, useful, dangerous, or unlucky depending on route context.

### Scientific Interpretation

Researchers may explain the Chrono-Loom through:

- time-crystal refraction
- atmospheric distortion
- crystal memory imprinting
- old geography echoes
- signal interference
- Core-adjacent field effects

This explanation may be partly correct but incomplete.

### Mythic Interpretation

Aeonic traditions may interpret the Chrono-Loom as planetary memory, Witness-sign, sacred recurrence, or old testimony.

This interpretation may be meaningful without being objectively proven.

### Restricted Possibility

The Chrono-Loom may reveal:

- old routes
- hidden structures
- erased settlements
- buried cities
- ancient coastlines
- Core-adjacent infrastructure
- traces of Aeonos / Àæonos routes
- things official maps deny

Do not fully confirm this in Book One.

## Aeonos / Àæonos Boundary

Aeonos and Àæonos are distinct terms and should not be collapsed.

Known canon:

- **Aeonos** = Aeonolacertian-named home world.
- **Àæonos** = Àæbsconditian-named home world.

Boundary rules:

- Do not fully explain their cosmological relationship in Book One.
- Do not reveal complete matter / anti-matter gateway mechanics yet.
- Do not make them exact mirror worlds unless separately approved.
- Do not treat the terms as interchangeable.
- Characters may misunderstand, mistranslate, or intentionally obscure the distinction.
- Evidence may force the distinction before the explanation arrives.
