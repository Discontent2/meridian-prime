> Fictional worldbuilding reference for the Meridian Prime universe.

This document is the dedicated faction atlas for Meridian Prime. It expands the faction material established in `docs/meridian-prime-bible.md` into a more operational reference for novels, comics, tabletop games, and 16-bit game design.

Factions in Meridian Prime are not only political groups. They are engines of memory, survival, propaganda, grief, and continuity.

## Primary Factions

The primary factions define the largest political, cultural, species-level, and ideological pressures in the Meridian Prime universe. Secondary factions are subgroups, sects, dissident currents, or institutional orders within this broader faction map.

---

### World Works Corporation

**Canon Status:** CONFIRMED CANON

**Faction Type:** orbital corporate regime, continuity authority, post-mission power structure.

**Primary Domain:** The Lodestar, orbital infrastructure, archives, logistics, security, public continuity, corporate governance.

**Public Image:** guardian of mission survival, steward of The Lodestar, protector of continuity.

**Hidden Truth:** World Works began as a cabal that used the Poisoned Contact, the Surface Insurgency, and the Auto-Coup to inherit Meridian Prime before arrival.

**Core Goal:** preserve control over Meridian Prime’s official history, infrastructure, security, archives, and future development.

**Core Fear:** public discovery that human pre-arrival interference helped catalyze the Catastrophic Interaction and weakened the native civilizations before colonization.

**Primary Strength:** numbers, organization, logistics, orbital control, public narrative management.

**Primary Weakness:** its legitimacy depends on a curated version of history.

**Technology Aesthetic:** solarpunk on The Lodestar; corporate-clean surfaces masking coercive control.

**Relationship to The Lodestar:** World Works rules from The Lodestar and treats the old generation ship as headquarters, archive-citadel, symbol, and command body.

**Relationship to Aeonos / àæonos:** publicly suppresses or distorts native-world knowledge; secretly knows enough to exploit native history, technology, and catastrophe records.

**Relationship to Control over Continuity:** originator and enforcer of the doctrine.

**Allies / Possible Allies:** Prime Ops Authority, loyal corporate security forces, compliant educational institutions, compromised archivists, HEO personnel.

**Enemies / Possible Enemies:** Continuity Dissidents, Traverse Loyalists, Livid Æ Revisionists, Broken Brood, Aeonolacertian witnesses, áæbsconditian factions that discover the truth.

**Story Uses:** central antagonist, compromised protector, archive gatekeeper, political machine, orbital ruling power.

**Game Uses:** enemy faction, surveillance systems, checkpoint bosses, corporate zones, orbital endgame, archive-lock puzzles.

**Open Questions:** Which World Works executive knows the most complete version of the Poisoned Contact? Is there a reform faction inside World Works?

### Meridians

**Canon Status:** CONFIRMED CANON

**Faction Type:** surface human population, civic culture, colonist-descended society.

**Primary Domain:** surface human civilization on Meridian Prime.

**Public Image:** ordinary citizens, workers, families, local communities, civic believers in continuity.

**Hidden Truth:** Meridians inherited the benefits and consequences of a pre-arrival crime most of them did not commit and do not know about.

**Core Goal:** survive, build lives, protect families, maintain dignity, and make meaning beneath World Works control.

**Core Fear:** social collapse, memory corruption, corporate punishment, and the possibility that their home was founded on a lie.

**Primary Strength:** willpower.

**Primary Weakness:** limited access to archives, degraded technology, and curated public knowledge.

**Technology Aesthetic:** dieselpunk, rugged surface infrastructure, patched colonial systems, institutional decay with a working engine.

**Relationship to The Lodestar:** The Lodestar is origin myth, corporate headquarters, visible orbital authority, and red-blinking reminder that power watches from above.

**Relationship to Aeonos / àæonos:** most Meridians know little, distorted, or nothing about the full native-world truth.

**Relationship to Control over Continuity:** most Meridians use it as reassurance, resignation, blessing, warning, or social pressure.

**Allies / Possible Allies:** Continuity Dissidents, Traverse Loyalists, Broken Brood, Livid Æ Revisionists, sympathetic Prime Ops workers.

**Enemies / Possible Enemies:** World Works, Prime Ops hardliners, propaganda institutions, any faction that treats ordinary Meridians as guilty inheritors rather than manipulated descendants.

**Story Uses:** protagonists, witnesses, rebels, families, workers, investigators, believers, doubters.

**Game Uses:** player origin, hub populations, side quests, moral stakes, resource scarcity, street-level investigation.

**Open Questions:** What does an ordinary Meridian believe when they see The Lodestar’s red lights? What daily ritual best expresses their willpower?

### Aeonolacertians

**Canon Status:** CONFIRMED CANON

**Faction Type:** native matter-world civilization, saurian survivor species, pre-human civilization.

**Primary Domain:** Matter Aeonos, hidden enclaves, ancestral machinery, memory sites, pre-human ruins, continuity systems.

**Public Image:** myth, forbidden archaeology, hidden native presence, or distorted rumor depending on what World Works allows people to know.

**Hidden Truth:** Aeonolacertians are a surviving native civilization whose population was reduced to roughly 1,000 by the Catastrophic Interaction.

**Core Goal:** survive as a people, preserve memory, understand the truth of the catastrophe, and decide whether humanity is enemy, tool, accident, or possible ally.

**Core Fear:** extinction, cultural dissolution, renewed áæbsconditian conflict, and human erasure of Aeonos history.

**Primary Strength:** brute physical strength and ancient advanced technology hidden beneath primitive-steampunk aesthetics.

**Primary Weakness:** low population, internal factional fracture, and incomplete trust between classes after catastrophe.

**Technology Aesthetic:** primitive steampunk in appearance, extremely advanced in function; brass, stone, pressure, steam, bone resonators, Bore standards, Boiler-Time, and K’al heat logic.

**Relationship to The Lodestar:** The Lodestar is the vessel of the arriving humans and the seat of the regime that profited from the Catastrophic Interaction.

**Relationship to Aeonos / àæonos:** native to Matter Aeonos; historically linked to àæonos through Bridge Script commerce terminals and later catastrophe.

**Relationship to Control over Continuity:** they may see World Works’ doctrine as a crude human imitation of much older continuity principles.

**Allies / Possible Allies:** Broken Brood, Steam-Oracles, Aeolipile, selected Meridians, Livid Æ Revisionists, Amaranth Schola under rare circumstances.

**Enemies / Possible Enemies:** World Works, Null Court hardliners, Veilknives, Sundering Clades if acting against reform, any force that threatens surviving enclaves.

**Story Uses:** ancient allies, wounded witnesses, hidden power, moral counterweight to human colonial history, keepers of pre-human truth.

**Game Uses:** powerful NPC allies, hidden city levels, thermal fortress maps, heavy units, pressure technology puzzles.

**Open Questions:** Are the Aeonolacertians hidden, integrated, imprisoned, or mythologized in the present day? Which faction first accepts that humans may have caused the catastrophe?

### áæbsconditians / Absconditians

**Canon Status:** CONFIRMED CANON

**Faction Type:** native anti-matter civilization, stealth-dominant survivor species, anti-life civilization.

**Primary Domain:** anti-matter àæonos, stealth networks, cyber-resonance systems, memory-signal operations, cross-world influence.

**Public Image:** unknown, demonized, mythologized, or classified as impossible by human authorities.

**Hidden Truth:** áæbsconditians are a surviving anti-matter civilization, commonly simplified in human records as Absconditians, whose population was reduced to roughly 1,000 by the Catastrophic Interaction.

**Core Goal:** survive, preserve áæbsconditian continuity, understand or exploit the truth of the Catastrophic Interaction, and determine how to respond to Matter Aeonos and humanity.

**Core Fear:** extinction, contamination by matter-side continuity, historical falsification, and permanent loss of àæonos sovereignty.

**Primary Strength:** stealth.

**Primary Weakness:** low population, factional secrecy, ideological rigidity among ruling elements, and dependence on hidden influence rather than open force.

**Technology Aesthetic:** cyberpunk; black glass, magenta/amaranth light, teal optics, invasive interfaces, neural lattices, anti-matter containment architecture, Antimatter Lattice Script, and Bridge Script resonance logic.

**Relationship to The Lodestar:** The Lodestar is a human power station whose ruling corporation exploited and distorted anti-matter communication logic.

**Relationship to Aeonos / àæonos:** native to anti-matter àæonos; historically linked to Aeonos through commerce, Bridge Script, and later catastrophe.

**Relationship to Control over Continuity:** they may see it as evidence that humans are not merely colonizers, but continuity thieves.

**Allies / Possible Allies:** Livid Æ Revisionists, Amaranth Schola, Madrigalists, selected Meridians, Aeolipile, Broken Brood.

**Enemies / Possible Enemies:** World Works, Aeonolacertian hardliners, Null Court rivals, Veilknife splinter missions, anyone threatening the Last Thousand.

**Story Uses:** stealth antagonists, secret allies, anti-life philosophers, hidden witnesses, memory intruders, keepers of alternate truth.

**Game Uses:** stealth systems, signal puzzles, phase movement enemies, cyber-resonance zones, hidden route mechanics.

**Open Questions:** How do áæbsconditians cross-influence from àæonos to Aeonos? Which faction first proves the official catastrophe narrative is incomplete?

### Prime Ops Authority

**Canon Status:** PROVISIONAL CANON

**Faction Type:** transit authority, security bureaucracy, surface-orbit gatekeeper.

**Primary Domain:** Prime Ops Space Port, shuttle access, surface-orbit traffic, restricted transit, cargo inspection, executive movement.

**Public Image:** transportation authority and security administration for the only legitimate route between Meridian Prime’s surface and The Lodestar.

**Hidden Truth:** Prime Ops is a choke point where World Works can quietly control who reaches orbit, what leaves the surface, and which records or people disappear into procedure.

**Core Goal:** maintain controlled access between the surface and The Lodestar.

**Core Fear:** unauthorized transit, smuggled evidence, dissident infiltration, native contact incidents, anomaly contamination, and breakdown of orbital authority.

**Primary Strength:** gatekeeping power.

**Primary Weakness:** every gate creates smugglers, false papers, black-market routes, and compromised insiders.

**Technology Aesthetic:** dieselpunk surface security blended with older shuttle systems and polished corporate orbital interfaces.

**Relationship to The Lodestar:** Prime Ops is the surface throat of The Lodestar’s orbital authority.

**Relationship to Aeonos / àæonos:** deliberately located far from known anomalous or native activity to preserve non-entangled operational stability.

**Relationship to Control over Continuity:** Prime Ops turns doctrine into checkpoints.

**Allies / Possible Allies:** World Works, corporate security, HEOs, compliant customs officials.

**Enemies / Possible Enemies:** Continuity Dissidents, smugglers, Traverse Loyalists, native agents, whistleblowers, desperate Meridians.

**Story Uses:** border crossings, heists, interrogations, shuttle drama, bureaucratic menace, first trip to The Lodestar.

**Game Uses:** checkpoint hub, stealth infiltration, forged-document quests, shuttle launch sequence, restricted-zone progression.

**Open Questions:** How independent is Prime Ops Authority from World Works? Which shuttle-yard technician knows too much?

### Traverse Loyalists

**Canon Status:** PROVISIONAL CANON

**Faction Type:** old mission traditionalists, archive protectors, anti-corporate civic memory faction.

**Primary Domain:** hidden civic circles, old mission families, archival fragments, ceremonial memory of The Traverse.

**Public Image:** traditionalists, eccentrics, historical societies, or harmless mission romantics.

**Hidden Truth:** some Traverse Loyalists believe the original mission was betrayed by the cabal that became World Works.

**Core Goal:** recover the original mission principles, restore the pre-corporate civic legacy of The Lodestar, and expose what was altered during the Auto-Coup.

**Core Fear:** that no clean version of the original mission survives and that even Ernest Skelton’s legacy may be compromised.

**Primary Strength:** inherited memory, archival obsession, old family networks, symbolic legitimacy.

**Primary Weakness:** nostalgia, fragmentation, limited power against World Works institutions, and possible blindness to native-world culpability.

**Technology Aesthetic:** old shipboard records, analog archives, preserved uniforms, ritualized Traverse-era tools.

**Relationship to The Lodestar:** they see The Lodestar as a sacred mission vessel stolen by World Works.

**Relationship to Aeonos / àæonos:** some may ignore native history while focusing on human mission betrayal; others may become key allies in exposing the broader truth.

**Relationship to Control over Continuity:** they oppose World Works’ version of continuity but may still believe continuity itself is sacred.

**Allies / Possible Allies:** Continuity Dissidents, Meridians, honest archivists, old mission families, sympathetic shuttle crews.

**Enemies / Possible Enemies:** World Works, Prime Ops hardliners, corporate educators, anyone who reveals that the original mission itself may have been compromised.

**Story Uses:** clue givers, old conspirators, flawed mentors, archive hunters, ideological rivals to younger dissidents.

**Game Uses:** lore unlock faction, archive quests, old-key access, ship-history puzzles, hidden safehouses.

**Open Questions:** What did Ernest Skelton know? Which Traverse Loyalist archive contradicts the official Auto-Coup record?

### Continuity Dissidents

**Canon Status:** PROVISIONAL CANON

**Faction Type:** surface resistance, truth network, anti-continuity underground.

**Primary Domain:** Meridian surface communities, underground networks, banned archives, memory-corruption incidents, illegal broadcasts.

**Public Image:** conspiracy theorists, criminals, cultists, destabilizers, or dangerous romantics, depending on World Works propaganda.

**Hidden Truth:** many dissidents have noticed real contradictions in public history and planetary memory.

**Core Goal:** expose the gap between public history and hidden history.

**Core Fear:** memory erasure, social isolation, false confession, disappearance into Prime Ops procedure, and learning that the truth is worse than the lie.

**Primary Strength:** persistence, improvisation, local trust, forbidden curiosity.

**Primary Weakness:** factional infighting, unreliable evidence, paranoia, infiltration, and limited resources.

**Technology Aesthetic:** dieselpunk pirate media, patched receivers, hidden printing devices, archive contraband, improvised signal gear, Glitch Script tags.

**Relationship to The Lodestar:** they see The Lodestar as the red-blinking eye of corporate control, but also as the likely container of truth.

**Relationship to Aeonos / àæonos:** most begin with rumors, fragments, and anomaly stories; some may become bridge figures between humans and native factions.

**Relationship to Control over Continuity:** they treat the phrase as a cage disguised as comfort.

**Allies / Possible Allies:** Meridians, Traverse Loyalists, Livid Æ Revisionists, Broken Brood, sympathetic Prime Ops workers, rogue HEOs.

**Enemies / Possible Enemies:** World Works, Prime Ops Authority, corporate informants, false dissident cells, factions that weaponize the truth without protecting ordinary people.

**Story Uses:** investigators, rebels, whistleblowers, unreliable witnesses, street-level entry into the larger conspiracy.

**Game Uses:** quest-givers, secret radio network, graffiti clues, black-market upgrades, alternate endings.

**Open Questions:** Which Continuity Dissident first connects Meridian memory corruption to Aeonos history? Which cell is actually a World Works trap?

## Native Secondary Factions

The Aeonolacertians and áæbsconditians are not monolithic civilizations. The Catastrophic Interaction, the Poisoned Contact, and the reduction of both populations to roughly 1,000 survivors fractured their societies into survivor factions, ideological orders, and specialist sects.

## Faction Strength Balance

No faction is strongest in every category.

**World Works** dominates open war through numbers and logistics.

**Aeonolacertians** dominate direct physical confrontation through brute strength.

**áæbsconditians** dominate covert conflict through stealth and infiltration.

**Meridians** dominate prolonged moral, emotional, and psychological struggle through willpower.

This balance keeps conflict dependent on terrain, timing, knowledge, morale, access, technology, and continuity.

## Faction Conflict Engines

### The Archive War

World Works, Traverse Loyalists, Continuity Dissidents, Livid Æ Revisionists, and Amaranth Schola scholars all want records, but each defines truth differently.

### The Survivor Argument

Aeonolacertians and áæbsconditians both survived catastrophe in tiny numbers. Their factions disagree over whether survival requires revenge, isolation, reform, alliance, or confession.

### The Bridge Script Revelation

Quantum Meridian Script terminals prove that Aeonolacertians and áæbsconditians once maintained commerce and communication. Any faction that controls surviving Bridge Script evidence can reshape the entire moral history of Meridian Prime.

### The Lodestar Question

Is The Lodestar humanity’s sacred cradle, World Works’ stolen throne, a prison for the truth, or all three?

### The Meridian Innocence Question

Ordinary Meridians did not commit the original crime, but they inherited its benefits. Native factions and dissidents may disagree over what justice should demand from them.
