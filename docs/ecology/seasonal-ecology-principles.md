# Seasonal Ecology Principles

> Canon foundation for how Meridian Prime ecology behaves across dry, cataract, haboon, wet, flood, freeze, and drawdown conditions.

**Canon Status:** MAIN CANON, EXPANDABLE

## Purpose

This file promotes the strongest curated ecology principles from World Building Department sandbox development into main canon.

It does not canonize every sandbox creature, plant, fungus, food, medicine, intoxicant, or contraband item.

It canonizes the **system logic** that should guide future ecology development.

Core principle:

**On Meridian Prime, ecology is not scenery. It is route pressure with a metabolism.**

---

## Central Seasonal Rule

Meridian Prime's ecology must be designed around seasonal transformation.

The planet's roads, basins, riverbeds, escarpments, wetlands, crystal flats, and lowlands change state across season cycles.

Every important ecological entry should answer:

**What does this lifeform do when the roads drown?**

This question applies to:

- megafauna
- megaflora
- mega-fungi
- food plants
- mushrooms
- medicines
- toxins
- contraband species
- sacred ecology
- route hazards
- settlement resources

If an ecological element does not change route logic, settlement survival, map accuracy, faction behavior, or seasonal risk, it probably belongs in background texture rather than central canon.

---

## Megafauna Rule

A Meridian Prime megafauna entry is not complete until it documents:

| Required Detail | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Dry-season behavior | How crews, maps, convoys, settlements, hunters, or poachers encounter it when roads exist. |
| Wet-season survival strategy | How it survives when roads, riverbeds, basins, and lowlands flood, freeze, or transform. |
| Map hazard role | Why cartographers, Prime Ops, NCI, Traverse crews, smugglers, or local guides care. |
| Cultural interpretation | How humans, Hydropolis, the Craton, Free Scabs, route families, or other groups misread, mythologize, protect, hunt, or exploit it. |

Canon line:

**A Meridian Prime megafauna entry is not complete until it explains what the animal does when the roads drown.**

Design notes:

- Megafauna should not be generic monsters.
- They are seasonal terrain actors.
- Their movements can close routes, predict floods, reveal wrecks, hide pirate lanes, or turn accurate maps into lies.
- Their presence should affect logistics, food chains, law, rituals, route planning, smuggling, and settlement risk.

---

## Mega-Flora Rule

Mega-flora answers a different seasonal question:

**What remains rooted when the world changes shape around it?**

A Meridian Prime mega-flora entry is not complete until it documents:

| Required Detail | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Dry-season form | How crews, routes, settlements, harvesters, or scavengers encounter it when land is exposed. |
| Wet-season survival strategy | How it survives flooding, snow burial, drowning, freezing, or seasonal transformation. |
| Travel / settlement effect | How it changes movement, docking, logging, signal flow, shelter, food, water, or hazard. |
| Cultural interpretation | Who claims, fears, harvests, protects, worships, taxes, maps, or refuses to map it. |

Canon line:

**When the roads drown, the animals move. The plants decide what remains.**

Design notes:

- Mega-flora should not be generic large plants.
- It should create landmarks, barriers, hidden-water clues, false ground, sacred groves, logging conflict, shelter, food sovereignty, signal interference, or docking hazards.
- It may determine which routes remain possible when maps fail.

---

## Mega-Fungi Rule

Mega-fungi asks:

**What feeds on the old world and decides what becomes useful again?**

A Meridian Prime mega-fungi entry is not complete until it documents:

| Required Detail | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Dry-season form | Dormant crust, spore dust, buried mycelium, mineral shell, wreck rot, or hidden vein. |
| Wet-season bloom | How it erupts, spreads, floats, infects, glows, filters, softens, or becomes useful. |
| Route / settlement effect | How it damages roads, machines, bodies, signals, food stores, archives, maps, or structures. |
| Cultural interpretation | Who harvests, fears, weaponizes, worships, regulates, denies, or mistakes it for safety. |

Canon line:

**Mega-fungi are the treaty between dead things and the next season.**

Design notes:

- Mega-fungi should carry ecology of rot, salvage, medicine, contamination, memory, spores, archive danger, signal weirdness, and terrible generosity.
- It may mark wrecks, dead roads, contaminated depots, hidden water, death-sites, or falsely safe terrain.
- It should rarely be merely decorative.

---

## Food Ecology Rule

Meridian Prime food is never only food.

A Meridian Prime food plant, mushroom, fungus, fruit, nut, legume, vegetable, or foodlike ecology is not complete until it answers:

- who eats it
- who fears it
- who taxes it
- who heals with it
- who abuses it
- who smuggles it
- who calls it sacred
- who misidentifies its poisonous cousin
- what season makes it safer or more dangerous
- what authority is allowed to call it safe

Canon line:

**On Meridian Prime, food is never just food. It is proof that something survived the last season and intends to charge rent.**

Guardrail:

Do not over-detail real-world-style preparation methods, poison recipes, dosage, extraction, or pharmacological procedure. Keep food, medicine, intoxicant, and poison worldbuilding fictional, story-facing, and consequence-driven.

---

## Mushroom Rule

Everyday mushrooms are distinct from mega-fungi.

Mega-fungi are ecological forces.

Mushrooms are the fruiting bodies, caps, shelves, puffballs, nodules, stalks, and truffles that people pick, cook, fear, trade, regulate, ban, ritualize, or misidentify.

A Meridian Prime mushroom is not complete until it answers:

- who can safely identify it
- what it resembles
- what season makes the mistake deadly
- who eats it
- who forbids it
- who harvests it for fictional medicine
- who abuses it recreationally or ritually
- what map clue it gives to terrain, water, rot, death, contamination, or hidden infrastructure

Canon line:

**On Meridian Prime, mushrooms are the fruit of hidden decisions. By the time a cap appears, the ground has been planning for weeks.**

## Political Ecology Rule

Ecology on Meridian Prime should create jurisdiction.

Major ecological resources or hazards may be interpreted as:

- route hazard
- ration base
- sacred ecology
- taxable harvest
- guest-law object
- black-market opportunity
- civic contamination risk
- corporate resource claim
- protected habitat
- smuggling cover
- proof of settlement
- evidence of map failure
- survival right

Do not treat ecological canon as neutral nature trivia.

A lifeform matters when it changes what people can eat, where they can travel, who has authority, who gets care, what maps must admit, or what law tries to hide.

---

## Seasonal Terms and Map-Relevant Phases

Future ecology may refer to seasonal phases such as:

- dry season
- cataract season
- haboon season
- wet season
- flood season
- freeze / snowfield period
- drawdown season
- wet retreat
- dry return

These are not all globally uniform. Different regions may experience or name the same cycle differently.

Do not assume every biome follows identical timing.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Do not make ecology decorative only.
- Do not make every creature a monster encounter.
- Do not make every fungus a horror threat.
- Do not make every plant edible or medicinal.
- Do not overpopulate canon with hundreds of names before story need exists.
- Do not promote entire sandbox catalogues without curation.
- Do not provide real poison, intoxicant, dosage, extraction, or medical instructions.
- Do not make World Works always wrong about ecology, but do let its labels flatten local knowledge.
- Do not make local knowledge infallible.
- Do not forget that seasonal change should make maps lie.
