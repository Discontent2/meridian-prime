## Core Identity

Craton mech culture grows from heavy labor, public witness, and Geodeum spectacle.

Craton mechs are not primarily sleek military machines. They are usually modified construction, quarry, loading, lifting, hauling, excavation, and industrial support machines that have been ritualized, ornamented, and adapted for Mech Geodeum use.

Craton society may use steampunk visual language without being technologically primitive. Brass, pressure gauges, piston banks, blackened iron, rivets, chain drums, vents, analog displays, ceremonial plates, and visible linkages are cultural style, maintenance philosophy, and public legibility.

Core lines:

```text
Craton mech culture begins with labor and ends in witness.
```

```text
A Craton mech is never allowed to forget what it was built to carry.
```

```text
The machine is not only a tool. In the Geodeum, it becomes testimony with joints.
```

## Cultural Principle

Craton mech culture treats machines as public extensions of burden, status, labor history, crew reputation, and Stonebound identity.

A Mech Geodeum contest is rarely just machine against machine.

It may also be:

```text
crew against crew
work lineage against work lineage
Crown claim against Crown claim
old quarry record against public spectacle
faction pride against social embarrassment
debt history against witnessed consequence
operator reputation against inherited machine memory
```

The crowd may cheer impact, but the culture remembers provenance.

## Mech Culture Roles

### Operators

Operators are the public face of Mech Geodeum machines.

They may be Stonebound, hired champions, claim-bearers, debt representatives, ceremonial proxies, or skilled industrial operators pulled into spectacle.

Operator identity is never assumed human. Craton machines may be fitted for Aeonolacertian, Tyranosapien, Velocisapien, or other non-human body plans as appropriate.

### Rig Crews

A mech's crew is often as culturally important as the operator.

Crew roles may include:

```text
rigwrights
pressure keepers
chain tenders
plate fitters
stone-dust cleaners
balance callers
seal readers
chime keepers
ring patchers
cradle fitters
```

These are cultural / story roles, not real-world engineering instructions.

### House or Yard Lineages

Some mechs carry the reputation of a work yard, quarry house, rig crew, family workshop, Stonebound circle, Crown sponsor, or civic faction.

A machine's lineage can matter as much as its operator.

### The Crowd

The crowd is part of the machine's public meaning.

A crowd may recognize old serial plates, chant a machine's former work-name, boo a false repaint, or treat a familiar dent as evidence.

In Craton mech culture, a crowd does not need to understand engineering to understand insult.

## Mech Types

These categories are broad story-facing types, not complete engineering taxonomies.

### Lift-Rig

A heavy loader or lifting frame adapted for arena use.

Best for:

```text
pinning
lifting
blocking
ring control
public burden imagery
Crown Stonebound spectacle
```

Stonejaw's Lift Rig belongs here.

### Quarry Walker

A stone-yard or quarry machine adapted for heavy terrain and impact endurance.

Best for:

```text
slow pressure
stone-floor traction
heavy strikes
dust cloud spectacle
old work-site memory
```

### Gate Setter

A machine derived from gate placement, wall setting, or barrier handling equipment.

Best for:

```text
threshold contests
passage-right symbolism
blocking lanes
opening or closing ritual space
```

### Haul Frame

A cargo or ore-hauling platform adapted for endurance and mass.

Best for:

```text
long contests
weight trials
crowd intimidation
burden imagery
```

### Brace Engine

A stabilizing, shoring, or support machine adapted to resist collapse, impact, or crowd-favored momentum.

Best for:

```text
defensive contests
trial-by-endurance scenes
holding a line
refusing movement
```

### Scaffolder

A machine derived from scaffold, bridge, or height-work systems.

Best for:

```text
vertical arena movement
repair culture
ring manipulation
sudden elevation changes
```

## Mech Geodeum Culture

A Mech Geodeum is not merely a metal fight pit.

It is a public arena where industrial machines carry social meaning.

A Mech Geodeum may stage:

```text
Crown contests
machine inheritance disputes
crew honor trials
public repair rites
debt contests
operator accusation scenes
proxy faction contests
passage-right machine ordeals
spectacle matches
ritualized machine retirement
```

In a Mech Geodeum, victory does not always mean the better machine won.

It may mean:

```text
the crowd accepted the story
the old machine's name survived
a crew's claim became harder to deny
a public embarrassment was made useful
a burden found a new operator
```

## Machine Inheritance

Craton mech culture treats inherited machines as dangerous public objects.

When an operator takes on a famous rig, they inherit more than hardware.

They may inherit:

```text
old debts
crowd expectations
unfinished claims
crew loyalty
machine-name pressure
prior operator reputation
sponsor obligations
public suspicion
ritual burden
```

Stonejaw's Lift Rig is the established example: Dragulies acquired it from the previous Crown holder named Stonejaw. This addendum does not define how or why that transfer happened.

## Maintenance as Ritual

Maintenance may be public, private, practical, ceremonial, or all four.

Craton culture may treat visible repair as honor, not shame.

Common cultural practices:

```text
leaving old work scars visible
ringing impact bells after a major contest
recording crew marks on replacement plates
naming dents after public events
keeping prior operator marks unless formally removed
polishing gauges before contests
scraping stone dust into witness bowls
mounting broken ring plates in crew yards
```

These are cultural images, not required universal customs.

## Visual Language

Craton mechs should generally feel:

```text
heavy
legible
worked-on
ritualized
industrial
brass-and-iron
stone-dusted
pressure-driven
publicly repaired
beautiful through burden
```

Avoid defaulting to:

```text
sleek military robots
smooth futuristic mecha
clean power armor
human-shaped armor suits

```

## Relationship To Aeonolacertian Design

Craton mech culture must account for non-human operators.

When designed for Aeonolacertian or Tyranosapien operators, machines must not assume human anatomy.

Possible accommodations:

```text
tail channels
wide hip clearance
snout-safe view gaps
clawed foot braces
non-human shoulder harnesses
forearm yokes
operator cradles instead of seats
standing or semi-standing posture
larger side-entry hatches
```

This is especially important for Dragulies and Stonejaw's Lift Rig.

## Relationship To Dragulies

Dragulies does not define all Craton mech culture.

He is the most visible current anchor for the concept because of his association with Stonejaw's Lift Rig, his Crown Stonebound status, and his public relationship to Geodeums.

Safe use:

```text
Dragulies as famous Crown Stonebound operator
Stonejaw's Lift Rig as inherited loader-type rig
Dragulies's rig as example of machine legacy
Mech Geodeum as extension of his public myth
```

Unsafe use without later approval:

```text
Dragulies as inventor of Craton mech culture
Dragulies as owner of all Crown mech customs
Dragulies as source of complete Craton law
Stonejaw's Lift Rig as proof of hidden cosmology
```
