# Craton Geodeums

**Canon Status:** Owner-Approved Canon Concept  
**Scope:** Craton / Geodeum architecture / public contest and judgment spaces  
**Use Level:** Canon foundation; individual sites, rituals, and legal procedures require later review  
**Protected Mystery Status:** Does not define gateway mechanics, Dry Gate truth, the Core, Contact Frame mechanics, protected route topology, or Book One answers.

## Core Definition

A **Craton Geodeum** is a stone-governed public contest and judgment chamber where combat, testimony, accusation, ritual passage, social legitimacy, and witnessed consequence may be decided under Craton authority or Craton-adjacent custom.

Some Geodeums are gladiator colosseum arenas.

Some Geodeums are trial-by-stone chambers.

The worst ones are both.

Core line:

```text
A Craton Geodeum is where conflict stops being private and becomes stone.
```

Alternate public saying:

```text
The crowd watches. The stone listens. Survival is sometimes mistaken for truth.
```

## Essential Identity

A Geodeum is not merely a crystal cave or a decorated fighting pit.

It is a bounded stone space where conflict becomes public, witnessed, and difficult to deny.

A Geodeum may decide or display:

```text
status
guilt
passage-right
social legitimacy
ritual standing
public accusation
champion authority
faction dispute
punishment by ordeal
classification pressure
```

A Geodeum does not need to be morally right.

It only needs to be recognized, endured, witnessed, and recorded by stone, crowd, custom, or authority.

## Two Primary Modes

### 1. Arena Geodeum

An **Arena Geodeum** is the colosseum mode: public, loud, performative, and physically dangerous.

Use cases:

```text
champion combat
status disputes
proxy faction contests
punishment spectacle
ceremonial duels
combat trials
public proving grounds
crowd-pressure scenes
```

Common features:

```text
tiered seating
crystalline or mineral walls
marked combat circles
gate lifts
raised stone plates
narrowing ring platforms
resonance drums
stone chimes
old claw marks
impact scars
repaired crystal ribs
mineral dust underfoot
```

### 2. Judgment Geodeum

A **Judgment Geodeum** is the trial-by-stone mode: solemn, quieter, and often more frightening than the arena form.

Use cases:

```text
accusation scenes
truth ordeals
passage disputes
naming pressure
classification disputes
sentence chambers
witness confrontations
legal or ritual ordeal
```

Common features:

```text
standing stones
central slab or ring
resonant floor
witness dais
mineral growths that respond to contact, voice, impact, or ritual position
acoustics that make every word feel binding
pressure doors that open only after the rite concludes
stone channels that mark where each participant stood
```

## Additional Subtypes

### Threshold Geodeum

A Geodeum tied to passage-right, border pressure, route legitimacy, or contested approach.

### Reliquary Geodeum

An older or more ceremonial Geodeum, less public and more sacred, archival, or ancestral in tone.

### Pit Geodeum

A harsher punitive form, rougher and more brutal, often treated as a place where public punishment hides under the language of proof.

### Crown Geodeum

An elite or formal Geodeum used for state-level, faction-level, or high ceremonial contests.

## Aeonolacertian Spatial Fit

Geodeums should not feel like human arenas with crystal decoration.

They belong to a world where Aeonolacertian and other non-human body plans exist in public space.

A Geodeum may account for:

```text
tail clearance
claw traction
low crouch lanes
sprint arcs
leap platforms
perch ledges
brace points
mineral ramps
vertical grip surfaces
circling body language
display structures under ritual lighting
```

This does not mean every Geodeum is exclusively Aeonolacertian, but Geodeum architecture should not erase saurian anatomy when Aeonolacertian use is relevant.

## Story Function

A Geodeum is useful when a story needs conflict to become public and binding.

Strong story uses:

```text
a fight that is also a trial
a trial that becomes a fight
a public accusation nobody can walk back
a champion forced to carry a faction's shame
a rigged ordeal meant to legalize an unjust outcome
a character winning the fight but losing the judgment
a witness forced to stand where the stone can remember them
a passage dispute settled through ritual contest
a classification dispute made physical
```

Best dramatic principle:

```text
In a Geodeum, victory may prove strength. It does not always prove innocence.
```

## Tone

Geodeums should feel:

```text
ancient but active
public but intimate
ritualized but dangerous
lawful but potentially cruel
spectacular but binding
sacred in posture, political in use
stone-heavy, witness-heavy, consequence-heavy
```

They should carry the feeling that the architecture has hosted too many arguments to believe in clean answers.
