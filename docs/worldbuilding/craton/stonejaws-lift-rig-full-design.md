# Stonejaw's Lift Rig: Full Design Document

**Canon Status:** Owner-Approved Canon Design Document  
**Applies To:** Dragulies, Crown Stonebound; Craton Geodeums; Mech Geodeums; Craton Stonebound culture  
**Primary Machine:** Stonejaw's Lift Rig  
**Machine Type:** Loader-type Geodeum mech / modified heavy labor frame  
**Operator Association:** Dragulies, Crown Stonebound  
**Legacy Source:** Acquired from the previous Crown holder named Stonejaw  
**Primary Canon Patch:** `docs/worldbuilding/craton/geodeum-mech-arenas-and-stonejaws-lift-rig.md`  
**Use Level:** Canon design foundation; exact engineering, control diagrams, weapon systems, and historical contest details require later review  
**Protected Mystery Boundary:** Does not define complete Craton law, Craton sovereignty, gateway mechanics, Dry Gate truth, the Core, Contact Frame mechanics, protected route topology, or Book One answers.

## Core Identity

**Stonejaw's Lift Rig** is Dragulies's famous loader-type Geodeum mech: a modified heavy labor frame with Craton steampunk industrial styling, inherited from the previous Crown holder, Stonejaw.

It is not a sleek military machine.

It is a construction-born arena relic, a public inheritance, a machine with work still trapped in its bones.

Core lines:

```text
Stonejaw's Lift Rig is the burden with arms.
```

```text
It was built to lift stone. The Geodeum taught it to lift verdicts.
```

```text
Every plate on the rig looks like it remembers being useful before it became famous.
```

## Silhouette

Stonejaw's Lift Rig should read instantly as a heavy loader frame repurposed for Geodeum spectacle.

Primary silhouette traits:

```text
broad industrial torso
high shoulder gantry
oversized forward loader arms
wide braced legs
heavy counterweight back
operator cradle set deep in the chest and shoulder structure
large pressure cylinders along arms and thighs
stone-scarred lift forks or clamp assemblies
low, forward-leaning working posture
cage-like upper frame around the pilot zone
visible steam / pressure vent stacks
rear balance mass echoing a tail-counterweight relationship
```

The rig should feel like it can shove a stone gate back into place, lift a collapsed scaffold, or pin a rival machine against the arena wall while the crowd mistakes force for justice.

Visual read from a distance:

```text
A hulking brass-and-iron loader mech with raised industrial arms, a deep operator cradle, a counterweighted back, and the posture of a work machine forced into ceremony.
```

## Loader-Frame Anatomy

Stonejaw's Lift Rig descends from construction / stone-yard heavy equipment.

Its anatomy should preserve that origin.

### Torso Frame

The torso is a reinforced loader body, more workhorse than knight.

Features:

```text
boxy pressure-core housing
riveted chest plates
stone-yard serial scars partly covered by Crown markings
front access plates with old repair seams
large central load spine
brace rings around the operator cradle
Craton sigils stamped into replacement panels
```

### Loader Arms

The arms are the identity of the rig.

They should be huge, forward-heavy, and descended from lifting equipment.

Features:

```text
oversized hydraulic / pressure pistons
multi-jointed lift assemblies
stone clamps, fork plates, or reinforced grip paddles
ceremonial edge guards
impact scars from arena use
chain housings and pressure cables
old lifting marks still visible beneath Geodeum paint
```

The arms are not elegant swords. They are public burden made mechanical.

### Legs and Feet

The legs are wide, braced, and built for load-bearing.

Features:

```text
heavy piston thighs
shorter power stride compared to lighter arena machines
wide claw-like industrial stabilizer feet
stone-grip cleats
dust guards
ankle braces made from quarry hardware
impact-dented shin shields
```

The stance should look like it can absorb a charge without asking the audience for permission.

### Back / Counterweight

The back carries the rig's industrial soul.

Features:

```text
heavy counterweight block
pressure tanks
vent stacks
coiled chain drums
old quarry balance plates
arena heraldry bolted over earlier work markings
```

The counterweight should visually echo Dragulies's own heavy tail logic without becoming a literal tail.

### Head / Sensor Assembly

Stonejaw's Lift Rig does not need a humanoid head.

Preferred options:

```text
no true head, only a raised cage-and-lamp sensor crown
brass lamp array over the operator cradle
protective brow plate shaped by accident into a grim stonejaw silhouette
pressure-gauge face cluster
iron grille that makes the machine seem to scowl
```

Avoid making it look like a human knight helmet. The rig is an industrial animal of posture, not a person-shaped robot.

## Cockpit / Operator Fit For A Tyranosapien

Stonejaw's Lift Rig must be designed around a Tyranosapien operator, not a human pilot.

Dragulies is large-bodied, saurian, heavy-tailed, broad-chested, and fully non-human in body plan.

The operator station should account for:

```text
large saurian torso
forward-leaning stance
tail accommodation or rear tail channel
wide hip and thigh clearance
non-human shoulder mechanics
shorter but powerful Tyranosapien arms
clawed hands
large clawed feet
neck and snout clearance
restraint systems that do not assume human anatomy
```

### Operator Cradle

The cockpit is better described as an **operator cradle** than a seat.

Features:

```text
standing / braced semi-standing operator posture
chest brace and shoulder harness fitted to Tyranosapien anatomy
tail channel or rear counterbalance slot
claw-grip control handles
forearm pressure levers sized for strong shorter arms
jaw-clear viewing gap or angled viewport
snout-safe breathing clearance
heavy foot braces for clawed feet
side-entry hatch wide enough for saurian body shape
```

The rig should look operated, wrestled, and leaned into rather than sat inside.

### Control Language

Controls should feel tactile, industrial, and ceremonial without becoming a real-world instruction set.

Story-facing controls:

```text
brass pressure levers
claw-grip toggles
forearm yokes
foot brace plates
load-balance gauges
stone-dust pressure dials
chain-tension bells
witness chime pull
Crown seal lock
```

Do not define real engineering or usable machinery operation.

## Arena Modifications

Stonejaw's Lift Rig was not born as a weapon. The Geodeum made it into one.

Arena modifications should still preserve its labor-machine ancestry.

Visual / story modifications:

```text
reinforced loader arms
arena plating over quarry frame
ceremonial Crown Stonebound sigils
stone-scored shoulder guards
removable ring plates from earlier matches
pressure-release vent flares for crowd visibility
impact bells that ring when the frame takes major force
protective operator cage
clamp guards
stabilizer cleats for Geodeum stone floors
old work serial marks deliberately left visible
```

The rig should not be overloaded with blades, guns, missiles, or sleek military parts.

If it harms, it harms by:

```text
weight
pressure
pinning
lifting
blocking
shoving
forcing space
controlling the ring
turning industrial purpose into public threat
```

## Steampunk Industrial Styling

Craton steampunk is style language, not technological limitation.

Stonejaw's Lift Rig should visually emphasize:

```text
brass pressure gauges
blackened iron frame
riveted plates
visible piston banks
steam / pressure vents
chain drums
analog dials
hand-stamped plates
exposed linkages
grease-darkened joints
stone dust
ceremonial metalwork
work scars left intact
```

The rig can be highly capable, but it should never look frictionless, glossy, or magically clean.

Its beauty is burden made legible.

## Inherited Stonejaw Legacy

Stonejaw's Lift Rig came from the previous Crown holder, Stonejaw.

This is canon.

What is established:

```text
Stonejaw was the previous Crown holder.
Stonejaw's Lift Rig belonged to Stonejaw before Dragulies acquired it.
Dragulies's use of the rig carries legacy and public pressure.
The machine's name preserves Stonejaw's presence in the arena.
```

What is not established yet:

```text
whether Stonejaw is alive, dead, missing, retired, disgraced, honored, or exiled
how Dragulies acquired the rig
whether Dragulies defeated Stonejaw directly
whether the Crown passed by contest, gift, debt, trial, inheritance, or scandal
whether Stonejaw approves of Dragulies
whether the rig contains hidden history beyond public inheritance
```

Legacy tension:

```text
Every time Dragulies enters the Mech Geodeum in Stonejaw's Lift Rig, the crowd sees two Crown holders: the one operating it and the one whose name still owns the metal.
```

## Relationship To Dragulies

Stonejaw's Lift Rig extends Dragulies's public myth but does not replace him.

Dragulies is still the core figure:

```text
Tyranosapien Crown Stonebound
Verdict-Breaker
public witness
Geodeum champion
person whose refusal changed what victory meant
```

The rig adds:

```text
industrial inheritance
Crown-machine legacy
Mech Geodeum presence
public burden
Stonejaw's unresolved shadow
```

Best relationship line:

```text
Dragulies does not wear the rig like armor. He stands inside an old argument and makes it move.
```
