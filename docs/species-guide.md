# Meridian Prime Species Guide

> Fictional worldbuilding reference for the Meridian Prime universe.

This document defines the canonical visual, anatomical, cultural, and design language for the major peoples of Meridian Prime, Aeonos, and àæonos.

This guide begins with the **Aeonolacertians** and **áæbsconditians / Absconditians**, whose visual canon is foundational for novels, comics, illustrations, tabletop materials, and 16-bit game design.

---

## Aeonolacertians / Saurasapiens

**Canon Status:** CONFIRMED CANON

**Species Type:** hybrid saurian people native to Matter Aeonos.

**Formal Species / Civilization Name:** Aeonolacertian.

**Common Umbrella Field Term:** Saurasapien.

**Core Visual Principle:** Aeonolacertians are not simple dinosaur people. They are a crossbred saurian civilization with mostly humanoid body plans, expressive saurian features, universal tails, vivid color palettes, and morph-type traits that express across mixed populations.

Aeonolacertians should look ancient, alive, intelligent, physically powerful, culturally complex, and technologically mature. Their bodies and clothing should communicate heat, pressure, motion, brass, copper, color, class-line heritage, and survivor history.

---

## Aeonolacertian Baseline Body Plan

Aeonolacertians have a mostly humanoid body plan with strong saurian features.

They stand upright, walk bipedally, use tools, wear clothing and gear, and express emotion through face, posture, tail, crest, frill, quill, and eye movement.

### Shared Anatomical Traits

- Mostly humanoid torso proportions with saurian musculature and posture.
- Universal tails used for balance, communication, combat posture, and emotional expression.
- Four-fingered human-like hands with claws and morph-type shape variations.
- Four-toed human-like feet with claws and morph-type shape variations.
- Saurian heads, jaws, brows, crests, frills, spines, quills, and snouts depending on morph-type.
- Almost vestigial lips: enough soft tissue for expression and some speech shaping, but not fully mammalian lips.
- Expressive faces capable of warmth, suspicion, grief, humor, command, and fear without becoming human faces pasted onto reptiles.
- Scaled skin, usually with class-associated vivid color palettes.
- Feathers, quills, filaments, crests, frills, or spines present in most lineages, though expression varies by morph-type.

### Hands and Feet

Aeonolacertian hands and feet are functionally human-like enough for tools, writing, mechanisms, weapons, and fine engineering, but their shapes must remain saurian.

**Hands:** four fingers, including an opposable or semi-opposable thumb equivalent, with claws. The exact claw curvature, finger length, palm shape, and knuckle structure vary by morph-type.

**Feet:** four toes, broad or narrow depending on morph-type, with claws. They may be booted, armored, wrapped, or partly visible depending on dress.

Design note: avoid giving Aeonolacertians ordinary human hands or human feet with scales painted over them. Their anatomy should feel adapted to tools but not mammalian.

## Aeonolacertian Population Structure and Morph Types

Aeonolacertians are mixed populations with recognizable morph-type tendencies rather than rigid biological castes.

Morph types often correlate with specialized roles, inherited family traditions, military functions, ritual duties, and cultural expectations, but individuals may cross boundaries. After the Catastrophic Interaction, survival pressures have blurred some older distinctions.

A city, enclave, or faction should show mixed Aeonolacertian populations: different morph-types living, working, arguing, fighting, raising young, and maintaining machinery together.

### Naming Systems

**Aeonolacertian** is the formal species and civilization name.

**Saurasapien** is the common umbrella field term for Aeonolacertian morph-type people. It is most often used by Meridians, field personnel, artists, scout teams, mechanics, and game/comic adaptation notes when referring to the five recognizable `-sapien` morph-type expressions as a group.

The term **Saurasapien** does not replace Aeonolacertian in formal canon. It is a practical human-facing umbrella label for the visible morph-type system.

The five Aeonolacertian morph-type lines have two common human-facing naming systems.

**Archival / World Works / academic labels** usually use the older `-sauroid` morphology terms. These appear in classification records, restricted dossiers, scientific notes, and official World Works analysis.

**Common field names** usually use the shorter `-sapien` shorthand. These are more likely to appear in Meridian dialogue, field reports, mechanic slang, scout notes, game text, and everyday speech.

Native Aeonolacertian names for these morph-type lines remain open for future development.

| Archival Morph Label | Common Field Name | Typical Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Velociraptoroid | Velocisapien | speed, scouting, rapid movement |
| Tyrannisauroid | Tyrannosapien | command, heavy war, force projection |
| Bajadasauroid | Bajadrasapien | signal, ritual science, resonance |
| Ankylosauroid | Ankylosapien | defense, engineering, endurance |
| Ceratopsinoid | Cerasapien | civic order, guardianship, family law |

Example usage:

- **World Works archive:** “Subject exhibits Tyrannisauroid-dominant morphology.”
- **Meridian mechanic:** “That’s Tyrannosapien pressure gear. Don’t touch it unless you want your arm educated.”
- **Field team note:** “The commission included one Saurasapien of each major field-recognized morph type.”
- **Possible native usage:** TBD.

### Canonical Morph-Type Lines

- **Velociraptoroid / Velocisapien**
- **Tyrannisauroid / Tyrannosapien**
- **Bajadasauroid / Bajadrasapien**
- **Ankylosauroid / Ankylosapien**
- **Ceratopsinoid / Cerasapien**

These are not separate species. They are trait-line expressions within one crossbred saurian civilization.

## Aeonolacertian Color and Ornamentation

Aeonolacertians use vivid colors. Their class-lines are color-associated, though exact palettes vary by region, family, age, role, and ritual context.

Colors are not merely biological. They are social language.

Scale color, feather coloration, quill dye, armor paint, copper ornament, and ritual markings can all signal role, faction, ancestry, grief, readiness for war, or oath-status.

### Material Palette

Aeonolacertian culture strongly features:

- brass
- copper
- oxidized copper
- dark iron
- pressure-treated leather
- colored scale paint
- polished stone
- red crystal for elite Heart Guard armor
- steam-darkened cloth
- field gear and adventure wear

### Scale and Armor Painting

Aeonolacertians paint scales, armor, crests, frills, and quills for war, mourning, ritual, memory rites, class-line ceremonies, siege defense, and faction identity.

Paint may be mineral-based, heat-resistant, copper-infused, luminescent under boiler light, or designed to change tone with temperature.

War-paint is not only decoration. It can mark:

- heat-readiness
- unit identity
- oath of defense
- mourning for the Thousandfold Rupture
- Central Boiler sanctum duty
- Bellows Rite participation
- Heart Guard final-stand status

## Aeonolacertian Clothing, Gear, and Armor

Aeonolacertian fashion blends adventure utility, thermal survival, class-line expression, and advanced steampunk visual logic.

### Civilian Clothing

Civilians wear adventure clothes and practical gear.

Common elements may include:

- rugged field jackets
- belts, pouches, harnesses, straps, and tool rolls
- heat-lined wraps
- copper clasps
- brass utility buckles
- traveling cloaks
- pressure-safe boots or foot wraps
- goggles or lens gear
- scale-safe layered garments
- packs designed around tail movement

Civilian wear should look ready for work, travel, repair, exploration, and sudden evacuation.

### Steam-Oracle Clothing

Steam-Oracles wear robes over adventure clothes.

Their robes should not look passive or fragile. They are practical ritual-engineering garments layered over field gear, tool harnesses, and heat-safe clothing.

Common elements may include:

- heavy robes slit or tailored for tails
- copper-thread trim
- pressure glyphs
- brass clasps
- soot-stained hems
- tool belts beneath robes
- goggles, lenses, or visor masks
- portable gauges and tuning instruments

### Aeolipile Clothing

The Aeolipile wear steampunk adventure wear and gear.

Their clothing must support movement through pressure chambers, memory sites, and hostile field conditions.

Common elements may include:

- compact pressure packs
- rotating brass devices
- throat-resonance collars
- breath-control gear
- heat-lined coats
- reinforced gloves
- acoustic tools
- valve keys
- portable Bellows Rite instruments

They should look like explorer-engineers who can operate a sacred machine while hanging from a pipe bridge in a steam storm.

### Infantry Armor

Aeonolacertian infantry wear steam automaton suits.

These are not simple armor plates. They are steam-circulating, pressure-assisted combat suits that preserve body heat, amplify strength, support shield-lock formations, and integrate with city thermal infrastructure.

Common elements may include:

- brass and dark iron exosuit components
- steam-circulating tubes
- pressure gauges
- heat vents
- shield-lock ports
- reinforced tail protection
- heavy boots or clawed armored feet
- boiler-linked backpacks
- armored throat and chest protection

The armor should feel heavy, functional, loud, hot, and terrifying in a corridor.

### Heart Guard Armor

Heart Guard armor is red crystal armor.

This armor is elite, sacred, dangerous, and built for last-stand defense of the Mother Heart. It is designed to survive or channel extreme **20 K’al** conditions during hyper-metabolic combat states.

Design principles:

- red crystal plates or facets
- brass/copper underframe
- heat-channeling internal conduits
- glowing core seams
- ceremonial but highly functional silhouette
- class-line compatible tailoring
- intimidating clarity under boiler light

The Heart Guard should look like living red crystal statues heated from within.

## Saurasapien Morph-Type Visual Guide

### Velociraptoroid / Velocisapien

**Common Field Name:** Velocisapien

**Role Tendencies:** scouts, messengers, tactical intelligence, rapid-strike defense, infiltration, memory-runners.

**Body Shape:** lean, agile, narrow-waisted, quick-limbed, light but strong.

**Head and Face:** narrow saurian skull, alert eyes, sharp expressive brows, quick jaw movement, subtle cresting.

**Hands and Feet:** longer fingers and toes, curved claws, excellent grip, fast tool-handling.

**Tail:** flexible, balancing, expressive, often used in quick directional movement.

**Feathers / Quills:** common. Often appear along forearms, head crest, tail ridge, neck, or shoulders.

**Color Association:** vivid greens, teals, amber striping, sharp contrast patterns, speed-markings, scout dyes.

**Clothing / Gear:** light adventure gear, climbing harnesses, goggles, compact weapons, messenger straps, scout packs.

**Design Notes:** They should read as quick and intelligent rather than merely small. Avoid making them birdlike to the point of losing saurian identity.

---

### Tyrannisauroid / Tyrannosapien

**Common Field Name:** Tyrannosapien

**Role Tendencies:** command, heavy war leadership, territorial defense, executive authority, Heart Guard candidates, ceremonial force.

**Body Shape:** large, powerful, deep-chested, thick-necked, heavy-jawed, imposing.

**Head and Face:** broad skull, strong brow, deep jaw, expressive but intimidating facial structure.

**Hands and Feet:** four fingers and four toes, but thicker and more robust than other morphs; claws adapted for grip, combat, and command gestures.

**Tail:** thick, muscular, stabilizing, often armored in military contexts.

**Feathers / Quills:** present but usually heavier, crest-like, mane-like, or ritualized rather than delicate.

**Color Association:** reds, deep oranges, black striping, iron-dark markings, heat-associated war colors.

**Clothing / Gear:** command harnesses, heavy coats, ceremonial armor, steam automaton suits, red crystal Heart Guard armor for elite defenders.

**Design Notes:** They can be majestic or terrifying, but should not be clumsy brutes. Command intelligence matters.

---

### Bajadasauroid / Bajadrasapien

**Common Field Name:** Bajadrasapien

**Role Tendencies:** signal communication, ritual science, environmental interpretation, diplomacy, memory traditions, pressure resonance.

**Body Shape:** taller or more elongated than many morphs, with an elegant neck and prominent dorsal or neck spines.

**Head and Face:** elongated facial structure, expressive eyes, graceful crests or spines, often visually striking.

**Hands and Feet:** balanced between dexterity and stability; suited to instruments, signal tools, and environmental reading.

**Tail:** long and expressive, useful for balance and ceremonial posture.

**Feathers / Quills:** common along crest, spine, neck, tail, or shoulders. Spines may be ornamented with copper rings or ritual paint.

**Color Association:** blues, violets, copper-greens, oxidized copper tones, signal-bright accents.

**Clothing / Gear:** robes, signal harnesses, lens gear, Steam-Oracle tools, diplomatic ornaments, ritual science equipment.

**Design Notes:** Bajadasauroids should look strange, intelligent, and elegant, with a profile artists can recognize instantly.

---

### Ankylosauroid / Ankylosapien

**Common Field Name:** Ankylosapien

**Role Tendencies:** protection, engineering, fortress-building, infrastructure, endurance, defensive warfare.

**Body Shape:** broad, sturdy, low-centered, heavily built, armored-looking even without armor.

**Head and Face:** reinforced skull shapes, heavy brows, blunt but expressive facial structure.

**Hands and Feet:** powerful four-finger hands and broad four-toed feet, suited to tools, bracing, and heavy work.

**Tail:** thick and strong; may show club-like traits, but not every individual has an obvious full club-tail. Tail armor is common.

**Feathers / Quills:** present but often shorter, heavier, bristle-like, or arranged between osteoderms and armor plates.

**Color Association:** ochre, rust, dark green, bronze, slate, blackened copper, defensive markings.

**Clothing / Gear:** heavy work gear, engineering harnesses, reinforced aprons, pressure suits, steam automaton armor, shield-lock equipment.

**Design Notes:** They should feel like living fortifications, but still emotionally expressive and socially complex.

---

### Ceratopsinoid / Cerasapien

**Common Field Name:** Cerasapien

**Role Tendencies:** civic order, elder councils, community defense, family structures, agriculture, law, ceremonial guardianship.

**Body Shape:** strong, grounded, broad-shouldered, dignified, often with a steady posture.

**Head and Face:** horned or frilled facial structure. Frills and horns can be prominent, but should vary by individual, family, age, and role.

**Hands and Feet:** stable, precise, often suited to civic tools, agriculture, ceremonial weapons, and defensive formations.

**Tail:** strong and balancing, often used in formal posture or defensive bracing.

**Feathers / Quills:** may appear along frill edges, neck, shoulders, or tail. Frills are often painted or ornamented.

**Color Association:** golds, creams, deep greens, red-browns, white ritual markings, civic color bands.

**Clothing / Gear:** civic robes, family ornaments, ceremonial armor, agricultural gear, legal or council insignia, defensive breastplates.

**Design Notes:** Ceratopsinoids should feel noble, communal, and disciplined without becoming generic fantasy elders.

## Aeonolacertian Expression and Communication

Aeonolacertian faces are expressive.

They do not emote exactly like humans, but they are capable of clear emotional communication through:

- eye shape and focus
- brow plates
- jaw angle
- vestigial lip movement
- nostril flare
- crest lift
- frill angle
- quill bristling
- tail position
- claw tapping
- throat rumble
- posture and heat-state

Artists and writers should avoid making Aeonolacertians unreadable monsters. Their alienness should come from a different expressive grammar, not from emotional blankness.

## Aeonolacertian Design Rules for Artists and Adaptations

1. Aeonolacertians are mostly humanoid with saurian features, not humans in masks and not ordinary dinosaurs walking upright.
2. Tails are universal.
3. Hands have four fingers with claws; feet have four toes with claws.
4. Faces are expressive, with almost vestigial lips.
5. Feathers, quills, crests, frills, and spines are common in most lineages.
6. Vivid colors are canon.
7. Class-lines are color-associated but not color-locked.
8. Brass, copper, oxidized copper, and pressure-machine ornamentation belong strongly to their culture.
9. Civilians look adventurous and practical, not medieval peasants.
10. Steam-Oracles wear robes over functional adventure gear.
11. Aeolipile dress like steampunk explorer-engineers.
12. Infantry wear steam automaton suits.
13. Heart Guard wear red crystal armor.
14. Scale and armor painting for war, ritual, grief, and oath-status is canon.
15. Morph types are mixed population traits associated with specialized roles, not separate species.
16. `-sauroid` terms are archival or academic labels; `-sapien` terms are common field names.
17. **Saurasapien** is the common umbrella field term for Aeonolacertian morph-type people.
18. **Aeonolacertian** remains the formal species and civilization name.

## Aeonolacertian Common Design Mistakes to Avoid

- Do not depict Aeonolacertians as generic lizardfolk.
- Do not make every individual look like the same dinosaur with different clothes.
- Do not erase the four-finger / four-toe rule.
- Do not make their hands and feet fully human.
- Do not remove tails.
- Do not make them dull brown by default; vivid coloration is canon.
- Do not make technology look crude just because it appears steampunk.
- Do not make Steam-Oracles look like fantasy wizards; they are ritual engineers.
- Do not make Heart Guard armor ordinary red metal; it is red crystal armor.
- Do not treat morph types as rigid castes with no mixing.
- Do not use the common `-sapien` field names as proof that Aeonolacertians are human; the terms are human-facing shorthand.
- Do not use **Saurasapien** as a formal replacement for **Aeonolacertian** in official canon or native history.

### Comics

Use morph-type silhouette first: readers should recognize major line tendencies even in shadow.

Morph-types can become readable sprite classes:

- Velociraptoroid / Velocisapien: fast scout / dash unit
- Tyrannisauroid / Tyrannosapien: heavy command / power unit
- Bajadasauroid / Bajadrasapien: signal / support / ritual-tech unit

The term **Saurasapien** can be used as the umbrella species label in menus, bestiaries, field guides, sprite sheets, and collectible lore cards when the adaptation needs a compact field-facing category.

Morph-types can inform role bonuses without locking characters into roles. A Velocisapien can be a scholar. An Ankylosapien can be a diplomat. The trait-line influences silhouette and cultural expectations, not destiny.

A tabletop adaptation may use **Saurasapien** as the playable ancestry/species umbrella and the five `-sapien` names as morph-type options.

## áæbsconditians / Absconditians / Antisapiens

**Canon Status:** CONFIRMED CANON

**Species Type:** blue-skinned anti-matter humanoid people native to àæonos.

**Formal Civilization / Species Name:** áæbsconditian.

**Simplified Human / Common Name:** Absconditian.

**Common Umbrella Field Term:** Antisapien.

**Plural Field Term:** Antisapiens.

**Core Visual Principle:** Antisapiens are áæbsconditian phenotype people whose visible differences are primarily expressed through signal behavior, glow patterning, stealth adaptation, resonance markers, identity markings, bio-cabling, technology access, signal aesthetics, and anti-matter biology rather than body-shape ecology.

Antisapiens should look elegant, unsettling, alive, intelligent, blue-skinned, cyber-resonant, and profoundly alien without becoming generic fantasy creatures.

## Antisapien Baseline Body Plan

Antisapiens are blue-skinned humanoids with anti-matter biological and cyber-resonant traits.

Their bodies are generally humanoid in silhouette, but their visual identity comes from their eyes, glow behavior, signal markings, posture, stealth adaptation, lattice ornamentation, bio-cabling, technology access, signal aesthetic logic, and how their presence interacts with light and perception.

### Shared Anatomical and Visual Traits

- Blue-skinned humanoid body plan.
- Black irises.
- Magenta pupils.
- Teal tapetum lucidum, often visible as low-light eye-shine.
- Smooth, living skin.
- Rounded or humanlike ears rather than pointed fantasy ears.
- Signal-band markings, glow asymmetries, lattice patterns, bio-cabling, signal aesthetics, or resonance traces depending on phenotype.
- Cyberpunk / anti-matter aesthetic language: black glass, magenta or amaranth light, teal optics, neural lattice ornamentation, stealth surfaces, and signal interfaces.

Antisapiens are living anti-matter people. Their anti-life nature is cosmological, biological, technological, and philosophical, not a horror-creature shortcut.

## Antisapien Bio-Cabling / Hair

Antisapien “hair” is not ordinary mammalian hair. It is **bio-cabling**: a living signal-organ expression tied to anti-matter biology, cyber-resonance, signal-band phenotype, identity, and social function.

**Antisapien bio-cabling varies by signal-band. It may be absent, hidden, luminous, bundled, or exposed depending on phenotype.**

### Nullsapien Bio-Cabling

**Expression:** absent or fully internalized.

**Visual:** bald, smooth scalp, severe symmetry.

**Meaning:** wireless signal control, sealed sovereignty, disciplined command.

**Rule:** Nullsapiens should generally be bald because they represent wireless technology and contained signal authority.

### Veilsapien Bio-Cabling

**Expression:** retractable dark fiber optics.

**Visual:** matte-black or midnight-blue strands with minimal reflectivity, able to flatten, retract, dim, or vanish into the silhouette.

**Meaning:** stealth, suppressed signal, covert observation, phase discipline.

**Rule:** from a distance, Veilsapien cabling may make them appear almost bald until light catches the strands.

### Amaranthsapien Bio-Cabling

**Expression:** luminous fiber optics with glowing tips.

**Visual:** magenta, amaranth, violet, teal, or electric-blue fiber-optic strands, often braided, looped, or arranged in harmonic patterns.

**Meaning:** resonance science, signal theory, dream-contact, harmonic computation, Madrigalist practice.

**Rule:** glowing tips may brighten during song, signal reading, dream-contact, or interface work.

### Vaultsapien Bio-Cabling

**Expression:** organized network-cable bundles.

**Visual:** thicker, orderly, jacketed cable-like strands in cobalt, teal, graphite, silver-gray, or muted magenta accents, often bound with identity clasps, memory tags, or record ornaments.

**Meaning:** identity preservation, genealogies, survivor ledgers, continuity custody, archival discipline.

**Rule:** avoid making this look like literal modern office ethernet cable; it should feel like an elegant in-world ceremonial network-cable analog.

### Lividisapien Bio-Cabling

**Expression:** exposed multicolored phone-wire hair.

**Visual:** visible internal cable-like strands in magenta, teal, yellow, orange, violet, pale blue, white, and other signal colors.

**Meaning:** contradiction reading, suppressed history, Split Signal analysis, revisionist truth-seeking.

**Rule:** Lividisapien cabling may look tangled, frayed, or asymmetrical, but it should still feel meaningful and beautiful rather than random.

### Bio-Cabling Design Balance

- **Nullsapien** = wireless / internal signal.
- **Veilsapien** = retractable dark fiber optics / hidden signal.
- **Amaranthsapien** = glowing fiber optics / harmonic signal.
- **Vaultsapien** = network-cable bundles / archived signal.
- **Lividisapien** = exposed phone wires / contradictory signal.

Bio-cabling can move, glow, dim, retract, pulse, or respond to interface states depending on band. It should feel biological and technological at once.

## Antisapien Signal-Band Technology Access

Each Antisapien signal-band is bound by a technology-access ceiling. This access is biological, cultural, political, and infrastructural. It reflects what systems their signal-band can safely interface with, what systems society permits them to operate, and what kinds of technology their bio-cabling and signal behavior are naturally adapted to.

**Each Antisapien signal-band can access its own technology tier and all lesser tiers. Higher tiers require special permission, restricted bypass, rare talent, ritual modification, or dangerous interface corruption.**

### Tier 5: Nullsapien

**Highest authorized access.**

Nullsapiens have access to:

- AI systems
- sovereign cognition systems
- predictive governance engines
- command intelligence
- high-order automated decision networks
- all lesser forms of Antisapien technology

This matches their bald/internalized bio-cabling and wireless signal authority. Nullsapiens do not visibly wear the network. They are permitted to command it.

### Tier 4: Veilsapien

Veilsapiens have access to:

- AR systems
- perception-layer overlays
- stealth-field interfaces
- phase-navigation displays
- visual misdirection networks
- VR systems
- archive mesh systems
- smart tech and legacy systems

This matches their retractable dark fiber-optic bio-cabling and stealth role. Veilsapiens specialize in editing perception, masking presence, and controlling what others think they see.

### Tier 3: Amaranthsapien

Amaranthsapiens have access to:

- VR systems
- resonance chambers
- dream-contact systems
- harmonic simulations
- immersive memory theaters
- virtual signal laboratories
- archive mesh systems
- smart tech and legacy systems

This matches their luminous fiber-optic bio-cabling with glowing tips. Amaranthsapiens specialize in simulated experience, resonance, dream logic, harmonic computation, and Madrigalist practice.

### Tier 2: Vaultsapien

Vaultsapiens have access to:

- intranet systems
- the Vault Mesh
- archive mesh systems
- identity vaults
- continuity ledgers
- survivor records
- population memory systems
- smart tech and legacy systems

**Vault Mesh** is the preferred term for the secured áæbsconditian intranet used for identity records, survivor ledgers, population continuity, official history, memory proofs, and protected internal archives.

This matches their organized network-cable bio-cabling. Vaultsapiens preserve continuity through ordered, secured, internal networks.

### Tier 1: Lividisapien

Lividisapiens have access to:

- smart tech
- old devices
- phreak-culture systems
- counter-system tools
- signal salvage
- legacy interfaces
- discarded hardware
- corrupted devices
- unofficial workarounds
- exposed wiring and forgotten infrastructure

**Wire access** and **fringe access** are common terms for Lividisapien interaction with discarded, outdated, unofficial, corrupted, or physically exposed systems.

This matches their exposed multicolored phone-wire bio-cabling. Lividisapiens are low-clearance but dangerous because they understand broken systems, abandoned channels, and the ugly little wires official power forgets to bury.

### Technology Access Design Balance
