# Chapter 02: The Honest Job

> Planning file for the first full mission-movement chapter after Chapter 1’s departure.

**Canon Status:** PLANNING CANON, EXPANDABLE

## Purpose

Turn Sequence 2 into the first full mission-movement chapter after departure.

Core question:

**Who is on MITE II, what does the job officially require, and what is the first practical sign that the mission shape is wrong?**

Chapter 2 should introduce the mission in motion after Chapter 1’s departure. It should make MITE II feel like real work before the deeper weirdness arrives.

---

## Chapter Function

Chapter 2 should establish:

- Red Umbrielor leaving the NCI yard / entering travel rhythm
- the MITE II Mod train settling into movement
- Tenet’s command style
- the official “honest” survey mission
- Horus’s approval lingering as unease
- Lindy remaining behind on dispatch unless future canon changes this
- the first three clearly introduced MITE II crew members
- one additional practical wrong detail in equipment, cargo, comms, or route shape

---

## Locked Chapter 2 Scene Choices

These choices are now locked for Chapter 2 unless later canon deliberately revises them.

### Crew Introduction Order

The first three MITE II crew anchors should appear in this order:

1. **Rudy Calder**
2. **Kavo “Forest” Rill**
3. **Ildi Vey**

This order follows the natural escalation from Chapter 1:

- Chapter 1 ends with Red Umbrielor moving.
- Chapter 2 begins with machine and Mod-train rhythm.
- The story then widens from machinery to terrain.
- The first deeper wrongness enters through signal.

### Rudy First: Machine Truth

Rudy Calder appears first because Chapter 1 ends with Red Umbrielor rolling.

He should enter through Red Umbrielor, the Mod train, the turkey roaster, or a practical machinery complaint.

Preferred entrance mode:

Rudy comes over comms or appears from the mechanic Mod, complaining that Red Umbrielor’s defrost is not broken, it is “protesting management.”

Chapter 2 function:

Rudy keeps the story grounded in machinery before the route and signal weirdness widen the field.

### Forest Second: Terrain Truth

Kavo “Forest” Rill appears second once the route begins to matter.

He should enter through route awareness, GPR prep, terrain instinct, or a calm observation that the official mission shape does not match how early they need a Mountaineer.

Preferred entrance mode:

Forest is already watching the route through GPR prep or exterior feeds, relaxed in posture but too focused for the job to feel ordinary.

Chapter 2 function:

Forest lets the reader feel that the route itself is not behaving like a harmless proposed extension corridor.

### Ildi Third: Signal Truth

Ildi Vey appears third through the first locked additional wrong detail.

Preferred entrance mode:

Ildi reports from the module-attached position that the Mod train is picking up a **ghost handshake** from infrastructure that should not exist along the proposed extension corridor.

Chapter 2 function:

Ildi introduces the first field-level wrongness after Horus’s approval stamp.

### First Additional Wrong Detail

The first additional wrong detail after Horus’s approval is:

**Ildi detects an old signal / ghost handshake where the route packet says there is no active or legacy infrastructure.**

This is now the locked Chapter 2 field clue.

Chapter 1 wrongness is authority:

**Horus approved the job packet.**

Chapter 2 wrongness is field evidence:

**The route is talking back.**

This wrongness should not reveal the hidden formation yet. It should only show that the packet is lying in a new way.

Reader-facing meaning:

The paper lied first. Then the old systems answered.

### Tenet’s Role in the Locked Wrongness

Tenet should not be passive while Rudy, Forest, and Ildi notice problems.

His Chapter 2 function is to:

- hear Rudy’s machine read
- weigh Forest’s terrain read
- evaluate Ildi’s signal read
- compare all three against the packet
- decide whether to keep rolling, slow down, call Lindy, or mark the discrepancy for later

Tenet should not jump straight to conspiracy.

He should read the situation as a field operator:

- the equipment is too ready
- the route is too vague
- the signal should not be there
- the job may still be survivable, but it is not shaped honestly

## Rudy Calder

Canon name:

**Rudy Calder**

Role:

**Lead mechanic / field repair / Red Umbrielor whisperer**

People / culture:

Meridian human, Dieselwright-adjacent by culture.

Age vibe:

50s.

Core vibe:

Cheerful fatalist, grease saint, laughs at things that should worry people.

Rudy knows Red Umbrielor’s bad habits almost as well as Tenet does. He can hear a pump lie from three tractors away.

### Story Function

Rudy represents **machine truth**.

If Rudy is worried, the equipment load is wrong.

He should notice practical machinery contradictions before anyone names conspiracy.

Possible first impression line:

**“That defrost isn’t broken. It’s protesting management.”**

### Character Notes

- warm
- blunt
- superstitious about machines, not people
- names problems before people are ready to hear them
- useful for explaining equipment through action, not exposition
- can comment on the turkey roaster, Mod couplings, redundant reefer support, and whether the MITE II load makes sense

### Guardrail

Do not make Rudy a generic comic mechanic. His humor should come from competence and fatalism.

## Kavo “Forest” Rill

Canon name:

**Kavo Rill**

Human / crew name:

**Forest Rill**

Role:

**Mountaineer / route guide / wilderness EMT / GPR path-reader**

People / species:

**Velocisapien / Velociraptoroid**

Culture vibe:

Pressure-runner, boiler-ska route culture.

Age vibe:

Late 30s to early 40s.

Core vibe:

Relaxed menace, precise, streetwise, impossible to rush.

### Naming Canon

Kavo Rill is his real name.

**Forest** is his human / crew name, given during his first Traverse season because he could **see the forest through the trees**.

The joke stuck partly because actual forests are rare on Meridian Prime, and partly because Traverse crews never let a useful joke die with dignity.

There is also a rough crew joke attached to the name:

**Forests are rare on Meridian Prime, and there’s a hot chick behind every tree.**

Treat this as crude Traverse Ops field humor, not formal narration unless the scene specifically wants that rough crew-room edge.

### Character Meaning

**Forest** is what humans and mixed Traverse crews call him because they like him, trust him, and claimed him through field humor.

**Kavo** is who he is when the joke drops away.

Kavo / Forest knows Mark Skelton and is a friend of the Skeltons, but he is not automatically loyal to Horus.

His relationship with Mark and the Skeltons should remain complicated and expandable.

### Visual Canon

- short sparse red proto-quill mohawk
- black-frame shades when not wearing route goggles
- subtle checker trim
- light route harness
- pressure-route badges
- claw-wraps
- relaxed posture that can become dangerous instantly

### Story Function

Kavo / Forest represents **terrain truth**.

If Forest is worried, the route is wrong.

He should be the first crew member who can say, from terrain instinct, that the job is shaped wrong.

Possible first impression line:

**“If the route was honest, they wouldn’t need me this early.”**

### Character Notes

- quiet until funny
- funny until dangerous
- dangerous until useful
- can lean against a Mod like he is bored, then cross bad terrain before anyone finishes objecting
- brings Saurasapien / Aeonolacertian pressure-rhythm aesthetics into the crew through movement, posture, route culture, and gear rather than exposition

### Guardrail

Do not make him a generic raptor scout, ninja, assassin, or animalistic alien. His speed and perception should feel social, urban, rhythmic, pressure-route trained, and professionally earned.

Canon name:

**Ildi Vey**

Role:

**Module-attached mech / infrastructure monitor / old-route systems reader**

People / species:

Antisapien, likely Lividisapien or related underclass signal-band. Keep exact subtype expandable unless already canonized elsewhere.

Culture vibe:

Punk / exposed-wire signal truth.

Age vibe:

Late 20s to mid 30s.

Core vibe:

Wiry, suspicious, brilliant, hears systems as arguments.

Ildi attaches to or rides with the module during Traverse to monitor:

- coupling strain
- comms
- heat
- dead channels
- corrupted terminals
- route infrastructure
- old signal behavior
- module health
- buried or half-awake systems

### Story Function

Ildi represents **signal truth**.

If Ildi is worried, the infrastructure is wrong.

She quietly foreshadows the buried infrastructure mystery.

Possible first impression line:

**“Your clean packet is bleeding old signal.”**

### Character Notes

- sarcastic
- vigilant
- hard to impress
- trusts cables more than people
- trusts old signals more than official reports
- notices comms that are too quiet, dead channels that are not dead enough, and ghost handshakes from infrastructure that should not exist

### Guardrail

Do not make Ildi mystical. Her weirdness should be technical, signal-based, and grounded in Antisapien / underclass exposed-wire logic.

## Chapter 2 Design Notes

Every crew introduction should reveal work first, personality second, backstory later.

The crew should not enter like a list. They should enter through pressure:

- Rudy through machine behavior or equipment disagreement
- Forest through route shape, timing, GPR prep, or terrain instinct
- Ildi through module systems, comm silence, or old-route signal behavior

Tenet should remain active. The crew can notice things, but Tenet must interpret, decide, prioritize, or command.

Chapter 2 should make the MITE II mission feel plausible enough that the first additional wrongness feels unsettling rather than obvious.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Do not introduce too many crew members at once.
- Do not turn Rudy into generic comic relief.
- Do not turn Forest into a generic raptor scout or ninja.
- Do not make Ildi mystical instead of technical.
- Do not reveal the hidden formation too early.
- Do not make Tenet passive while the crew notices everything.
- Do not over-explain species, class, or genre aesthetics in Chapter 2.
- Do not forget that every crew introduction should reveal work first, personality second, backstory later.
- Do not let the ghost handshake reveal the hidden formation, the Core, or gateway mechanics yet.
