# Book One Novella Beat Grid

> Draftable chapter-by-chapter beat grid for the 9-chapter Book One novella structure.

**Canon Status:** PLANNING CANON, EXPANDABLE

## Purpose

Turn the 9-chapter novella structure into a draftable chapter grid.

Core question:

**What does each chapter open on, turn on, reveal, and end on?**

Primary source:

- `docs/story/book-one-novella-structure.md`

---

## Novella Shape

Use this 9-chapter structure:

1. Cold Start
2. The Honest Job
3. Proposed Extension Corridor
4. The Wrong Quiet
5. Growth Event
6. Footprints
7. Rob’s Map
8. Door Under the World
9. The Route to It

Target total length:

**35,000 to 45,000 words**

Recommended chapter length:

Approximately **3,500 to 5,000 words**, flexible by chapter pressure.

Core novella movement:

**Tenet follows a job forward and learns to read Rob’s death backward.**

Ending target:

**Tenet did not find the truth. He found the route to it.**

---

## Equipment Naming Note

**MADS** is Red Umbrielor’s onboard diagnostic / alert system.

**MD-8** is **Meridian Distillate 8** fuel.

Do not use MD-8 as the computer, diagnostic voice, or alert system.

- `docs/equipment/red-umbrielor-systems.md`

## Chapter 1: Cold Start

### Chapter Title

**Cold Start**

### Opening Image

Tenet’s hand on cold scarred steel beside Red Umbrielor before dawn.

### Primary Location

NCI staging yard and Red Umbrielor’s cab.

### POV / Focus

Tenet Nocsid III through work, machine familiarity, grief restraint, and family friction.

### Main Action

Tenet prepares Red Umbrielor for MITE II departure.

Required beats:

- Tenet stands beside Red Umbrielor before dawn.
- MADS complains.
- MD-8 fuel handling / procedure may appear as part of the NCI yard and Red Umbrielor’s field reality.
- Red Umbrielor’s defrost is dead.
- Tenet uses the turkey roaster.
- The under-seat compartment holds Grandfather Ten’s pistol.
- Rob’s empty passenger seat establishes absence.
- Lindy Nocsid hands over the MITE II job packet.
- Tenet sees Horus Skelton’s approval stamp.
- Tenet says, **“What the—”**
- Tenet accepts the job anyway.

### Key Crew Member Pressure

Lindy Nocsid is not MITE II field crew, but she acts as the first pressure point:

- family pressure
- NCI dispatch pressure
- early warning system
- the person who hands Tenet clean paperwork that smells wrong

### Reveal

The job is legal, but Horus Skelton’s approval makes it wrong by scale.

### Tenet Choice

Accept the job anyway because work is still work, the crew needs leadership, and imperfect paperwork does not stop every Traverse.

### Exit Image

Red Umbrielor rolling out with the wrongness aboard.

### Do Not Reveal Yet

- Rob’s poisoning
- hidden formation
- footprints
- Core
- Aeonos / Àæonos
- full Horus motive
- assassin inside NCI

### Approximate Word Target

**3,500 to 4,500 words**

## Chapter 2: The Honest Job

### Chapter Title

**The Honest Job**

### Opening Image

The Mod train settling into motion behind Red Umbrielor.

### Primary Location

Red Umbrielor, the MITE II Mod train, and early route departure corridor.

### POV / Focus

Tenet in command, expanding from machine focus to crew and mission rhythm.

### Main Action

MITE II enters travel rhythm and the first three crew anchors are introduced through work.

Required beats:

- MITE II enters travel rhythm.
- Tenet settles into command.
- Rudy Calder is introduced first through machine truth.
- Kavo “Forest” Rill is introduced second through terrain truth.
- Ildi Vey is introduced third through signal truth.
- The official mission is restated as private survey / route-proofing work along a proposed extension corridor.
- Ildi detects an old signal / ghost handshake where the packet says no active or legacy infrastructure exists.

### Key Crew Member Pressure

- Rudy Calder = machine truth
- Kavo “Forest” Rill = terrain truth
- Ildi Vey = signal truth

### Reveal

**The paper lied first. Then the old systems answered.**

### Tenet Choice

Treat the ghost handshake as a field discrepancy, not a conspiracy proof, while keeping the crew moving and watching the route more closely.

### Exit Image

Ildi’s ghost handshake repeating against a route that should be silent.

### Do Not Reveal Yet

- source of ghost handshake
- hidden formation
- Core / gateway mechanics
- footprints
- Rob’s murder truth

### Approximate Word Target

**3,500 to 4,500 words**

## Chapter 3: Proposed Extension Corridor

### Chapter Title

**Proposed Extension Corridor**

### Opening Image

Red Umbrielor and the Mod train moving through a corridor that exists more clearly in the packet than in the land.

### Primary Location

On Traverse, along the proposed extension corridor.

### POV / Focus

Tenet comparing mission language to field reality while the crew’s three pressure gauges keep reporting mismatches.

### Main Action

The mission stays mostly in motion while the proposed extension corridor label proves too vague to be operationally honest.

Required beats:

- The mission continues mostly in motion.
- The proposed extension corridor label proves too vague to be operationally honest.
- Rudy notices equipment or repair capacity beyond ordinary survey need.
- Forest notices route timing requires Mountaineer attention earlier than the packet implies.
- Ildi tracks the ghost handshake but cannot identify it.
- Tenet compares cargo, route timing, comms, and packet language.

Important choice:

Chapter 3 should stay mostly in motion rather than stop for a major check. The route should feel like it is quietly pulling MITE II forward before the crew realizes how much control they have surrendered.

### Key Crew Member Pressure

All three crew anchors apply pressure:

- Rudy questions equipment readiness.
- Forest questions route timing.
- Ildi questions signal silence.

### Reveal

**The route label says one thing. The loadout says another.**

### Tenet Choice

Continue in motion, but begin treating the job as a hazard assessment rather than ordinary survey work.

### Exit Image

The proposed extension corridor stretching ahead while Tenet’s packet, cargo manifest, and route display refuse to agree with each other.

### Do Not Reveal Yet

- hidden formation in full
- footprints
- Rob’s murder truth
- full Mark / Horus chain

### Approximate Word Target

**3,500 to 4,500 words**

## Chapter 4: The Wrong Quiet

### Chapter Title

**The Wrong Quiet**

### Opening Image

A comms panel showing too little traffic for a mission supposedly this harmless.

### Primary Location

MITE II Mod train in motion, interior comms / systems spaces, and route exterior feeds.

### POV / Focus

Tenet watching the job narrow around the crew while Ildi, Forest, and Rudy identify different kinds of silence.

### Main Action

The comms plan, route shape, and Mod-train behavior reveal that MITE II is structured around concealment.

Required beats:

- The comms plan proves unusually quiet or restricted for a harmless survey.
- Lindy remains behind on dispatch, but may appear through comms or a delayed message.
- Ildi detects a dead channel that is not dead enough.
- Forest notices the route avoids normal visibility.
- Rudy reads Mod-train strain / prep as overbuilt for the stated job.
- Tenet realizes the mission is structured around concealment.

### Key Crew Member Pressure

Ildi is the sharpest pressure in this chapter because she can tell the difference between dead silence and silenced infrastructure.

Forest and Rudy support the pressure by confirming route and machine mismatches.

### Reveal

**The job is quiet in the way traps are quiet.**

### Tenet Choice

Keep the crew moving while tightening internal documentation and evidence control.

### Exit Image

Tenet logs an internal discrepancy the official packet does not ask for, while the dead channel keeps breathing under Ildi’s instruments.

### Do Not Reveal Yet

- hidden formation fully
- footprints
- Chrono-Floquet Distillate
- assassin
- Core mechanics

### Approximate Word Target

**3,500 to 4,500 words**

## Chapter 6: Footprints

### Chapter Title

**Footprints**

### Opening Image

The hidden formation appearing where terrain and policy kept it out of sight.

### Primary Location

Near or within visual range of the hidden formation near the Claw.

### POV / Focus

Tenet assessing evidence under field pressure while the crew debates what the evidence means.

### Main Action

MITE II reaches or sees the hidden formation, and footprints make the anomaly impossible to dismiss as geology.

Required beats:

- MITE II detects, reaches, or gets visual access to the hidden formation near the Claw.
- The formation is out of view of The Path and hidden by rollers / pressure ridges / no-fly-zone logic.
- Growth pattern and timing are wrong.
- A crew member finds footprints near or within the formation.
- Crew debates whether footprints are contamination, distortion, human, Meridian, Saurasapien, Antisapien, old, fresh, or impossible.
- Tenet recognizes footprints change the risk category.

### Key Crew Member Pressure

Forest and Ildi are likely most important here:

- Forest reads the terrain around the prints.
- Ildi reads whether the prints align with the ghost handshake / buried system behavior.

Rudy pressures the scene through practical survival: how long the Mod train can safely remain near the formation.

### Reveal

**The formation is not just geology. Something used it.**

### Tenet Choice

Preserve evidence rather than allowing it to be filed as harmless survey noise.

### Exit Image

Tenet looking from the footprints to the hidden formation, knowing the official map has just failed.

### Do Not Reveal Yet

- who made the footprints
- full route destination
- full Horus plan
- complete Rob death truth

### Approximate Word Target

**4,000 to 5,000 words**

## Chapter 7: Rob’s Map

### Chapter Title

**Rob’s Map**

### Opening Image

Grandfather Ten’s pistol, the formation evidence, or a route detail triggering Tenet’s memory of Rob’s final trip.

### Primary Location

MITE II field position near the formation, Red Umbrielor’s cab, or a temporary evidence / planning space inside the Mod train.

### POV / Focus

Tenet shifting Rob’s death from private grief into actionable evidence.

### Main Action

The MITE II mystery begins to echo The Last Traverse.

Required beats:

- A detail near the formation echoes something Rob said, did, or withheld during The Last Traverse.
- Grandfather Ten’s pistol becomes newly significant through symbol, mechanism, inscription, memory trigger, or route association.
- Tenet remembers Rob’s reason for choosing him: because Tenet still had time.
- The House Ten Built begins to feel connected to MITE II.
- Crystalpnomia may be named as Rob’s accepted diagnosis.
- Tenet begins to suspect Rob’s final trip was preparation, not nostalgia.

### Key Crew Member Pressure

This is Tenet’s internal pressure chapter.

Crew pressure may come from:

- Ildi connecting old signal to old route records
- Forest recognizing a non-Meridian route logic
- Rudy remembering something Rob once said about old NCI work

### Reveal

**Rob’s death was not an answer. It was a map folded wrong.**

### Tenet Choice

Stop treating grief as private baggage and begin treating it as evidence.

### Exit Image

Tenet holding or checking the pistol differently than he did on page 1, not because he understands it fully, but because it has started pointing.

### Do Not Reveal Yet

- complete poisoning chain
- assassin identity
- full Chrono-Floquet Distillate mechanics
- full secrets of The House Ten Built

### Approximate Word Target

**4,000 to 5,000 words**

## Chapter 8: Door Under the World

### Chapter Title

**Door Under the World**

### Opening Image

The formation, ghost handshake, and footprint evidence aligning on Tenet’s displays or in the physical landscape.

### Primary Location

At or near the hidden formation and its connected terrain / buried infrastructure.

### POV / Focus

Tenet under climax pressure, balancing crew survival, evidence, truth, and the lie he was hired to serve.

### Main Action

MITE II proves the formation is connected to a larger buried route or gateway-adjacent system without solving the whole cosmology.

Required beats:

- MITE II proves the formation is connected to an old route, buried infrastructure, gateway-adjacent system, or Core-adjacent mystery.
- Ghost handshake, footprints, and formation align enough to prove the packet lied for a reason.
- Aeonos / Àæonos may become relevant through naming, signal response, ruin marks, footprints, or route language, but only enough to force the distinction.
- Horus’s approval and Mark Skelton’s delayed reporting become part of the same chain.
- Physical danger at or near the formation drives the climax.
- Tenet must decide what honest work means when the job itself is a lie.

### Key Crew Member Pressure

All three anchors pressure the climax:

- Rudy: Can the machine / Mod train survive?
- Forest: Is there a traversable line through the danger?
- Ildi: Is the signal opening, warning, calling, or recording?

### Reveal

**The land is not hiding a secret. It has opened a door.**

### Tenet Choice

Refuse the clean lie and choose the dangerous route toward truth while protecting the crew.

### Exit Image

MITE II surviving the immediate danger while the hidden route / door remains real behind them or ahead of them.

### Do Not Reveal Yet

- full Core function
- full Aeonos / Àæonos stakes
- Horus’s entire plan
- assassin identity unless later canon deliberately changes this

### Approximate Word Target

**4,500 to 5,500 words**

## Chapter 9: The Route to It

### Chapter Title

**The Route to It**

### Opening Image

Aftermath inside Red Umbrielor or the Mod train: damage, evidence, silence, and decisions waiting to be made.

### Primary Location

MITE II aftermath position, return route staging point, or Red Umbrielor’s cab after the formation crisis.

### POV / Focus

Tenet choosing what truth can survive the report, what must be carried privately, and what Rob’s route now demands.

### Main Action

Resolve the novella’s immediate mission truth while opening the larger saga.

Required beats:

- Tenet decides what to report, what to conceal, and what to carry forward.
- Crew survives the immediate route crisis.
- MITE II cannot be considered honest work anymore.
- Lindy may receive a partial truth, coded report, or silence she understands.
- Horus loses an easy move but not the game.
- Mark’s role remains complicated.
- Assassin inside NCI remains unidentified or only faintly suggested.
- Tenet carries forward the pistol, data, coordinates, sample, map, or memory differently than page 1.

### Key Crew Member Pressure

The crew becomes the ethical pressure:

- Rudy wants survivable truth.
- Forest wants route truth.
- Ildi wants signal truth preserved before it is scrubbed.
- Lindy may represent report truth from afar.

### Reveal

**Tenet has not found the truth. He has found the route to it.**

### Tenet Choice

Choose what to report officially, what to conceal from Horus / Prime Ops / NCI channels, and what to carry forward personally.

### Exit Image

Proposed final image:

Tenet in Red Umbrielor’s cab at low light, Grandfather Ten’s pistol secure under the seat, the route data hidden somewhere Horus cannot easily reach, while the dashboard shows a line that was not on the original packet.

The route does not lead home.

It leads onward.

### Do Not Resolve

- full Core mystery
- assassin inside NCI
- complete truth of Rob’s poisoning
- full Aeonos / Àæonos stakes
- secrets of The House Ten Built
- Horus’s entire plan

### Approximate Word Target

**3,500 to 5,000 words**

## Novella Drafting Guardrails

- Do not explain the whole Meridian Prime setting.
- Do not solve the Core.
- Do not fully reveal Rob’s poisoning chain.
- Do not identify the assassin unless later canon deliberately chooses that.
- Do not make Aeonos / Àæonos exposition arrive before evidence forces the distinction.
- Do not overpopulate the MITE II crew in early chapters.
- Do not let Horus become cartoonish.
- Do not let Mark become automatically guilty of everything Horus does.
- Do not reveal every secret of The House Ten Built.
- Field procedure must create plot pressure, not decoration.
- Every chapter should reveal through action, not lore lecture.
