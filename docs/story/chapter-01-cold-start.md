# Chapter 01: Cold Start

> Beat sheet and draftable scene plan for the opening chapter of Book One.

**Canon Status:** PLANNING CANON, EXPANDABLE

## Chapter Purpose

Turn Sequence 1: Cold Start into a concrete first chapter plan.

Chapter 1 should introduce Tenet Nocsid III through work, machine, grief, family tension, and one wrong detail.

Core chapter question:

**What exactly happens in Chapter 1, beat by beat?**

---

## Chapter Promise

Chapter 1 should promise the reader:

- this is a working world
- Tenet knows what he is doing
- Red Umbrielor matters
- Rob Holliday is gone but still present in absence
- NCI is family-rooted contractor culture
- Lindy Nocsid is Tenet’s younger sister and NCI dispatcher / staging-yard coordinator
- MITE II looks like honest work
- Horus Skelton’s approval makes the job feel wrong
- Tenet will take the job anyway

Opening rule:

**Begin with work. Let grief leak through the work. Let wrongness appear as paperwork. Let family hand him the packet.**

---

## First Image

Tenet Nocsid III stands beside **Red Umbrielor** before dawn, one hand on cold steel, waiting for the **MD-8** system to stop complaining.

The machine is awake before the sun is.

Red Umbrielor does not start cleanly. It complains, coughs, warms, and gradually remembers its job.

The first image should show:

- Tenet’s hand on old scarred steel
- cold before dawn
- MD-8 system complaints
- the NCI staging yard still half-asleep
- a machine with history
- a worker who knows how to wait without wasting time

Reader feeling:

**This world is entered through cold steel and a stubborn engine.**

---

## Chapter Setting

Primary location:

**NCI staging yard before MITE II departure.**

The yard should feel:

- industrial
- patched
- lived-in
- cold
- practical
- privately competent
- less polished than Prime Ops
- full of people who know machines by sound

Background elements may include:

- older modified tractors
- Mods being coupled
- numbered and named NCI equipment
- MD-8 fuel handling
- crew moving in pre-dawn layers
- field lights
- vapor, cold exhaust, frost, and machinery noise
- Lindy coordinating dispatch activity
- a sense that departure is a ritual, not a spectacle

---

## Lindy Nocsid Chapter 1 Canon

**Lindy Nocsid** is Tenet Nocsid III’s younger sister.

Chapter 1 role:

**NCI dispatcher / staging-yard coordinator**

Lindy brings or transmits the MITE II job packet to Tenet.

Core vibe:

- sharp
- bruised
- funny when cornered
- allergic to pity
- rough past, good heart somewhere
- knows how NCI bends rules
- understands that clean paperwork can be scrubbed paperwork
- not corrupt by default
- not innocent either

Lindy is not a neutral messenger. She is family, which means the packet arrives with history, friction, old bruises, and unsaid care.

Do not fully define Lindy’s rough past in Chapter 1.

Chapter 1 should imply that Lindy has survived things, made mistakes, and learned how paperwork hides blood without turning her into a lore explainer.

---

## Opening Problem: Red Umbrielor’s Defrost

Red Umbrielor’s defrost is not working properly.

Tenet has to turn on the **turkey roaster** long enough to warm the cab and remove his outer layers.

The turkey roaster is a crew / NCI field term for an improvised or auxiliary cab-heating setup used when proper defrost or heat is lagging.

The turkey roaster beat should accomplish several things:

- establish practical field texture
- show Red Umbrielor as old, beloved, and imperfect
- let Tenet solve a small problem without drama
- give him an excuse to remove outer layers and settle into the cab
- show that NCI gear works because people know its defects

Do not over-explain the turkey roaster.

Let it appear as normal field language.

Possible tone:

Red Umbrielor’s defrost does not fail catastrophically. It fails in the way old trusted machines fail: predictably, annoyingly, and with the confidence that somebody who loves it will compensate.

## Beat Sheet

### Beat 1: Hand on Cold Steel

Tenet stands beside Red Umbrielor before dawn.

He waits for the MD-8 system to stop complaining.

He knows the sound of the machine well enough to distinguish a real problem from ordinary morning hatred.

Purpose:

- open with tactile work
- introduce Tenet as patient and competent
- introduce Red Umbrielor as character-adjacent

Reader learns:

- Tenet is experienced
- Red Umbrielor is old and trusted
- this world values practical competence

### Beat 2: The Machine Complains Correctly

Tenet runs the opening checks.

The MD-8 system complains, the defrost lags, and the cab refuses to clear properly.

Tenet turns on the turkey roaster.

The cab warms enough for him to peel off his outer layers.

Purpose:

- make the machine feel real
- introduce field slang
- avoid opening with exposition
- show Tenet solving small problems calmly

Reader learns:

- equipment failure is ordinary
- Red Umbrielor has personality through defect and history
- Tenet works by habit, not panic

### Beat 3: Under-Seat Weight

As Tenet removes or shifts his outer layers, the under-seat compartment becomes relevant.

Grandfather Ten’s pistol is stored in the **under-seat compartment**.

Tenet may check that the compartment is closed, feel the weight through habit, or avoid looking at it too long.

Purpose:

- introduce the pistol quietly
- connect the physical cab to family legacy
- plant emotional gravity without explaining it

Reader learns:

- Tenet carries an inherited object
- the object matters
- the reader is not yet supposed to know everything

Do not reveal:

- whether the pistol is a literal key
- Rob’s full death-chain
- the final confrontation at The House Ten Built

### Beat 4: Rob’s Empty Seat

The cab’s passenger side reminds Tenet of Rob Holliday.

Rob enters through absence.

Possible beat options:

- Tenet avoids looking at the passenger seat too long.
- The turkey roaster makes the cab smell like a previous trip.
- Tenet almost hears Rob call the defrost problem “bullshit.”
- A small mark, strap, or scuff reminds him of Rob’s duffel during The Last Traverse.
- Lindy mentions Rob and immediately makes it sound like a joke because sincerity costs too much before dawn.

Purpose:

- let grief enter through object and habit
- avoid flashback dump
- establish Rob as emotionally important
- show that Lindy and Tenet grieve differently

Reader learns:

- Rob is gone
- Tenet’s grief is controlled, not absent
- Lindy knows the wound too
- The Last Traverse matters but remains mostly hidden

### Beat 5: Yard Motion

The NCI staging yard wakes around Tenet.

Crew prepare equipment.

Mods are checked.

Private contractor culture appears through small actions and speech.

Lindy coordinates dispatch with more authority than her title suggests.

Purpose:

- introduce NCI as a working culture
- imply legacy and family without explaining the whole company
- show that MITE II is a real operation
- place Lindy inside NCI’s practical nervous system

Reader learns:

- NCI is competent and old
- this is not Prime Ops
- private work has its own rituals
- Lindy belongs to NCI but is not comfortably soft inside it

### Beat 6: Lindy Arrives with the Packet

**Lindy Nocsid** brings or transmits the MITE II job packet.

She is Tenet’s younger sister and the NCI dispatcher / staging-yard coordinator.

The packet is **both physical and digital**.

Physical packet:

- field copy
- printed, marked, durable, possibly clipped or sleeved
- used because digital systems fail, freeze, desync, or get argued with

Digital packet:

- official route lease data
- authorization chain
- equipment manifests
- contract designation
- updates tied to NCI systems

Purpose:

- introduce the job through family, not neutral bureaucracy
- show NCI field habits
- give Tenet something to react to
- establish Lindy as sharp, bruised, and difficult to read

Reader learns:

- MITE II is private survey / proofing work
- Tenet is the NCI MITE II HEO-IC / field lead by contract designation
- he is not an official Prime Ops Lead
- Lindy is family and dispatcher, which complicates the packet handoff

### Beat 7: What the Packet Says

Tenet reviews enough of the packet for the reader to understand the mission surface.

The packet says or implies:

- MITE II is NCI-run.
- NCI leased proposed route access for surveying and proofing.
- Tenet is contract-designated HEO-IC / field lead.
- The job is private survey / route-proofing work.
- The job is legal on paper.

Purpose:

- establish the job without overloading the reader
- show Tenet’s professional reading habits
- contrast normal mission language with what comes next

Reader learns:

- this is not a random adventure
- Tenet is being paid to do specific work
- paperwork matters in this world

### Beat 8: Horus Approved It

Tenet sees Horus Skelton’s approval on the job packet.

He says:

**“What the—”**

The line should be short and instinctive.

Not theatrical.

Not a speech.

He is reacting before he polishes the reaction.

Purpose:

- establish the first wrong detail
- show that Horus’s involvement is unusual at this scale
- let Tenet’s expertise reveal danger before the plot explains it

Reader learns:

- Horus Skelton matters
- Horus is high authority
- this packet is over-authorized
- Tenet notices the mismatch

### Beat 9: Lindy Reaction

Lindy notices Tenet’s reaction.

Possible Lindy behavior:

- says, “Yeah. Saw that.”
- pretends not to hear, then proves she heard everything
- asks if he wants the official answer or the useful one
- says the approval came through clean
- jokes weakly and fails
- warns Tenet not to overthink clean paperwork
- admits the packet came down fast

Purpose:

- make the wrongness familial and social
- show that Lindy notices what Tenet notices
- show that she may know more than she says
- create tension without revealing conspiracy

Reader learns:

- the approval is not obviously illegal
- it is uncomfortable because of scale and cleanliness
- people survive by knowing when not to ask questions
- Lindy knows that clean paperwork can be scrubbed paperwork

### Beat 10: Tenet Reads the Scale

Tenet does not think “conspiracy.”

He thinks like a field operator.

Possible internal logic:

- a small route-proofing lease should not need director-level approval
- if Horus signed directly, someone wanted the packet insulated
- clean paperwork can be worse than dirty paperwork
- the job may still be honest, but it has been touched by power

Purpose:

- keep Tenet practical
- avoid premature noir monologue
- establish his suspicion as expertise

Reader learns:

- Tenet knows how authority normally moves
- the mismatch is procedural, not mystical
- the first crack in the job is bureaucratic

### Beat 11: The Choice

Tenet accepts the packet and the job anyway.

Reasons:

- the work is still work
- he has a crew
- the machine is ready enough
- imperfect paperwork does not stop every Traverse
- someone competent has to keep people alive
- he wants honest pay for honest work
- his sister is standing there with the packet, and that makes refusal less simple

Purpose:

- show Tenet’s core engine
- prove he is not naive or reckless
- keep the story moving

Reader learns:

- Tenet’s first response to wrongness is to do the job right
- this is how the trap gets him
- Lindy and Tenet can disagree without explaining the whole family wound

### Beat 12: Departure Motion

The chapter ends with Red Umbrielor moving or about to move.

Possible final beat:

Tenet closes the packet, secures it, glances once at the under-seat compartment, then starts Red Umbrielor forward.

Lindy watches him go with the expression of someone who delivered a clean packet and still expects blood on the corners.

The yard opens around him.

The job is legal.

The machine runs.

The wrongness comes with them.

Purpose:

- end on commitment
- move from preparation into journey
- make the first chapter feel like ignition

Reader learns:

- the story has started rolling
- the reader is now On Traverse with Tenet

## Chapter 1 Reader Knowledge Target

By the end of Chapter 1, the reader should know:

- Tenet Nocsid III is experienced and practical.
- Red Umbrielor is old, trusted, and central.
- MD-8 fuel and machine procedure matter.
- The turkey roaster is part of Red Umbrielor’s field reality.
- NCI is family-rooted contractor culture.
- Lindy Nocsid is Tenet’s younger sister and NCI dispatcher / staging-yard coordinator.
- Lindy has a rough past and a good heart somewhere, but the exact facts remain hidden.
- MITE II is private survey / route-proofing work.
- Tenet is contract-designated HEO-IC / field lead, not Prime Ops Lead.
- Rob Holliday is gone and emotionally important.
- Grandfather Ten’s pistol is stored in the under-seat compartment.
- Horus Skelton approved the job packet.
- Tenet finds that strange.
- Lindy notices Tenet noticing.
- Tenet takes the job anyway.

Reader feeling:

**The job is legal. The machine runs. The grief is quiet. The stamp is wrong. Family handed him the packet.**

Do not reveal:

- Rob was poisoned.
- Chrono-Floquet Distillate exists.
- Crystalpnomia is false in Rob’s case.
- Horus’s full resentment over Tenet I choosing Rob.
- the assassin inside NCI.
- the hidden formation near the Claw.
- the footprints.
- the Core.
- gateway mechanics.
- full Aeonos / Àæonos cosmology.
- all secrets of The House Ten Built.
- the final confrontation between Rob and Horus.
- the full truth of Lindy’s rough past.
- whether Lindy knows more about the MITE II job than she admits.

Chapter 1 should make the reader curious, not briefed.

## Drafting Tone Notes

Chapter 1 should feel:

- cold
- practical
- tactile
- lightly funny through field language
- emotionally restrained
- suspicious but not paranoid
- industrial
- grounded in work
- sharpened by sibling friction

Avoid:

- lore dump openings
- dream prologues
- cosmic exposition
- too many proper nouns in the first page
- Tenet explaining himself to the reader
- immediate melodrama over Rob
- Lindy explaining her backstory

These are seeds, not final draft lines.

- Red Umbrielor complained before dawn.
- Tenet kept one hand on the cold steel and waited for the MD-8 system to finish lying to itself.
- The defrost had quit sometime in the night, because old machines believed in timing.
