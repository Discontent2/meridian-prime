# Meridian Prime Character Visual Guide

> Character-focused visual development guide for the core Book One cast.

**Canon Status:** VISUAL DEVELOPMENT GUIDE, EXPANDABLE

This guide supports character art, comic and graphic novel adaptation, image prompting, tabletop reference, and game visual development for Meridian Prime.

It preserves locked visual canon, reduces image-generation drift, and identifies unresolved visual questions that need approval. It does not override character, species, story, legacy, traverse-system, or visual-style source files.

## Canon Labels for Character Art

Use these labels in prompts, concept sheets, comic adaptation notes, game references, and tabletop portraits.

### Canon

Already established in the repo or explicitly locked. Canon character markers should be preserved unless a later source-of-truth file revises them.

### Proposed Canon

A strong suggestion awaiting approval. Use this for details that fit canon but are not yet locked.

### Visual Development Concept

Exploratory design work used to test silhouettes, costume directions, mood, palettes, props, and adaptation logic. Useful but not binding.

### Non-Canon Promo / Adaptation Art

Promotional or adaptation-facing art that may compress, foreshadow, stylize, or combine story material in ways that are not literal in-universe canon.

## Character Design Principles

Meridian Prime characters should read through:

- work
- pressure
- role
- route history
- field competence
- class culture
- species body plan
- machinery relationships
- grief or concealment pressure
- official versus field-world positioning

Visual design should support the novella's restraint. Book One character art should not make everyone look like they already know the full saga. Early designs should prioritize field work, procedure, old machinery, grief under control, and the difference between clean paperwork and lived truth.

A good character design should answer:

- What work has shaped this person?
- What system do they trust?
- What system do they distrust?
- What do they know how to survive?
- What pressure do they carry without explaining it aloud?

## Tenet Nocsid III

**Primary unresolved visual target.**

### Canon Role

Tenet Nocsid III is:

- primary protagonist
- experienced Traverse operator
- NCI MITE II HEO-IC / field lead by contract designation
- practical, field-proven, socially capable, and grief-marked
- someone who wants honest pay for honest work, while the world keeps making honest work impossible
- visually tied to Red Umbrielor, NCI, old machinery, Grandfather Ten's pistol, Rob's absence, and honest work under dishonest systems

Tenet is not a rookie, tourist, sheltered observer, conspiracy hunter by default, chosen one, or clean corporate hero. He becomes dangerous because competence, grief, and family history place him where buried truth becomes unavoidable.

### Canon-Facing Visual Direction

Tenet should look like someone who knows what cold steel means before dawn.

He should read as:

- competent before heroic
- practical before dramatic
- field-proven before iconic
- grief-marked without looking broken
- suspicious through habit, not paranoia
- capable of command without theatrical authority

His design should feel like it belongs inside Red Umbrielor's cab and outside in route conditions. He should look able to run checks, read weather, talk to a mechanic, hear a machine lie, argue with paperwork, and keep a crew alive.

### Visual Anchors

Canon-facing Tenet art should include or imply:

- Traverse workwear
- practical cold-weather layers
- NCI field culture
- old-machine familiarity
- worn gloves
- utility pockets or field tools
- cab-life details
- under-seat pistol awareness
- posture of someone used to command without theatrics
- eyes and expression shaped by suspicion, restraint, and field habit
- clothing that can plausibly work inside Red Umbrielor's cab and outside in route conditions

### Proposed Canon Needed

The following details are not yet locked in the source files and require approval before becoming canon:

- exact age-read
- hair color, texture, and style
- face shape
- build
- skin tone
- facial hair or clean-shaven status
- signature garment
- recurring NCI family marker
- visible scars or lack of scars
- exact way he carries field tools
- exact way Grandfather Ten's pistol affects his silhouette or posture

### Proposed Canon Direction 1: Field-Worn Contractor Lead

**Status:** Proposed Canon.

**Silhouette:** Medium-heavy practical field silhouette built from layered cold-weather workwear. Not bulky enough to hide movement, not sleek enough to feel corporate.

**Clothing palette:** NCI-worn neutrals, oil-dark browns, faded work black, cold gray, rust red accents tied subtly to Red Umbrielor, and small safety-color details used for function rather than style.

**Posture:** Upright but relaxed. He stands like someone who expects machinery, weather, and people to argue before breakfast.

**Face / expression logic:** Controlled, watchful, dryly skeptical. Grief shows as restraint around the eyes, not theatrical sorrow.

**Key props:** Worn gloves, field jacket, utility pockets, packet or route sheet, cab keys or machine tag, implied under-seat pistol rather than open display.

**Avoid:** Making him look like a soldier, clean officer, noir detective, or rebel icon. This version should be worker-first.

### Proposed Canon Direction 2: Old-Route Machine Operator

**Status:** Proposed Canon.

**Silhouette:** More cab-centered and machine-shaped. Slight forward lean from years of controls, cold starts, inspection walks, and listening to tractor vibration.

**Clothing palette:** Dark field layers, faded red-brown machine stains, gray thermal layers, rubbed black gloves, old canvas, scratched metal fasteners.

**Posture:** Habitual readiness. One hand often near steel, controls, a door frame, a handhold, or a tool. He looks most natural touching machinery.

**Face / expression logic:** Focused, patient, and difficult to surprise. Suspicion appears as professional pattern recognition.

**Key props:** Work gloves, cab rag, small diagnostic habit-object, field radio, folded route paper, machine-worn jacket sleeves.

**Avoid:** Turning him into a superheroic machine pilot or greasepunk caricature. He is not flashy about machine competence.

### Proposed Canon Direction 3: Grief-Contained NCI Heir

**Status:** Proposed Canon.

**Silhouette:** Practical NCI work silhouette with one or two inherited details that quietly connect him to family history.

**Clothing palette:** Muted NCI contractor colors, weathered dark layers, a small family-linked red or brass detail, and field-worn cold gear.

**Posture:** Command-capable but emotionally guarded. He does not collapse under grief; he works around it like a bad joint or old warning light.

**Face / expression logic:** Warmth is possible, but not easily offered. Grief lives in pauses, glances toward empty seats, and how long he avoids looking at certain objects.

**Key props:** Grandfather Ten's pistol as an inheritance object, NCI field marker, old route packet, Red Umbrielor cab environment, Rob's empty passenger seat in composition.

**Avoid:** Making him look like a tragic prince, chosen heir, or revenge poster. The inheritance should feel like responsibility, not destiny cosplay.

### Tenet Design Avoid List

Do not make Tenet:

- a rookie
- a chosen-one fantasy hero
- a generic action soldier
- a noir detective cosplay figure
- a clean corporate officer
- a superheroic machine pilot
- a rebel poster boy too early
- a hollow grief statue
- visually disconnected from Red Umbrielor, NCI, and Traverse work

## Red Umbrielor as Character Extension

**Canon.**

This guide does not fully design Red Umbrielor. It defines how the machine should function visually beside Tenet.

### Locked Anchors

Red Umbrielor is:

- MMS Quadtrack 289
- "Red Umbrielor"
- massive Quadtrack
- number 289
- equipped with a front blade
- connected to a tracked habitation module
- scarred by years of work
- NCI-linked / family-linked
- emotionally tied to Tenet's independence and field life

### Tenet / Red Umbrielor Echo Logic

Tenet and Red Umbrielor should visually echo each other:

- old but capable
- scarred but maintained
- independent but burdened by inheritance
- practical, not polished
- field-proven rather than glamorous
- carrying more history than the official job packet admits

The machine should not be a background vehicle only. In Book One imagery, Red Umbrielor is part of Tenet's visual identity, almost a weathered metal second body.

Detailed exterior, interior, cab layout, scar map, name placement, number placement, and Mod connection should be handled in `docs/visual-development/red-umbrielor-and-traverse-vehicles.md`.

- signs of too much responsibility too early

- exact age-read
- hair color, cut, and styling
- build
- skin tone
- signature garment
- NCI marker or family marker

- purely corporate

## Rob Holliday

**Locked visual canon.**

### Locked Visual Markers

Rob Holliday has these established visual markers:

- white hair swept / combed to one side
- square glasses
- mustache connected to goatee
- curled eyebrow tips
- overweight but not obese
- warm smile
- salmon polo shirt

### Canon Role

Rob Holliday is:

- former superintendent
- co-founder of NCI with Tenet Nocsid I
- mentor / uncle-like figure to Tenet Nocsid III
- old friend of Tenet I
- old friend of Horus Skelton before the rupture
- emotionally central to The Last Traverse
- funny
- warm
- blunt
- story-rich
- judgment-driven
- known for saying "Bullshit"

### Visual Direction

Rob should look warm, funny, and trustworthy at first glance, but not harmless. His design should carry old field judgment beneath the salmon polo and warm smile.

The salmon polo is important because it makes Rob visually human, specific, and slightly disarming inside a cold industrial pressure-world. It should not be replaced by generic expedition gear in canon-facing portraits.

### Preservation Rules

- Do not remove the salmon polo.
- Do not remove square glasses.
- Do not make him bald.
- Do not make him obese.
- Do not make his eyebrows ordinary if the image is canon-facing.
- Do not turn him into an ominous secret-master.
- Do not treat him as mere comic relief.
- Do not make his warmth erase his judgment.

## Kavo "Forest" Rill

**Canon with locked / established anchors.**

### Locked / Established Anchors

Kavo "Forest" Rill is:

- real name: Kavo Rill
- human / crew name: Forest Rill
- Velocisapien / Velociraptoroid
- Mountaineer / route guide / wilderness EMT / GPR path-reader
- pressure-runner, boiler-ska route culture
- relaxed menace
- short sparse red proto-quill mohawk
- black-frame shades when not wearing route goggles
- subtle checker trim
- light route harness
- pressure-route badges
- claw-wraps
- relaxed in posture until danger requires motion

### Visual Direction

Forest should read as terrain truth. His design should communicate that he sees the route before others admit there is a route problem.

He should be quick, sly, relaxed, and dangerous, but not because he is a generic raptor predator. His speed and perception should feel social, urban, rhythmic, pressure-route trained, and professionally earned.

### Preservation Rules

- Preserve Velocisapien body plan.
- Preserve universal tail and saurian anatomy.
- Preserve four-fingered clawed hands and four-toed clawed feet.
- Preserve route harness and pressure-runner logic.
- Preserve short sparse red proto-quill mohawk unless a future canon file revises it.
- Use 2-tone ska / boiler-ska influence through posture, trim, attitude, movement, and street-route styling, not musical props.

### Avoid

Do not make Forest:

- a generic raptor scout
- a ninja
- an assassin archetype
- an animalistic alien
- a velociraptor with human clothes pasted on
- a human with scales pasted on
- oversized punk mohawk by default
- literal band costume
- a creature who reads as nonverbal or uncivilized

## Group Visual Logic: The Three Truths

Rudy, Forest, and Ildi should act as Tenet's three pressure gauges.

| Character | Truth Function | Visual Framing |
|---|---|---|
| Rudy Calder | machine truth | machine strain, tools, engine sound, repairs, practical failure, grease logic |
| Kavo "Forest" Rill | terrain truth | route lines, GPR awareness, terrain posture, exterior feeds, stillness before motion |
| Ildi Vey | signal truth | signal artifacts, old infrastructure, dead channels, interface glow, contradiction |

### Comic Use

- Rudy panels should favor mechanical spaces, tool gestures, vibration, weight, and practical fault cues.
- Forest panels should favor route geometry, exterior danger, posture shifts, line-of-sight, and terrain silence.
- Ildi panels should favor interface light, signal interruption, dead-channel noise, diagnostic overlays, and half-awake infrastructure.

### Concept Art Use

The three should be visually distinct but not equally theatrical. Their designs should signal what kind of wrongness they can detect.

### Game UI Use

In a game adaptation, the three truths can become visual diagnostic lanes:

- machine status / Rudy
- terrain status / Forest
- signal status / Ildi

This should not turn them into simple RPG classes. Their function is story pressure first.

## Character Image Prompt Rules

When drafting image prompts:

1. State whether the image is Canon, Proposed Canon, Visual Development Concept, or Non-Canon Promo / Adaptation Art.
2. Preserve locked visual markers.
3. Preserve species body plans.
4. Preserve Book One reveal discipline.
5. Do not add unauthorized props, factions, scars, uniforms, weapons, or backstory symbols.
6. Do not make every character look equally dramatic.
7. Let role and work shape costume.
8. Use music-genre logic as cultural design grammar, not literal performance gear.
9. For Saurasapiens, avoid ordinary human hands or feet with scales painted over them.
10. For Antisapiens, avoid elves, orcs, zombies, or generic blue cyberpunk humans.
11. For Meridians, show work-culture identity rather than treating them as plain baseline humans.
12. For Book One art, do not visually spoil Core mechanics, full Aeonos / Àæonos cosmology, Rob's poisoning truth, the assassin inside NCI, or full Horus plan.
13. Do not make Meridian Prime look like Antarctica with renamed labels.
14. Keep early Book One art procedural, cold, field-grounded, and emotionally restrained.

## Working Rule

A Meridian Prime character should look like they belong to a job, a pressure system, and a history.

The design should not shout the secret. It should make the viewer feel that this person has already learned which parts of the world lie first.
