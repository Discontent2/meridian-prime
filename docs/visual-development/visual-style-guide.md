# Meridian Prime Visual Style Guide

> High-level visual development guide for the Meridian Prime universe.

**Canon Status:** VISUAL DEVELOPMENT GUIDE, EXPANDABLE

This file centralizes visual language already established or implied across Meridian Prime canon. It is intended to help artists, writers, game designers, comic teams, and tabletop adaptation teams make visual choices that feel coherent across the universe.

This file does not override the species guide, traverse-system guide, character files, legacy files, or Book One story structure files. When a visual question affects story, species, technology, character, setting, or reveal order, the relevant core canon file remains the source of truth.

## Canon Labels for Visual Work

Use these labels clearly in all visual-development notes, image prompts, concept sheets, cover studies, and adaptation materials.

### Canon

Already established in the repo or explicitly locked by the creator. Canon visual markers should be preserved unless a later canon file revises them.

### Proposed Canon

A strong suggestion awaiting approval. Proposed Canon should not be treated as final until it is approved and added to a source-of-truth file.

### Visual Development Concept

Exploratory design logic used to test mood, silhouette, composition, material, or adaptation direction. It may be useful even if it never becomes literal canon.

### Non-Canon Promo / Adaptation Art

A promotional, cover, menu, reference, or marketing image that may compress, stylize, combine, or foreshadow elements in ways that are useful outside literal in-universe continuity.

## Core Visual Identity

Meridian Prime should feel:

- industrial
- cold
- pressure-haunted
- procedural
- lived-in
- beautiful but lethal
- full of patched machinery
- shaped by heat culture, crystal growth, route danger, old infrastructure, and official concealment

The visual world should feel like a place where survival depends on field procedure, working machines, old routes, quiet expertise, and knowing when official paperwork is lying.

Meridian Prime is not Antarctica with renamed labels. Real expedition logic may inspire procedure, survival systems, route discipline, and field culture, but the planet must remain its own pressure-world.

## Visual Design Pillars

### 1. Cold Industrial Survival

Cold is not just weather. It is a design pressure.

Use:

- cold steel
- frost-crusted machinery
- insulated work layers
- pre-dawn staging yards
- vapor, exhaust, condensation, and ice-load texture
- glove-worn controls
- lights cutting through cold air
- field gear that looks used rather than styled

Avoid turning cold into clean postcard emptiness. The cold should cling to work, machinery, breath, metal, fabric, and exposed route infrastructure.

### 2. Pressure and Heat Culture

Heat matters because cold and pressure rule the world around it.

Use:

- boiler-room warmth against hostile exterior cold
- heat-safe clothing
- valve logic
- thermal stains
- pressure gauges
- steam-darkened cloth
- heated cabs and improvised warmers
- cultural markings tied to heat, route duty, mourning, or survival

Aeonolacertian visual culture should especially communicate heat, pressure, rhythm, brass, copper, color, and survival under extreme conditions.

### 3. Patched Machinery and Procedural Danger

Machines should look maintained by people who know exactly which failures matter.

Use:

- visible repairs
- patched panels
- named and numbered equipment
- stenciled defect-names on MITE machines
- tool marks
- field modifications
- crew-made labels
- hazard tape, paint, flags, and worn procedural markings
- systems that work because someone kept arguing with them

Do not over-polish field machinery unless the asset is intentionally higher-priority, official, or corporate-clean.

### 4. Route Memory and Hidden History

Routes remember what official records soften.

Use:

- old markers
- route flags
- handwritten annotations
- worn maps
- half-buried infrastructure
- memorial hazard names
- cracked signage
- equipment that carries crew stories
- places that feel known by field people but misrepresented by paperwork

The best route imagery should suggest that the land, machines, and crews remember more than Prime Ops admits.

### 5. Official Control Versus Field Truth

Meridian Prime should visually distinguish official certainty from field knowledge.

Official control may look like:

- clean forms
- polished badges
- controlled palettes
- standardized signage
- tidy UI panels
- approved route packets
- carefully maintained public spaces

Field truth may look like:

- grease pencil corrections
- informal marks
- patched interfaces
- worn maps
- crew annotations
- improvised fixes
- old signal ghosts
- people trusting machines, terrain, and signals more than paperwork

### 6. Beauty Under Lethal Conditions

The world should be harsh, but not visually dead.

Use beauty through:

- crystal growth
- furnace light
- cold dawn color
- frost on scarred steel
- black flags against hostile terrain
- glowing signal traces
- ritual paint
- old copper and oxidized green
- cab light in a dark route field

Beauty should never erase danger. It should sharpen it.

## Technology Aesthetic Hierarchy

Keep technology visually ranked by culture, funding, and philosophy.

| Group / System | Visual Direction |
|---|---|
| Prime Ops | Useful, controlling, bureaucratic, official. Clean enough to imply authority, practical enough to run operations, cold enough to hide decisions inside process. |
| NCI | Family-rooted, practical, modified, scarred, deniable. Older machines, private fixes, field knowledge, inherited equipment, and marks of use. |
| SpITE equipment | Higher-priority, cleaner, better-supported, more corporate, better maintained, less visibly personality-laden than MITE gear. |
| MITE equipment | Older, patched, field-modified, personality-laden, named, numbered, and loved through complaint. |
| Aeonolacertian technology | Apparently primitive steampunk, but actually advanced. Brass, copper, pressure devices, boiler culture, ritual engineering, and hidden sophistication. |
| Meridian human technology | Degraded dieselpunk. Industrial, repaired, bureaucratically controlled, district-shaped, and often older than official confidence suggests. |
| Lodestar technology | Proposed Canon / Visual Development Concept: solarpunk aesthetic used by its inhabitants. Keep this restrained until supported by a dedicated source file. |
| Absconditian technology | Cyberpunk aesthetic and extremely advanced. Signal systems, bio-cabling, black-glass interfaces, stealth, resonance, and controlled cybernetic elegance. |

Technology should show who controls it, who maintains it, who fears it, and who has to survive when it breaks.

## Species Visual System Overview

### Aeonolacertians / Saurasapiens

**Canon.**

Aeonolacertians are a mixed saurian civilization native to Matter Aeonos. Saurasapien is the common umbrella field term for the recognizable morph-type expressions.

Core visual logic:

- mixed saurian civilization
- mostly humanoid body plans
- universal tails
- four-fingered clawed hands
- four-toed clawed feet
- expressive saurian faces
- vivid scale palettes
- crests, frills, feathers, quills, spines, and morph-type traits
- body-shape ecology plus pressure-rhythm aesthetics

They should not look like generic lizardfolk, simple dinosaur people, or humans wearing reptile masks. Their bodies should look adapted to tools, speech, clothing, class culture, field work, ritual, and pressure civilization.

#### Saurasapien Morph-Type Visual Lanes

| Morph Type | Visual Lane | Design Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Velocisapien / Velociraptoroid | 2-tone ska / boiler-ska / pressure-route street style | courier swagger, route speed, underclass confidence, offbeat survival |
| Tyrannosapien / Tyrannisauroid | doom metal / black ceremonial commandwear | command weight, war authority, dread, final-stand power |
| Bajadrasapien / Bajadasauroid | ethereal goth / darkwave / steam-oracle drone | resonance, ritual science, pressure memory, strange elegance |
| Ankylosapien / Ankylosauroid | crust punk / sludge metal / foundry-hardcore | defense, infrastructure, labor endurance, barricade loyalty |
| Cerasapien / Ceratopsinoid | romantic goth / funeral doom / civic procession | law, family, mourning, ceremonial guardianship, grief as order |

Genre influence is design grammar, not literal musical performance.

Translate genre through:

- fashion
- posture
- attitude
- silhouette
- ritual styling
- environment
- ornamentation
- class culture
- body-shape ecology
- materials
- movement language

Do not default to:

- instruments
- microphones
- guitars
- drum kits
- concert stages
- parody costumes
- marching-band props

A Saurasapien lineup may look band-like in Non-Canon Promo / Adaptation Art, but in canon-facing work it should read as five subcultures from one pressure civilization.

### Antisapiens / Absconditians

**Canon.**

Antisapien is the common umbrella field term for Absconditian / áæbsconditian phenotype people. They are blue-skinned anti-matter humanoids with black irises, magenta pupils, teal tapetum lucidum, and signal-band phenotype logic.

Core visual logic:

- blue-skinned anti-matter humanoids
- black irises
- magenta pupils
- teal tapetum lucidum
- cyber-resonance
- bio-cabling
- signal markings
- ritual makeup
- interface behavior
- signal-band movement and glow rhythm

Antisapien bands are primarily readable through signal behavior, glow rhythm, bio-cabling, technology access, movement, ritual styling, and interface posture.

#### Antisapien Signal-Band Visual Lanes

| Signal Band | Visual Lane | Design Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Nullsapien | ambient | hidden control, wireless authority, black-glass calm, AI sovereignty |
| Veilsapien | underground hip hop | stealth, coded movement, street intelligence, AR field adaptation |
| Amaranthsapien | dungeonsynth | VR ritual, dream-interface, synthetic mysticism, resonance scholarship |
| Vaultsapien | industrial | archives, identity systems, continuity machinery, ordered pressure |
| Lividisapien | punk | contradiction, exposed wiring, phreak culture, raw truth, recovered history |

Do not make Antisapiens elves, orcs, zombies, generic cyberpunk humans, or horror shorthand. Ritual makeup is cultural and signal-aesthetic, not proof of death or decay.

## Human / Meridian Visual Logic

**Canon foundation with Proposed Canon / Visual Development Concept expansion where noted.**

Meridians are human-descended colony people shaped by World Works control, corporate infrastructure, restricted education, occupational tracking, district culture, and generations of survival under official narratives.

Meridians should never feel like ordinary humans standing between cooler nonhuman peoples. Their visual strength is work-culture identity.

Core human visual logic:

- workwear
- dieselpunk degradation
- practical cold-weather layers
- field patches
- family contractor clothing
- industrial survival gear
- Prime Ops paperwork and control aesthetics
- district and job-based culture
- clothing, tools, posture, workplace habits, grime, polish, music logic, and proximity to official truth

### Meridian Class-Culture Visual Lanes

| Meridian Class | Visual Lane | Design Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Continuants | civic synthpop / corporate hymn | approved memory, polished control, cheerful order, institutional compliance |
| Primehands | heavy metal / work chant | mass, muscle, launchpad thunder, logistics, restricted-zone labor |
| Dieselwrights | blues rock / rockabilly / greasepunk | repair culture, oil, engines, stubborn survival, machine intelligence |
| Scarwalkers | desert rock / outlaw country / weird western | routes, anomaly edges, maps, field truth, survival beyond approved records |
| Ghostwavers | post-punk / pirate radio dub / signal collage | static, forbidden memory, street broadcast, rumor, signal ghosts |

Meridian classes are colony-function class cultures, not biological castes. They should be readable through clothing, tools, posture, workplace setting, district cues, and scene rhythm.

## Traverse Ops Visual Language

**Canon.**

Traverse Ops should look like moving industrial survival systems, not ordinary convoys.

A Traverse is a procedural field organism: tractors, Mods, fuel, route logic, maintenance, hazard marking, command hierarchy, field medicine, signal monitoring, and crew memory moving together through lethal terrain.

Visual anchors:

- traverse tractors
- Mods
- fuel bladders
- GPR systems
- Trail flags
- black-flagged Path logic
- Track Bikes
- Magic Carpet / Recovery Sled
- Tool
- route paperwork
- cold exhaust
- field lights
- procedural hazard markings
- numbered equipment
- cab interiors that look lived-in and worked-in
- mechanic storage, berthing, comms, kitchen, and field lab modules

### MITE Equipment

MITE equipment should look:

- older
- patched
- named
- numbered
- field-modified
- personality-laden
- scarred
- field-loved
- practical because crews know its defects

MITE machines are often insult-named by crews. This is affection, superstition, frustration, and survival knowledge.

### SpITE Equipment

SpITE equipment should look:

- higher-priority
- cleaner
- better-supported
- more corporate-maintained
- less visibly personality-laden
- more likely to feel official and resourced

SpITE should not look soft. It should still be dangerous field work, but with better iron and more official confidence.

### Prime Ops Versus On Traverse

Prime Ops should feel like a practical control point that decides what officially happened.

On Traverse should feel like a temporary survival culture where machinery, terrain, route rules, weather, and procedure matter more than normal hierarchy.

## Key Character Visual Anchors

This guide does not fully design every major character. It preserves locked anchors and identifies where dedicated character sheets are needed.

### Tenet Nocsid III

**Canon-facing notes.**

Tenet is:

- an experienced Traverse operator
- practical
- field-proven
- grief-marked
- tied visually to Red Umbrielor, NCI, old machinery, Grandfather Ten's pistol, and honest work under dishonest systems

Tenet should not look like a rookie, tourist, chosen one, or generic action hero. He should read first as someone who knows what cold steel means before dawn.

Exact physical appearance should be locked in a dedicated character visual guide.

### Rob Holliday

**Locked visual canon.**

Rob Holliday has these established visual markers:

- white hair swept / combed to one side
- square glasses
- mustache connected to goatee
- curled eyebrow tips
- overweight but not obese
- warm smile
- salmon polo shirt

Rob should not be redesigned away from these anchors. His warmth and humor matter visually, but he should not become mere comic relief. His final visual language should carry judgment, regret, and old field trust under the smile.

### Red Umbrielor / MMS Quadtrack 289

**Locked visual anchors.**

Red Umbrielor is:

- massive MMS Quadtrack
- number 289
- equipped with a front blade
- connected to a tracked habitation module
- scarred by years of work
- NCI-linked / family-linked
- emotionally tied to Tenet's independence and field life

Red Umbrielor is not a generic tractor. It is Tenet's signature machine and a major part of his visual identity.

Detailed vehicle reference should be handled in a dedicated vehicle guide.

### Kavo "Forest" Rill

**Locked visual anchors.**

Kavo "Forest" Rill is:

- Velocisapien / Velociraptoroid
- short sparse red proto-quill mohawk
- black-frame shades when not wearing route goggles
- subtle checker trim
- light route harness
- pressure-route badges
- claw-wraps
- relaxed in posture until danger requires motion

Forest should not become a generic raptor scout, ninja, assassin, or animalistic alien. His speed and perception should feel social, urban, rhythmic, pressure-route trained, and professionally earned.

## Book One Reveal Discipline for Art

Book One is currently structured as a novella. Visual development should support restraint, not inflate the first story into a full encyclopedia.

Use this rule:

**Reveal enough to make the next route necessary, not enough to solve the whole world.**

### Early Book One Art May Show

- NCI staging yard
- Red Umbrielor
- cold industrial work
- job packet wrongness
- Horus approval stamp unease
- MITE II Mod train
- Rudy / Forest / Ildi as practical pressure gauges
- ghost handshake as signal unease
- pressure ridges and growth rollers as terrain wrongness
- Grandfather Ten's pistol as inheritance object, without explaining its full meaning
- Rob's empty passenger seat as grief image

### Early Book One Art Should Not Fully Reveal

- Core mechanics
- full Aeonos / Àæonos cosmology
- true nature of the hidden formation
- exact origin of footprints
- Chrono-Floquet Distillate truth
- assassin inside NCI
- full Horus plan
- the complete meaning of The House Ten Built
- the full function of Grandfather Ten's pistol

### Reveal-Safe Visual Mood by Early Chapter

- Chapter 1: work, machine, grief, family, paperwork wrongness.
- Chapter 2: movement, crew pressure gauges, legal mission surface, ghost handshake.
- Chapter 3: route label mismatch, loadout mismatch, corridor unease.
- Chapter 4: quiet comms, restricted information, dead channel that is not dead enough.
- Chapter 5: terrain wrongness, pressure ridges, growth rollers, route danger.
- Chapter 6 and beyond: hidden formation and footprints may enter, but still without solving their origin or full cosmology.

## Adaptation Art Labels

### Canon

Use for:

- official character markers
- established species anatomy
- confirmed vehicle identifiers
- known props and route equipment
- story-accurate panels or illustrations

Canon art should preserve locked details and reveal order.

### Proposed Canon

Use for:

- proposed costume refinements
- suggested palettes
- unapproved character physical details
- new vehicle markings
- new environment layout ideas

Proposed Canon should be easy to approve, reject, or revise.

### Visual Development Concept

Use for:

- silhouette tests
- mood boards
- species variation studies
- vehicle shape exploration
- environment concepts
- game UI experiments
- tabletop reference prototypes

Visual Development Concept art may explore without becoming binding.

### Non-Canon Promo / Adaptation Art

Use for:

- comic covers
- game-menu visuals
- symbolic posters
- band-like Saurasapien lineup compositions
- character montage art
- dramatic foreshadowing pieces
- marketing images that compress multiple story stages

Non-Canon Promo / Adaptation Art must be clearly labeled when it shows material out of literal sequence or reveals more than the novella would reveal at that point.

## Image Prompting Rules

When generating or drafting image prompts:

1. State the canon source or visual principle.
2. Keep the prompt focused.
3. Avoid unauthorized lore.
4. Mark the image as canon-facing, concept-only, or promotional.
5. Preserve species body plans and vehicle logic.
6. Preserve reveal discipline.
7. For character images, do not alter locked visual markers unless explicitly asked.
8. Do not make Meridian Prime look like Antarctica with renamed labels.
9. Do not use literal music props for Saurasapien genre logic unless explicitly marked as Non-Canon Promo / Adaptation Art.
10. Translate music-genre influence through clothing, posture, attitude, silhouette, environment, ornamentation, materials, movement language, and class culture.
11. Keep Book One images narrow, procedural, and emotionally loaded unless the task explicitly calls for broader saga art.
12. Distinguish field truth from official control through materials, lighting, signage, UI, paperwork, and wear patterns.

## Working Rule

Every design should feel like it belongs to the same pressure-world.

A good Meridian Prime image should make the viewer feel that something useful is under strain, something beautiful is dangerous, and someone in the field knows the truth before the paperwork does.
