## Canon Labels for Vehicle Art

Use these labels in prompts, concept sheets, comic adaptation notes, game reference, tabletop materials, and promotional compositions.

### Canon

Already established in the repo or explicitly locked. Canon vehicle names, numbers, functions, and story-facing details should be preserved unless a later source-of-truth file revises them.

### Proposed Canon

A strong suggestion awaiting approval. Use this for placement, proportion, markings, paint shade, cab layout, and other vehicle details that fit canon but are not yet locked.

### Visual Development Concept

Exploratory vehicle design work used to test silhouette, layout, surface treatment, support gear, and adaptation logic. Useful but not binding.

### Non-Canon Promo / Adaptation Art

Promotional or adaptation-facing art that may compress, stylize, foreshadow, or dramatize vehicle systems in ways that are not literal in-universe canon.

## Core Vehicle Design Principle

Traverse vehicles are moving industrial survival systems, not ordinary convoys.

Every Traverse vehicle should communicate:

- field survival
- route procedure
- cold industrial pressure
- patched machinery
- crew dependence
- official versus private equipment hierarchy
- distance from help
- machines that are loved through complaint

A good Meridian Prime vehicle image should feel like the crew depends on the machine, argues with the machine, knows the machine by sound, and fears the one failure the mechanic cannot fix in the field.

Do not use Antarctica itself as a canon setting reference. Meridian Prime may draw from expedition logic, cold operations, route marking, and survival procedure, but it is its own pressure-world.

## Red Umbrielor / MMS Quadtrack 289

**Canon, with unresolved design details marked as Proposed Canon Needed.**

Red Umbrielor is the primary unresolved vehicle visual target. Treat it as Tenet Nocsid III's visual extension, not just a background machine.

### Locked Canon Anchors

Red Umbrielor is:

- MMS Quadtrack 289
- "Red Umbrielor"
- Tenet's signature machine
- massive MMS Quadtrack
- number 289
- equipped with a front blade
- connected to a tracked habitation module
- scarred by years of work
- NCI-linked / family-linked
- emotionally tied to Tenet's independence and field life
- used by Tenet during The Last Traverse
- associated with MITE II / NCI private contractor operations

Red Umbrielor is not an ordinary MITE defect-name tractor. Its name is a special exception tied to NCI founder tradition, Tenet family history, or long-standing crew legend. Do not rename Red Umbrielor as a defect-name tractor.

### Canon-Facing Visual Direction

Red Umbrielor should feel:

- old but capable
- scarred but maintained
- private but operationally serious
- emotionally loaded but not magical
- proven through storms, contracts, breakdowns, field repairs, and bad decisions
- NCI-linked without looking like a polished corporate asset

It should look like a machine that can survive ugly work because people who love it know exactly how it fails.

### Exterior Silhouette

**Proposed Canon Needed for exact proportions.**

Red Umbrielor should read as:

- massive tracked industrial Quadtrack
- broad, heavy, low-confidence-in-the-terrain stance
- front blade as a major silhouette feature
- cab designed for field operation rather than comfort
- rear coupling logic for habitation module / Mod train
- rugged machine profile, not sleek military sci-fi
- visually heavy enough that stopping it feels like an event

The silhouette should make clear that this is a Traverse machine built for survival work and haulage, not a tank, luxury expedition vehicle, hovercraft, or modern snow vehicle with new labels.

### Color and Surface Treatment

**Canon foundation with Proposed Canon Needed for exact paint shade and marking layout.**

Red Umbrielor should show:

- scarred red paint
- work-worn panels
- frost
- dust
- route grime
- field scratches
- patched bodywork
- NCI / family-linked markings where appropriate
- number 289 clearly visible
- name placement for "Red Umbrielor" treated as Proposed Canon Needed

Avoid making Red Umbrielor:

- pristine
- glossy
- toy-like
- luxury-clean
- over-militarized
- too symmetrical or factory-perfect
- visually generic

The red should feel earned and weathered, not showroom-bright. It may be beautiful in cold dawn light, but the beauty should come from use, age, and survival.

### Numbering and Name Placement

**Canon:**

- number 289 must be visible in canon-facing designs
- "Red Umbrielor" is the machine's name
- Red Umbrielor is not to be renamed as a defect-name tractor

**Proposed Canon Needed:** exact placement of the number and name.

Restrained placement options for future approval:

1. Number 289 on the cab side and blade corner.
2. Number 289 on side body panels and rear module connection point.
3. "Red Umbrielor" stenciled near the cab door or side panel.
4. Smaller NCI / family-linked marking near a maintenance panel, not a giant corporate logo.

Do not lock these placements without approval.

### Cab Interior Logic

**Canon foundation with Proposed Canon Needed for exact layout.**

Chapter 1 anchors:

- Tenet prepares Red Umbrielor before dawn.
- The MD-8 system complains.
- Red Umbrielor's defrost is dead or lagging.
- Tenet uses the turkey roaster.
- The under-seat compartment holds Grandfather Ten's pistol.
- Rob's empty passenger seat carries emotional weight.

The cab should feel:

- functional
- worked-in
- cramped enough to carry history, but not cartoonishly tiny
- worn at the controls
- patched at heat and defrost systems
- marked with field labels
- familiar enough that Tenet moves through it by habit
- cold at first, then gradually livable
- full of small evidence that people have survived inside it

Visual features may include:

- worn controls
- old labels and field tags
- frost or fogged glass at cold start
- patched heat / defrost workaround
- turkey roaster as improvised or auxiliary cab-heating setup
- passenger seat treated as grief space
- under-seat compartment treated as quiet inheritance space
- scuffs, straps, and marks that can recall Rob's final ride without over-explaining it

Do not reveal the full meaning of Grandfather Ten's pistol too early. The under-seat compartment may be shown, implied, or handled through Tenet's habit, but Book One early art should not explain whether the pistol is a key or reveal Rob's full death-chain.

**Proposed Canon Needed:**

- exact dashboard layout
- seat arrangement
- size of the cab
- location of the turkey roaster
- exact under-seat compartment design
- visibility of the pistol in Chapter 1 art
- how much cab decoration or NCI personalization appears

### Tenet / Red Umbrielor Relationship

Red Umbrielor shapes Tenet's visual identity.

In character and vehicle art, Tenet should look natural near its steel. The machine is where grief, work, and suspicion overlap.

Tenet and Red Umbrielor should visually echo each other:

- practical
- scarred
- maintained
- independent
- field-proven
- burdened by inheritance
- carrying more history than the official job packet admits

Red Umbrielor should not feel magical or sentient. It should feel character-adjacent because Tenet and the story treat machinery, memory, and field dependence seriously.

## MITE Vehicle Language

**Canon.**

MITE means **Meridian Inland Traverse Extension**.

MITE crews support lesser inland extraction sites, exploration zones, surveying routes, and prospecting operations. They are lower prestige than SpITE crews but often more adaptable, improvisational, and field-clever.

MITE crews get the hand-me-down traverse tractors.

### Canon Anchors

MITE tractors are:

- older
- named
- numbered
- field-modified
- personality-laden
- usually sarcastically named after defects, chronic failures, or personality problems
- stenciled directly with their names

Defect-name examples:

- Tight Tracks
- Cold Start
- Hard Left
- Lazy Blade
- No Reverse
- Soft Brake
- Three Tries
- Leaky Saint

Canon line:

**Machines get insulted. Cracks get respected.**

### Visual Direction

MITE vehicles should look:

- older
- patched
- scarred
- field-modified
- named
- numbered
- loved through complaint
- more personality-laden than higher-priority Traverse vehicles
- practical because crews know their defects

MITE design should communicate that these machines are not worse because they are silly. They are dangerous, useful, stubborn, and intimate. Their flaws are known, named, compensated for, and cursed at.

### Avoid

Do not make MITE vehicles:

- polished corporate assets
- junkyard cartoons
- Mad Max cars
- random scrap monsters
- luxury expedition RVs
- generic sci-fi crawlers
- clean modern snow vehicles with renamed labels
- less competent than their crews know them to be

## SpITE Vehicle Language

**Canon.**

SpITE means **Spire Inland Traverse Excursion**.

SpITE Traverses are higher-priority seasonal Traverses to Spire. SpITE II is the heavy mainbody Traverse to Spire and should feel like the steel avalanche.

### Canon Anchors

Newer higher-priority Traverse tractors resemble large modern tracked agricultural / industrial tractors with:

- heavy tracks
- front blade
- rear articulating crane
- cleaner corporate maintenance profile
- numbered but usually unnamed exterior
- higher-priority SpITE or major Traverse assignment

### Visual Direction

SpITE vehicles should look:

- higher-priority
- cleaner
- better-maintained
- more corporate-supported
- heavy and dangerous
- less externally personality-laden
- numbered but usually unnamed externally

SpITE machines should feel like World Works and Prime Ops committed serious iron to serious work. They should not be soft, sterile, or luxury-clean. They still belong to a lethal pressure-world.

### Avoid

Do not make SpITE vehicles:

- gentle
- decorative
- too pristine to operate in field conditions
- luxury-clean
- identical to MITE machines except for paint
- overly militarized by default
- cartoonishly villainous corporate hardware

## Lead Tractors

**Canon.**

Lead tractors are specialized command / sensing platforms.

### Canon Anchors

Lead tractors:

- resemble compact tracked carrier / groomer-style machines
- carry equipment rather than pulling full Mod trains
- run GPR
- lead route reading
- act as the command / sensing platform for the Traverse
- visually distinguish the Lead's authority

### Visual Direction

Lead tractors should read as route-brain machines, not simple scouts.

Visual cues may include:

- GPR equipment
- compact tracked body
- command visibility
- route-sensing hardware
- field maps / sensor displays
- hazard marking gear
- navigation systems that look physically maintained rather than abstract
- exterior surfaces worn by route work, not combat styling

### Avoid

Do not make Lead tractors:

- sleek recon tanks
- tiny snowmobiles
- generic scout cars
- luxury command pods
- clean drones with treads
- visually disconnected from the rest of Traverse Ops

## Mods / Modules

**Canon.**

Official term: **Module**.

Crew term: **Mod**.

Tractors generally pull modules. Each follow tractor pulls at least one multifunctional Mod and usually a fuel bladder behind the Mod.

### Common Mod Functions

Mods may combine functions rather than existing as single-purpose boxes.

Common functions include:

- berthing / kitchen / comms
- mechanic storage / repair support
- science support / field lab
- cold storage / reefer support
- solar generation / power support

### Visual Direction

Mods should feel:

- modular
- practical
- heavy
- insulated
- towable
- patched
- field-serviceable
- dragged through bad country
- inhabited by crews who know where every useful thing is stored

A Mod should not look like a luxury trailer. It should look like survival infrastructure in a box with scars, couplings, vents, locks, tie-downs, and lived-in field logic.

### Avoid

Do not make Mods:

- sleek luxury trailers
- generic cargo boxes
- magical portable houses
- clean sci-fi pods
- decorative train cars
- visually weightless

## Fuel Bladders

**Canon, with unresolved fuel nomenclature.**

### Canon Anchors

- Follow tractors usually pull a fuel bladder behind the Mod.
- Fuel bladders are black.
- Traverse tractors run on a Meridian Prime equivalent of AN8.
- The exact official fuel name remains unresolved in `docs/traverse-system.md`.

### Visual Direction

Fuel bladders should look:

- vulnerable
- essential
- heavy
- black
- logistically terrifying if damaged
- patched, numbered, and inspected
- practical rather than stylish

The fuel bladder should make distance from help feel real. It is not glamorous, but it is one of the reasons the Traverse survives.

### Naming Guardrail

Do not finalize the official Meridian Prime fuel name or crew slang unless it has been specifically locked in a source-of-truth file.

### Avoid

Do not make fuel bladders:

- decorative
- brightly colored unless a future canon file says so
- hard sci-fi energy cells
- magic resource pods
- visually unimportant

## Recovery Sled / Magic Carpet / Tool

**Canon.**

### Canon Anchors

Official term: **Recovery Sled**.

Crew term: **Magic Carpet**.

The Magic Carpet is:

- a large black high molecular weight plastic sheet
- approximately 100 feet long and 40 feet wide
- used to tow damaged or immobilized equipment
- attached to a Tool

The Tool is:

- the tow bar only
- equipped with skis, D-rings, and tow hardware
- separate from the Magic Carpet itself

Color canon:

- Tools are red.
- Fuel bladders are black.
- Magic Carpets are black.

### Visual Direction

The Magic Carpet should look deceptively simple but essential. It is practical field recovery material, not magic.

The Tool should be clearly separate from the Magic Carpet. In visual art, make sure the red Tool reads as the tow-bar assembly and the black sheet reads as the Recovery Sled / Magic Carpet.

### Avoid

Do not make the Magic Carpet:

- a hover device
- a literal flying carpet
- magical technology
- a rigid sled by default
- a sci-fi anti-gravity recovery system

Do not make the Tool:

- the whole sled
- black by default
- visually indistinguishable from the sheet

## Track Bikes

**Canon.**

### Canon Anchors

Track Bike is both official and crew term.

Track Bikes are:

- standard Traverse Ops equipment
- carried two per Traverse team for redundancy
- electric
- numbered
- equipped with deployable solar panel arrays
- unable to travel while the panel array is deployed
- maintained by the team mechanic
- stored on the Magic Carpet Tool tongue

Common use cases:

- flag replacement
- checking missing markers
- short local route inspection
- return-trip Path maintenance
- moving around safe zones or camped Traverse layouts

### Visual Direction

Track Bikes should look:

- practical
- local
- exposed
- field-serviceable
- compact enough for support duty
- tough enough for route maintenance
- more like Traverse tools than heroic vehicles

The deployable solar panel array should read as useful but limiting. A Track Bike cannot travel while the panel array is deployed.

### Avoid

Do not make Track Bikes:

- sleek cyberbikes
- combat motorcycles
- hover bikes
- stunt vehicles
- glamorous hero rides
- too clean to live on a Traverse

## Trail Flags and Path Markers

**Canon.**

### Canon Anchors

- Most Trail flags use Meridian Prime bamboo poles with cloth flags.
- Trail flags are placed roughly every mile depending on terrain, weather, urgency, and who is on the Track Bike that day.
- The Path is the proven black-flagged passage through a crevasse field.
- The Path is serious, quiet, somber, and procedural.

### Visual Direction

Trail flags should be humble but emotionally powerful.

They should feel like small objects holding back large danger. Their power comes from procedure, repetition, weather exposure, and crew trust.

Black Path flags should immediately make the visual mood quieter.

### Avoid

Do not make flags:

- decorative fantasy banners
- military parade standards
- colorful festival markers by default
- too large or ornate
- visually divorced from route procedure

## Book One Vehicle Reveal Discipline

Book One is currently structured as a novella. Vehicle art should support restraint.

Use this rule:

**Reveal enough to make the next route necessary, not enough to solve the whole world.**

### Early Book One Vehicle Art May Show

- NCI staging yard
- Red Umbrielor
- cold-start checks
- MD-8 system complaints
- turkey roaster / defrost workaround
- under-seat pistol compartment without explaining it
- Rob's empty passenger seat
- MITE II Mod train
- Mods and black fuel bladders
- Track Bikes as support gear
- route flags
- field lights, cold exhaust, frost, and route grime
- pressure ridges / growth rollers as terrain danger

### Early Book One Vehicle Art Should Not Reveal

- Core mechanics
- full hidden formation function
- exact footprint origin
- unrevealed Aeonos / Àæonos mechanics
- full Horus plan
- assassin identity
- full meaning of Grandfather Ten's pistol
- complete meaning of The House Ten Built
- full Rob death-chain

### Reveal-Safe Vehicle Mood by Early Chapter

- Chapter 1: cold steel, Red Umbrielor, NCI yard, defrost issue, turkey roaster, under-seat compartment, Rob's empty seat, job-packet wrongness.
- Chapter 2: Red Umbrielor and MITE II Mod train in motion, crew pressure gauges, legal mission surface, first ghost-handshake unease.
- Chapter 3: route label mismatch, equipment loadout mismatch, corridor unease.
- Chapter 4: quiet comms, restricted information, dead channel that is not dead enough.
- Chapter 5: terrain wrongness, pressure ridges, growth rollers, vehicle survival under route danger.
- Chapter 6 and beyond: hidden formation and footprints may enter, but vehicle art should still avoid solving origin, function, or full cosmology.

## Vehicle Image Prompt Rules

When drafting vehicle prompts:

1. State whether the image is Canon, Proposed Canon, Visual Development Concept, or Non-Canon Promo / Adaptation Art.
2. Preserve locked vehicle names, numbers, and functions.
3. Keep Red Umbrielor's number 289 visible in canon-facing designs.
4. Do not rename Red Umbrielor as a defect-name tractor.
5. Keep MITE vehicles older, patched, and field-modified.
6. Keep SpITE vehicles cleaner and higher-priority but still dangerous.
7. Preserve Traverse Ops as moving industrial survival systems.
8. Do not make Traverse vehicles ordinary convoys, generic tanks, luxury RVs, sci-fi hovercraft, or modern Antarctic vehicles with renamed labels.
9. Preserve Book One reveal discipline.
10. Do not add unauthorized weapons, logos, factions, or technology.
11. Keep support gear practical and field-serviceable.
12. If a visual detail is not canon, mark it Proposed Canon or Visual Development Concept.
13. Do not finalize the official fuel name unless a source file locks it.
14. Do not make every vehicle equally dramatic. Let function shape silhouette.
15. Keep the world cold, industrial, procedural, lived-in, and pressure-haunted.

## Working Rule

A Traverse vehicle should look like a survival argument made of steel, fuel, weather, field memory, and maintenance.

Red Umbrielor should make the viewer feel that Tenet did not simply climb into a machine. He climbed into history that still starts, complains, and moves.
