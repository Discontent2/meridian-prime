# Meridian Prime Technology

> Fictional worldbuilding reference for the Meridian Prime universe.

This document tracks the technological systems, measurement conventions, infrastructure logic, and aesthetic contradictions of Meridian Prime, The Lodestar, Aeonos, and àæonos.

---

## Aeonolacertian Metrology and Boiler-Time

Aeonolacertian technology appears primitive and steampunk-like to humans, but its measurements reveal a mature and internally consistent civilization. Their engineering is organized around pressure, heat, rhythm, structural scale, and biological survival.

Their civilization does not begin with abstract distance or solar time. It begins with the pipe, the beam, the vent, and the boiler.

Three core principles shape Aeonolacertian metrology:

**Pressure defines size.**

**Heat defines distance.**

**Rhythm defines time.**

---

### The Bore

**Canon Status:** PROVISIONAL CANON

**Aeonolacertian Concept:** baseline pneumatic-mechanical pipe standard.

**Human Approximation:** variable by city or era, but treated as the master engineering unit.

The **Bore** is the standard inner diameter of a baseline pneumatic-mechanical pipe. Everything mechanical can be expressed as a fraction or multiple of the Bore.

Bolts, rivets, pressure couplings, steam-gun calibers, armor sockets, valve mouths, vent barrels, corridor conduits, and artillery fittings all derive from the Bore.

This makes Aeonolacertian machinery highly modular despite its ritualized appearance. What humans misread as ornament is often measurement, calibration, or compatibility code.

**Story Uses:** a Meridian mechanic realizes an ancient Aeonolacertian weapon and a city boiler share the same measurement grammar; a Steam-Oracle identifies a forged artifact because its rivets are off-Bore; a World Works engineer mistakes a ceremonial pipe for decorative plumbing.

**Open Questions:** Is there one ancient imperial Bore, or do different surviving enclaves preserve incompatible Bore traditions?

### The Reach / Xal

**Canon Status:** PROVISIONAL CANON

**Aeonolacertian Term:** **Xal**

**Human Approximation:** roughly 3 meters / 10 feet.

The **Reach**, or **Xal**, is the distance of one standard structural iron support beam used to build Aeonolacertian city corridors.

The Reach governs corridor scale, bridge spacing, support ribs, gate width, defensive choke points, and armored movement. Aeonolacertian cities are built for large bodies, brass-plated armor, heat conduits, pressure weapons, and communal huddling.

A corridor may be described as two Xal wide, three Xal high, or eight Xal between pressure gates.

**Story Uses:** gives artists, writers, and game designers a consistent visual scale; allows characters to read city function from beam spacing; creates tactical meaning for corridor width during sieges.

**Open Questions:** Are elite or sacred corridors built in perfect Xal ratios, while damaged post-catastrophe corridors show improvised human-like repairs?

### The Vent-Distance / Tlax-Xal

**Canon Status:** PROVISIONAL CANON

**Aeonolacertian Term:** **Tlax-Xal**

**Human Approximation:** roughly 500 meters.

The **Vent-Distance**, or **Tlax-Xal**, is the distance a standard steam vent can push usable heat before condensation cools back into water.

Aeonolacertian city outposts, thermal waystations, defensive nodes, and military corridors must be placed one Vent-Distance apart to maintain viable heat movement. This allows soldiers, workers, and travelers to move through sealed or hostile environments without losing core body temperature.

To Aeonolacertians, logistics are thermal before they are geographic. Distance is not only how far one can walk. It is how long heat can remain alive.

Destroying or disabling a Vent-Distance outpost can break a thermal corridor, isolating troops or forcing retreat.

**Military Phrase:** **Breaking the Corridor**

**Meaning:** severing a chain of thermal outposts so Aeonolacertian forces cannot safely advance, hold position, or retreat.

**Story Uses:** siege strategy, evacuation drama, battlefield horror, survival puzzles, visible city planning, misunderstood native infrastructure.

**Open Questions:** Did World Works map Vent-Distance corridors without understanding their biological necessity?

### The Boiler Cycle

**Canon Status:** PROVISIONAL CANON

The **Boiler Cycle** is the Aeonolacertian system of time measurement.

Many Aeonolacertian metropolises are sealed, subterranean, or protected from outside threats. As a result, time is not primarily measured by the sun. Time is dictated by the city’s central boiler, pressure regulators, and condensation cycles.

To live inside an Aeonolacertian city is to live inside a clockwork body. The boiler does not merely power the city. It tells the city when to work, vent, gather, fight, rest, and remember.

### The Stroke / Tzk’

**Canon Status:** PROVISIONAL CANON

**Aeonolacertian Term:** **Tzk’**

**Human Approximation:** roughly 1.5 human seconds.

The **Stroke**, or **Tzk’**, is the smallest common unit of time. It equals the rhythmic mechanical tick of a city’s central clockwork regulator.

The Stroke is felt as much as counted. In sealed cities, citizens may sense the regulator through floor vibration, pipe rhythm, armor resonance, or boiler pulse.

**Story Uses:** battle timing, ritual chanting, machine calibration, suspense scenes where a character counts strokes before a vent or gate opens.

**Open Questions:** Do damaged cities develop irregular Strokes, causing cultural and biological stress?

---

### The Vent / Xis-tlatl

**Canon Status:** PROVISIONAL CANON

**Aeonolacertian Term:** **Xis-tlatl**

**Human Approximation:** if calculated as 4,000 base-8 Strokes, approximately 51.2 human minutes.

The **Vent**, or **Xis-tlatl**, occurs every 4,000 Strokes in base-8 counting. At each Vent, the metropolis automatically purges excess condensation from the main lines, creating a massive city-wide echo of rushing steam.

This serves as an Aeonolacertian equivalent of an hour, though it is not solar. It is civic, mechanical, thermal, and biological.

The Vent is not quiet. It is a public breath.

**Story Uses:** city-wide scene transitions, alarms, rituals, shift changes, synchronized attacks, moments when whispered secrets are hidden under the roar of steam.

**Open Questions:** Are some forbidden meetings timed to occur during the Vent because the steam roar defeats surveillance?

---

### The Shift / Na-Tzk’

**Canon Status:** PROVISIONAL CANON

**Aeonolacertian Term:** **Na-Tzk’**

**Human Approximation:** 8 Vents, approximately 6 hours, 49 minutes, and 36 seconds if using the base-8 Vent calculation.

The **Shift**, or **Na-Tzk’**, consists of 8 Vents.

It is the standard biological rotation for saurian workers and soldiers before they must return to a high-heat chamber to recharge core body temperature.

A Shift is not simply a work period. It is the duration of safe exertion before thermal renewal becomes necessary.

**Story Uses:** worker schedules, military deployments, timed infiltration, exhaustion, heat-starvation danger, return-to-boiler rituals.

**Open Questions:** What happens to soldiers forced beyond one Shift without access to heat?

### Military and Civic Uses

Aeonolacertian metrology links city design, military doctrine, labor systems, and ritual life.

#### City Design

- Corridors are measured by the Reach / Xal.
- Pipes, valves, rivets, and weapon fittings derive from the Bore.
- Outposts and waystations are spaced by Vent-Distance / Tlax-Xal.
- Sealed cities run on the Boiler Cycle instead of sun-time.

#### Military Doctrine

Aeonolacertian commanders use clicks, hisses, and glottal stops like battlefield code, cutting through artillery, steam roar, armor impact, and breach chaos.

Examples:

**Q’at-atli na-q’at!**

**Translation:** Protect the Central Boiler at all costs.

**Literal Meaning:** Blood for the Mother-Heart.

**Context:** A desperate rally cry when the inner sanctum of a city is breached.

---

**Xisssss-tlatl q’at-xis!**

**Translation:** Vent the steam-guns. / Fire.

**Literal Meaning:** Hiss-release the pressure-weapons.

**Context:** An artillery command to unleash a volley of pressurized fire or steam.

---

**K’atli-tlax tzk’!**

**Translation:** Hold the line and maintain body heat.

**Literal Meaning:** Lock scales for warmth.

**Context:** An order given to heavy infantry in steam-circulating brass armor as they brace against an incoming wave.

#### Siege Soundscape

Aeonolacertian cities do not use human-style sirens. Alarms may be sounded by massive tuned steam whistles venting in rhythmic **Xis-tl-Xis-tl** patterns.

At trench lines or breach points, saurian soldiers do not merely stand together. They click brass-plated armor together in **tl-tl-tl** patterns to form conductive heat grids, sharing boiler-warmth while whispering tactical plans in deep throat rumbles.

#### Cultural Logic

A city is a body.

A boiler is a heart.

A corridor is a vein.

A foundry is a memory organ.

A soldier is kept warm at range.

Time is pressure in motion.
