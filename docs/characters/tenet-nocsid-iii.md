# Tenet Nocsid III

> Character canon for the primary protagonist of the Traverse / hidden-route story.

**Canon Status:** CONFIRMED CANON, EXPANDABLE

## Canon Name

Full canon name:

**Tenet Nocsid III**

Common narration may call him:

- Tenet
- Tenet Nocsid

Do not use “Tenet III” as his full legal or formal name unless context requires shorthand. His full canon name is **Tenet Nocsid III**.

---

## Core Character Role

Tenet Nocsid III is the primary protagonist of the Traverse / hidden-route story.

Tenet is not a rookie, tourist, or sheltered observer. He is an experienced Traverse operator with real field credibility.

Known Traverse experience includes:

- SpITE I
- SpITE II
- SpITE III
- MITE I
- WWISSaRD work

By the time of the MITE II story, Tenet has already worked prestige routes, support routes, science-support routes, and extension-route culture. He understands Traverse Ops procedure, danger, slang, machinery, and the quiet rules of being **On Traverse**.

Canon character engine:

**Tenet wants an honest day’s pay for an honest day’s work, but the world keeps making honest work impossible.**

This is his grounded protagonist engine. Tenet does not begin as a revolutionary, prophet, conspiracy hunter, or chosen one. He becomes dangerous because competence, grief, and unfinished family history place him where buried truth becomes unavoidable.

---

## Current Story Placement

Tenet’s current main-story placement is:

**MITE II**

Tenet is an **NCI MITE II HEO-IC / field lead by contract designation**, not an official Prime Ops Lead.

He is recognized in the field as a Traverse operator and HEO-IC by designation, but he is not on Prime Ops payroll.

He works for:

**NCI**

NCI stands for:

**Nocsid Contractors Inc.**

Internal or informal names for NCI:

- **The Family**
- **The Company**

MITE II is an NCI-run mission using NCI crew and equipment.

MITE II allows private contractors, making it more flexible, deniable, and concealable than SpITE or WWISSaRD operations.

NCI leased proposed route access for surveying and proofing. Horus Skelton approved the lease in his capacity as **World Works Director of Traverse Operations**.

MITE II’s public-facing mission is private survey / route-proofing work. Its deeper function is tied to the hidden formation near the Claw and the search for a concealable route connected to Horus Skelton’s interests.

---

## NCI Relationship

Nocsid Contractors Inc. is part of Tenet’s inheritance-shadow, not merely a random employer.

NCI was founded by:

- **Tenet Nocsid I**, nicknamed **Ten**
- **Rob Holliday**

NCI modifies older Traverse tractors and Mods for “Special” Missions.

NCI is a family-rooted private Traverse contractor with old machines, field credibility, deep Traverse Ops knowledge, private loyalties, and records Prime Ops never fully controlled.

NCI should feel capable, deniable, practical, and family-haunted.

---

## Private Contractor Naming and Numbering

Private contractors working under approved Traverse leases can choose their own internal naming and numbering conventions for tractors, Mods, tools, and mission equipment, as long as Prime Ops lease paperwork and safety requirements can still identify assets when required.

This allows NCI to preserve older machine names, legacy names, private numbering customs, and “Special” Mission designations that do not perfectly match standard Prime Ops conventions.

**MMS Quadtrack 289, “Red Umbrielor”**

Short forms:

- Red Umbrielor
- 289
- Quadtrack 289

Red Umbrielor is Tenet’s major Traverse machine and part of his visual identity.

Canon traits:

- massive MMS Quadtrack
- number 289
- front blade
- tracked habitation module
- scarred by years of work
- proven through storms, contracts, breakdowns, and bad decisions

## Personality

Tenet is:

- competent
- practical
- field-proven
- socially capable
- respected by crews
- comfortable with danger
- impatient with empty bureaucracy
- capable of humor under pressure
- quietly grief-marked after Rob’s death
- drawn toward unknown routes
- suspicious of routine when routine becomes soul-deadening
- grounded in work ethic rather than ideology

Tenet fears mundane stagnation more than danger.

He does not hate ordinary work. He hates work that becomes dishonest, hollow, or trapped inside systems that bury truth.

## The Last Traverse

**The Last Traverse** happened before the current MITE II story.

The Last Traverse is the final journey Tenet takes with Rob Holliday to the remote site known as:

**The House Ten Built**

During The Last Traverse:

- Rob contacts Tenet through a priority transmission.
- Rob asks Tenet for a ride to The House Ten Built.
- Rob brings only a single duffel bag.
- Rob rides in the passenger seat instead of the hab module.
- Tenet drives Red Umbrielor.
- They revisit old landmarks, routes, stories, and memories.
- Rob shows signs of knowing his time is nearly over.
- Tenet does not fully understand the weight of the trip until later.
- The journey becomes Rob’s farewell to the life he helped build.

Rob Holliday is dead after The Last Traverse.

True cause of death:

**Horus Skelton had Rob Holliday poisoned through an assassin covertly working for Horus while employed at NCI.**

The public-facing cause of Rob’s death may remain obscured, softened, or misreported inside the story.

For expanded legacy canon, see `docs/legacy/the-last-traverse.md`.

## Tenet I / Rob / Horus Photograph

A key photograph at The House Ten Built shows three young men standing beside a military MMS Quadtrack:

- Tenet Nocsid I / Grandfather Ten
- Rob Holliday
- Horus Skelton

All three are smiling.

Canon meaning:

This photograph proves that Tenet I, Rob, and Horus Skelton were once friends before the rupture.

This should become a major emotional and conspiracy clue.

Do not treat Horus as a random corporate antagonist. Horus was once part of the old Traverse brotherhood around Tenet I and Rob.

---

## Horus Skelton Integration

**Horus = Horus Skelton**

Horus Skelton is Mark Skelton’s father.

Horus has been the **World Works Director of Traverse Operations** for decades.

Horus was once close to Tenet I and Rob Holliday.

Later, Horus becomes associated with corporate control, hidden agendas, and the buried truth connected to the Core, the Claw, and the possible route to Aeonos / Àæonos.

Story function:

Horus is not merely a villain from outside Tenet’s world. He is part of Tenet’s inherited history. His betrayal, compromise, or obsession should feel personal because he was once one of the men in the photograph.

Horus covertly had Rob Holliday poisoned through an assassin working inside NCI.

## Grandfather Ten’s Pistol

Rob gives Tenet Nocsid III an old pistol that belonged to Tenet Nocsid I.

This happens during The Last Traverse at The House Ten Built.

Tenet still carries this pistol during the MITE II story.

The pistol is not merely a weapon. It is an inheritance object.

Canon meaning:

- Tenet I’s unfinished burden
- Rob’s final act of trust
- a symbol that Tenet still has time
- a physical link between Tenet, Tenet I, Rob, and the old rupture
- a responsibility Tenet does not fully understand when he receives it

Rob gives it to Tenet because:

**Tenet still has time.**

This line should be treated as thematically important.

## Chrono-Loom

The Chrono-Loom is part of Tenet’s world and The Last Traverse.

The Chrono-Loom appears at dusk.

It shows or suggests:

- ancient mountains where none exist now
- forgotten coastlines
- shadows of erased cities
- old horizons
- memories of Meridian Prime before current maps and records

Canon framing:

**For a few brief moments every evening, Meridian Prime remembers things people have forgotten.**

The Chrono-Loom should be treated as a poetic, visual, and possibly scientific phenomenon connected to time crystals, memory, old geography, and buried history.

Open future question:

Does the Chrono-Loom merely show atmospheric / crystal refraction, or can it reveal old routes, hidden structures, erased settlements, the Core, or traces of the route to Aeonos / Àæonos?

## Integrated Story Spine

Before the MITE II conspiracy, Tenet Nocsid III took Rob Holliday on The Last Traverse to The House Ten Built.

There, Tenet saw evidence that Tenet I, Rob Holliday, and Horus Skelton were once friends before an old rupture changed everything.

Rob gave Tenet Tenet I’s pistol and left him with the burden of time, choice, and unfinished family history.

Rob died after The Last Traverse, poisoned on Horus Skelton’s orders through an assassin embedded in NCI.

Later, Tenet joins NCI’s MITE II private survey mission, thinking he is taking honest contractor work.

The hidden formation near the Claw does not begin Tenet’s story.

It reopens it.

## Artist and Adaptation Notes

- Tenet should look like a seasoned Traverse operator, not a polished corporate hero.
- Tenet’s design should imply field competence, weather exposure, practical clothing, and machine familiarity.
- Red Umbrielor should feel like a character-adjacent machine: scarred, old, trusted, and storied.
- The House Ten Built should look modest, remote, weathered, stubborn, and emotionally heavy.
- Rob Holliday should preserve his signature visual markers: salmon polo, white side-combed hair, square glasses, mustache-goatee, curled eyebrow tips, warm smile.
- Grandfather Ten’s pistol should be treated as a meaningful inherited object.
- The Chrono-Loom should be beautiful, eerie, memory-rich, and tied to the sense that Meridian Prime remembers what people erase.
- The photo of Tenet I, Rob, and Horus should be staged as a major visual clue.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Do not make Tenet Nocsid III a rookie.
- Do not make Tenet a generic chosen one.
- Do not detach Tenet from NCI family legacy.
- Do not make Red Umbrielor an ordinary unnamed tractor.
- Do not treat Rob Holliday as comic relief only.
- Do not make The Last Traverse irrelevant to the main plot.
- Do not make Horus Skelton a random corporate villain with no personal history.
- Do not reveal the secrets of The House Ten Built until story timing requires it.
- Do not treat Grandfather Ten’s pistol as merely a weapon.
- Do not make the Chrono-Loom only decorative if later story uses can connect it to memory, history, routes, or hidden truth.
- Do not confuse Aeonos and Àæonos.
- Do not portray Tenet as an official Prime Ops Lead during MITE II. He is NCI MITE II HEO-IC / field lead by contract designation.
