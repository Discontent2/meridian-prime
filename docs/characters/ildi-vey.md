# Ildi Vey

> Character canon for Ildi Vey, MITE II signal-truth crew anchor and restricted Conjugate Chimera character.

**Canon Status:** MAIN CANON, RESTRICTED / EXPANDABLE

## Core Character Role

**Ildi Vey** is one of the first three clearly introduced MITE II crew anchors.

Role:

**Module-attached mech / infrastructure monitor / old-route systems reader**

People / species:

Antisapien, likely Lividisapien or related underclass signal-band. Exact subtype remains expandable unless later canon specifies further.

Culture vibe:

**punk / exposed-wire signal truth**

Age vibe:

Late 20s to mid 30s.

Core vibe:

**wiry, suspicious, brilliant, hears systems as arguments**

---

## Three Truths Structure

Ildi represents **signal truth** among the first three MITE II crew anchors:

- **Rudy Calder** = machine truth
- **Kavo “Forest” Rill** = terrain truth
- **Ildi Vey** = signal truth

If Ildi is worried, the infrastructure is wrong.

She should notice comms that are too quiet, dead channels that are not dead enough, corrupted terminals, route infrastructure that should not exist, and old signal behavior before ordinary crew members understand the danger.

---

## Work Function

Ildi attaches to or rides with the module during Traverse to monitor:

- coupling strain
- comms
- heat
- dead channels
- corrupted terminals
- route infrastructure
- old signal behavior
- module health
- buried or half-awake systems

She is technical rather than mystical.

Her weirdness should be grounded in Antisapien / underclass exposed-wire logic, field experience, signal sensitivity, and infrastructure literacy.

Possible first impression line:

**“Your clean packet is bleeding old signal.”**

---

## Restricted Character Canon: Conjugate Chimera

Ildi Vey is canonized as a **Conjugate Chimera** under restricted / hidden character canon.

This is not public-facing early Book One exposition.

Reader-facing first impression:

Ildi is a brilliant, suspicious, module-attached systems reader who represents signal truth.

Hidden / restricted truth:

Ildi is a rare bi-phase survivor whose matter-compatible body contains sealed conjugate microstructures, containment tissues, or boundary-state systems that make her unusually sensitive to old signal behavior, infrastructure lies, ghost handshakes, and contact-frame-adjacent anomalies.

Core rule:

**Ildi survives because her opposing systems never truly touch.**

Her biology may explain why she hears systems as arguments, but it does not replace her technical skill.

Her competence comes from training, anger, experience, underclass signal logic, and being very hard to fool.

Cross-reference:

- `docs/cosmology/conjugate-chimeras.md`
- `docs/cosmology/conjugate-contact-frames.md`

---

## Reveal Discipline

Do not reveal Ildi’s Conjugate Chimera nature too early unless the novella structure is deliberately revised.

Recommended reveal ladder:

1. **Early Book One:** Ildi detects the ghost handshake before anyone else. The reader reads this as technical brilliance.
2. **Mid / later story:** Ildi’s body reacts before her instruments fully explain why.
3. **Later series truth:** Ildi is not merely reading signal. She is partly a living boundary sensor.

Important guardrails:

- Do not make Ildi the answer to the Core.
- Do not make Ildi the source of the ghost handshake.
- Do not make Ildi a universal detector.
- Do not make her biology clean, controlled, or cost-free.
- Do not erase her Antisapien / underclass signal-band identity.
- Do not make every Antisapien a Conjugate Chimera by association.
- Do not make her a monster-of-the-week reveal.

Canon tone:

**Ildi’s signal truth has teeth under the skin.**

## Symptoms and Story Texture

Possible Ildi-specific symptoms or textures:

- signal migraines
- containment fevers
- cold spots along scar-lines
- pain when dead channels are not dead enough
- field seizures near unstable old infrastructure
- panic around Contact Frame breach alarms
- medical files marked do-not-scan or restricted
- instinctive distrust of clean packets and official silence
- physical discomfort when the route is lying through infrastructure

Use these sparingly.

They should deepen Ildi, not turn every scene into symptom exposition.

## Personality Notes

Ildi is:

- sarcastic
- vigilant
- hard to impress
- technically brilliant
- suspicious of clean systems
- more trusting of cables than people
- more trusting of old signals than official reports
- difficult to frighten in ordinary ways
- deeply aware that being known can mean being owned

Canon line:

**Being known can mean being owned.**

This line should inform Ildi’s secrecy, defensiveness, and relationship to authority.

Ildi’s character functions as:

- signal truth
- technical suspicion
- infrastructure conscience
- underclass systems literacy
- buried evidence detector
- living boundary horror under controlled reveal

She should not function as:

- a magic scanner
- a convenient clue machine
- a secret chosen one

## Relationship to MITE II

At the start of the MITE II story, Ildi should be treated by the crew as a systems specialist, not as a known Conjugate Chimera unless later canon decides that someone aboard already knows.

Her Chapter 2 locked field clue:

**Ildi detects an old signal / ghost handshake where the route packet says there is no active or legacy infrastructure.**

Reader-facing meaning:

**The paper lied first. Then the old systems answered.**

Ildi should introduce the first field-level wrongness after Horus’s approval stamp.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Do not reveal Ildi’s restricted biology before establishing her technical competence.
- Do not make Ildi’s biology more important than her personhood.
- Do not make Ildi a universal detector.
- Do not use Ildi to solve the Core early.
- Do not make her the source of the ghost handshake.
- Do not make her powers free or perfectly controlled.
- Do not turn her into a chosen one.
- Do not overwrite Antisapien canon through her.
- Do not make her trust World Works or WWISSaRD easily without cause.
- Do not make her condition public knowledge without deliberate story reason.
