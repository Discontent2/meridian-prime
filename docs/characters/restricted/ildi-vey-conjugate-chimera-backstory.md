## Restricted Thesis

Ildi Vey is not merely a survivor of an experiment.

She is a survivor of a **body-sized containment crime** who became a systems reader because systems were the first monsters that learned her name.

She learned to read infrastructure because infrastructure once read her.

She learned to distrust clean packets because the cleanest records were the ones that hurt people most efficiently.

She learned that official silence has texture.

She learned that a sealed boundary can be medical, legal, spatial, emotional, and alive.

Best current thesis:

**Ildi was not born as an answer. She was made into a question no institution could safely file, then became brilliant enough to interrogate the filing system back.**

---

## Central Backstory Principle

Ildi survived a false-voluntary **Bi-Phase Integration** event.

The paperwork created consent.

The truth did not.

Core thematic line:

**Consent without truth is only paperwork.**

The event did not make Ildi a stable hybrid.

It made her a maintained boundary.

Her survival depends on opposing systems inside her never truly touching.

Core Conjugate Chimera rule:

**Ildi survives because her opposing systems never truly touch.**

Development line:

**She was not stable. She was correctly unstable.**

The horror is not that she is half-one thing and half-another.

The horror is that she is one person forced to remain a border.

## Canonized Restricted Backstory Spine

The current restricted canon model:

1. Ildi was Antisapien first, likely Lividisapien or related underclass signal-band unless later canon specifies further.
2. In her twenties, she lived close to fringe infrastructure, signal labor, underclass records, debt pressure, and predatory contracts.
3. A close friend betrayed her by selling, exposing, or handing over her profile, vulnerabilities, signal history, and trust.
4. The friend may have been desperate, compromised, or morally cornered, but they knew enough to be guilty.
5. Ildi entered what appeared to be a voluntary paid experiment or diagnostic contract.
6. The consent was real only on paper because the truth of the experiment was withheld.
7. The experiment exposed her to Bi-Phase Integration conditions and transformed her into a viable Conjugate Chimera.
8. Her matter-compatible body now contains sealed conjugate microstructures, containment tissues, boundary-state systems, or translated anti-phase biological / signal structures.
9. The internal boundary did not solve the contradiction. It kept the contradiction from becoming annihilation.
10. Institutions attempted to classify, contain, study, own, or euphemize her survival.
11. Ildi escaped complete institutional capture by becoming too technically necessary, too hard to classify, and too dangerous to lie to.

Do not overstate this as a complete solved timeline.

The exact facility, full culprit chain, dates, and all witnesses remain expandable.

## Ildi's Body as Record

After the Bi-Phase Integration event, Ildi developed involuntary autobiographical memory responses.

These are not ordinary dreams and not chosen flashbacks.

Development term:

**autobiographical memory mind-pops**

A mind-pop can seize the present with dreamlike force.

Possible effects:

- the current room overlays with a remembered place
- present voices become past voices
- a scanner tone becomes laughter from an old job
- a scar responds before Ildi consciously knows what memory opened
- systems react to her memory-state as if it were signal input
- she may lose seconds, misread a face, or perceive a hidden system route through memory imagery

Core line:

**A mind-pop is not only a memory. It is a memory trying to become environment.**

## Scar Biofluorescence

Ildi's scars are memory-reactive boundary sites.

During involuntary autobiographical memory mind-pops, some scars may biofluoresce according to:

- memory type
- scar location
- emotional intensity
- nearby signal pressure
- whether the memory acts as warning, wound, lure, or map
- whether Ildi is resisting, suppressing, reframing, or reading it
- whether the observer is capable of seeing the biofluorescence unaided

Core principle:

**The glow should not be pretty first. It should be revealing first.**

Beauty may appear, but it should feel uneasy because the light means something inside her has been forced open.

Canonized restricted visibility rule:

**Conjugate Chimeras and Aeonolacertians can perceive or interpret Conjugate Chimera scar biofluorescence without the aid of a UV source. All other races require a strong enough UV light source, such as blacklight, or diagnostic / sensor mediation to visualize it.**

Implications:

- Ildi can feel and internally read scar responses even when others see nothing.
- Another Conjugate Chimera could visually recognize the scar-light without blacklight.
- Aeonolacertians can visually recognize the biofluorescence unaided.
- Other races generally need blacklight, diagnostic translation, camera enhancement, or secondhand interpretation.
- Under ordinary lighting, most non-Chimera / non-Aeonolacertian observers may miss the scar-light entirely or see only subtle texture, heat, or skin irregularity.
- Blacklight scenes can become medical aid, violation, interrogation, proof, or tactical advantage.

Forest implication:

**Forest can recognize dangerous scar-state changes directly because he is Aeonolacertian, without forcing Ildi under blacklight or demanding an explanation before helping.**

## The Locked Door

The locked-door event is real enough to shape Ildi's restraint, but its exact form remains unresolved.

Possible forms:

1. **Containment Door:** She sealed a lab, clinic, or module bay while something inside was still alive or partly alive.
2. **Data Door:** She deleted or encrypted a registry that contained the only proof certain subjects existed.
3. **Body Door:** She survived by allowing emergency systems to shut down part of her own boundary response, leaving permanent damage.
4. **Community Door:** She refused to reveal a Mirrorborn route, leaving people outside without access but protecting those inside.
5. **Signal Door:** She ignored a distress call because the signal pattern was bait wearing a dead person's voice.

Current canon stance:

**The locked door is protected unresolved backstory.**

Do not solve it casually.

It should remain the bruise under her restraint: Ildi knows some correct choices never become clean choices.

## Relationship to Forest

Forest should not be the person who explains or heals Ildi's condition.

He should be the person who notices what the room is doing to her before the room admits it is doing anything.

Professional-first trust markers:

- He sees a physical reaction and asks what needs doing, not what happened to her.
- He notices when a scanner is making her more dangerous by naming her incorrectly.
- He covers her body language with tactical movement, not sympathy theater.
- He treats her pain as operationally real without turning her into a fragile object.
- He refuses to let others translate her silence into consent.

Possible first restricted trust beat:

Ildi has a boundary fever during an old-signal event. Forest realizes the route is not merely hurting her; it is using her as a reply path. He does not say, “Are you okay?” He says, “Tell me which line to cut.” She gives him three words. He obeys. That is the beginning.

Do not make Forest her therapist, handler, owner, or savior.
