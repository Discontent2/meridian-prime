# Seasonal Map Logic

> Canon foundation for Meridian Prime maps, route charts, hazard layers, ecological cartography, contraband routes, and seasonal truth problems.

**Canon Status:** MAIN CANON, EXPANDABLE

## Purpose

This file promotes curated cartographic logic from World Building Department sandbox development into main canon.

It does not canonize every proposed map layer, route, market, organism, or location.

It canonizes the principle that maps on Meridian Prime are political, seasonal, ecological, and often incomplete.

Core principle:

**A map on Meridian Prime is not a picture of land. It is an argument about what can still be survived.**

---

## Central Map Rule

Meridian Prime maps should not be treated as static geographic charts.

They should show:

- seasonal route truth
- ecology that changes travel
- food and water access
- flood, freeze, haboon, and drawdown states
- legal boundaries
- false surfaces
- hidden water
- corporate omissions
- sacred no-map zones
- black-market corridors
- suppressed records
- Hydropolis civic verification
- Craton guest-law restrictions
- World Works liability shadows
- Traverse route hazards

Canon line:

**The official map is where authority says the road should be. The route map is where survival says the road actually is.**

## Megafauna Map Logic

A megafauna map mark should answer:

1. **Where does the animal make travel dangerous?**
2. **How does the danger change by season?**
3. **What does the animal reveal that ordinary terrain maps miss?**

Canon line:

**Some hazards move because they are alive. Some animals matter because they move the hazard.**

Possible symbol families:

- herd corridor
- predation zone
- false terrain mark
- dormancy field
- nesting / nursery exclusion
- wet migration path
- sacred fauna corridor
- protected civic fauna zone
- pirate-weaponized fauna zone

---

## Mega-Flora Map Logic

A mega-flora map mark should answer:

1. **How does this flora alter movement or visibility?**
2. **How does that alteration change by season?**
3. **Who treats the flora as hazard, resource, refuge, or sacred boundary?**

Canon line:

**Plants do not merely occupy the map. They edit it.**

Possible symbol families:

- landmark flora
- false ground / hidden water
- barrier growth
- protected / sacred flora
- resource / extraction flora
- signal flora
- flood expansion flora
- dormancy field
- concealment flora

## Mega-Fungi Map Logic

A mega-fungi map mark should answer:

1. **What dead, broken, drowned, contaminated, or forgotten thing is feeding it?**
2. **How does its bloom change route safety, signal clarity, or structural reliability?**
3. **Who treats it as contamination, food, warning, witness, medicine, trap, or sacred response?**

Canon line:

**Fungi mark where the map has begun to digest itself.**

Possible symbol families:

- sporefall zone
- rot field
- false surface
- archive / signal contamination
- death landmark
- hidden water indicator
- food / medicine fungi
- sacred fungal ecology
- pirate concealment fungi

## Food Map Logic

A Meridian Prime food map should answer:

1. **What can be eaten here?**
2. **What nearby thing looks edible but kills, sickens, confuses, or compromises identity?**
3. **Who controls the harvest?**
4. **What season makes the map lie?**

Canon line:

**A food map is a survival document with politics hiding in the pantry.**

Possible layer families:

- nutritional harvest zone
- toxic lookalike zone
- medical harvest zone
- recreational / restricted food zone
- ration route
- food sovereignty claim
- corporate harvest block
- civic verified food zone
- under-mapped sacred food zone
- contraband food route

---

## Intoxicant / Medicine / Ritual Harvest Map Logic

A substance map should answer:

1. **What is harvested here?**
2. **Who is allowed to name it safe?**
3. **What happens if it is taken in the wrong season or jurisdiction?**
4. **Who smuggles, blesses, bans, or verifies it?**
5. **What route becomes more dangerous because someone used it?**

Canon line:

**A cup on Meridian Prime is never only a cup. It is a route permit with a pulse.**

Possible layer families:

- verified medicinal harvest zone
- route-readiness stimulant zone
- route-prohibited substance zone
- civic quarantine zone
- guest-law ritual food zone
- fermentation / festival corridor
- contraband current
- false-light substance hazard
- illegal sedation / sabotage marker
- emergency medicine cache

Guardrail:

Maps may show fictional hazards and legal categories, but should not provide real-world drug, poison, dosage, extraction, or trafficking instructions.

## Black Market Map Logic

A black-market route map should answer:

1. **What moves here that official maps deny?**
2. **Who needs it badly enough to risk buying it?**
3. **Who is harmed if it moves?**
4. **Who is harmed if it does not move?**
5. **What official silence made the route profitable?**

Canon line:

**A black market map is where the official map develops a guilty shadow.**

Possible layer families:

- survival contraband route
- suppressed record flow
- illegal cartography layer
- identity violation route
- sacred violation alarm
- civic origin crime
- salvage / wreck economy
- tech blind zone
- counterfeit rescue marker
- corporate shadow corridor

## Seasonal Map Treatment

### Dry Season

Dry-season maps should emphasize:

- exposed roads
- overland Traverse routes
- dry riverbed crossings
- crust collapse hazards
- stored-food routes
- Ghost Fuel trails
- illegal scanner use
- Redline Map exchanges
- dormant ecology that only appears dead
- hidden water indicators
- exposed wrecks
- animals mistaken for terrain

Map note:

**Dry does not mean empty. It means some things are waiting.**

### Cataract Season

Cataract-season maps should emphasize:

- water waking in canyons and descent routes
- early flood growth
- routes becoming food-isolated
- canyon spray crops
- washed-out evidence trails
- emergency repair markets
- animal movement ahead of rising water
- unstable switchbacks
- substances prohibited before descent

Map note:

**When the water wakes, every shortcut becomes an argument.**

### Haboon Season

Haboon-season maps should emphasize:

- low visibility
- false rescue lights
- counterfeit camp beacons
- storm-cover smuggling
- spore-dust interactions
- emergency medicine shortages
- animal panic routes
- signal-mask and blind radio use
- Bridefield chart scams
- lost-kin record movement under cover of chaos

Map note:

**Haboon hides the road, the buyer, and the body count.**

### Wet Season

Wet-season maps should emphasize:

- waterborne travel
- canal cities
- flooded road ghosts
- barge routes
- seasonal ports
- Hydropolis tower / canal navigation
- Free Scab pirate currents
- wet-gate docking
- floating hazards
- submerged predator beds
- preserved civic habitats
- Craton / Blackstep wet access logic

Map note:

**Wet season does not erase the map. It changes who can read it.**

### Drawdown / Wet Retreat

Drawdown maps should emphasize:

- newly exposed routes
- stranded organisms
- mudlocked machinery
- fresh harvest zones
- rotting flood debris
- washed-in contraband
- found bodies
- renewed land claims
- false ground where water recently stood

Map note:

**When the water leaves, it does not give back the same road.**

## Official vs Local Maps

Different authorities produce different truth.

### Prime Ops / World Works Maps

May emphasize:

- legal route status
- approved corridors
- liability-controlled hazards
- corporate harvest blocks
- simplified ecological labels
- suppression of embarrassing failures
- euphemized risk language

### NCI / Traverse Crew Maps

May emphasize:

- vehicle survivability
- local repair knowledge
- practical hazard marks
- crew warnings
- unofficial route corrections
- dead zones
- wrongness noticed by field operators

### Hydropolis Maps

May emphasize:

- civic verification
- signal contamination
- Contact Frame protocols
- wet / dry vertical migration
- archive security
- protected fauna / flora zones
- identity and origin rules

### Craton Maps

May be partial, sacred, restricted, oral, performed, or intentionally unmappable to outsiders.

They may emphasize:

- guest-law thresholds
- pressure corridors
- sacred fauna / flora
- Geodeum routes
- gate authority
- no-map zones
- memory routes

### Black-Market Maps

May emphasize:

- survival gaps
- suppressed records
- false safety
- stolen proofs
- illegal routes
- poaching wounds
- counterfeit rescue
- forbidden evidence

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Do not make maps neutral.
- Do not make maps static.
- Do not make official maps automatically correct.
- Do not make local maps automatically correct.
- Do not turn all cartography into conspiracy. Sometimes maps are wrong because the planet changed overnight.
- Do not confirm protected mysteries through map labels too early.
- Do not make Dry Port rumor maps proof of the Dry Port.
- Do not fully map sacred Craton space without explicit canon approval.
- Do not turn Hydropolis under-city plans into casual loot.
- Do not provide real-world smuggling or controlled-substance instruction through fictional maps.
