# Hydropolis

> Canon documentation for Hydropolis, the primary Antisapien redoubt and seasonal cyberpunk city-state of the Mirror Basin.

**Canon Status:** MAIN CANON, EXPANDABLE

## Core Canon Status

**Hydropolis** is the primary Antisapien redoubt on Meridian Prime.

It is canonized as:

- a major Meridian Prime location
- the central Antisapien / áæbsconditian phenotype redoubt
- a seasonal, semi-submerged, semi-underground cyberpunk city-state
- a wet-season tower / canal / floating-court city
- a dry-season buried vault / server-catacomb / black-glass substrate redoubt
- a city built in the **Mirror Basin**, a hydrocrystal lowland shaped by first contact with Àæonos
- the safest known matter-side port for true Àæonos-origin conjugate-life visitors using Conjugate Contact Frames
- a major multimedia expansion zone
- not a separate ordinary continent
- not part of the Anticlinal Craton
- not owned or managed by humans, World Works, or Prime Ops
- not a direct explanation for Book One unless later canon explicitly develops that connection

Canon line:

**Hydropolis is where Meridian Prime learned to keep the signal alive.**

## Core Concept

Hydropolis is not simply another continent, a conventional underground city, or a fully migratory settlement.

It is a place, a network, a seasonal city, and a redoubt.

During wet seasons, Hydropolis becomes a floating / canal / tower city of neon rails, black-glass barges, signal spires, drowned markets, AR veils, wet streets, and floating courts.

During dry seasons, much of Hydropolis withdraws into exposed crystal flats, underground vaults, rail tunnels, server catacombs, mirror canals, sealed redoubt districts, and black-glass substrate infrastructure.

Canon framing:

**Hydropolis has two bodies: a wet body above the water and a dry body under the crystal.**

## Relationship to Antisapiens

Hydropolis is the major homeland / redoubt of Antisapiens on Meridian Prime.

Antisapiens are Meridian Prime-produced, matter-compatible áæbsconditian phenotype people naturally produced after first contact with Àæonos.

Hydropolis is the major civic, political, cultural, technological, and signal-memory center for that people.

Hydropolis should feel like the planet built a city out of:

- water
- reflection
- black glass
- cable
- signal
- memory
- refusal
- wet light
- buried infrastructure
- conjugate-origin cultural memory

Poetic contrast:

- The Anticlinal Craton says: **We held the gate.**
- Human Meridian settlement says: **We built the settlement.**
- Hydropolis says: **We survived the signal.**

## Relationship to Àæonos and First Contact

Hydropolis is located in the Mirror Basin, a vast hydrocrystal lowland where Meridian Prime first answered or was altered by contact with Àæonos.

This does not yet fully explain the physics, religion, or biology of first contact.

Canon-safe framing:

Hydropolis grew around the place where the Antisapien phenotype, signal-band society, and Meridian Prime's response to Àæonos became historically, biologically, or culturally undeniable.

Do not overdefine yet:

- the exact mechanics of first contact
- whether the Mirror Basin was created, altered, awakened, or revealed by Àæonos contact
- whether Hydropolis was founded before, during, or after the first stable Antisapien population
- whether the city is built around a physical scar, signal wound, translated ruin, or hydrocrystal basin phenomenon

Canon line:

**Antisapiens are what Meridian Prime grew after touching Àæonos and surviving. Hydropolis is where that survival became a city.**

## Geography: The Mirror Basin

The **Mirror Basin** is the larger hydrocrystal lowland region containing Hydropolis.

It should be treated as:

- an inland lowland basin
- a hydrocrystal / aquifer region
- a wet/dry cycle landscape
- a place of water, reflection, rails, black glass, and buried signal infrastructure
- strategically positioned between major trade routes without belonging to humans or Aeonolacertians
- distinct from the Anticlinal Craton and the Endorheic Shard Sea

Possible subregion terms:

- Blackwater Flats
- Signal Delta
- Hydroglass Basin
- Veilwater Districts
- Drowned Circuit underways
- Mirror Canals
- Substrate Rails

These names remain expandable and do not need to be fully mapped yet.

## Wet / Dry Seasonal Structure

Hydropolis changes shape with Meridian Prime's wet/dry cycle.

### Wet Season Hydropolis

During wet seasons, Hydropolis becomes:

- a canal city
- a tower city
- a barge city
- a floating-court city
- a neon rail-water city
- an AR-veiled public maze
- a diplomatic port for Contact Frame visitors
- a signal market network
- a place where black-glass towers rise from floodwater

Wet-season visual read:

**Black-glass towers, magenta rail-light under canals, teal eye-shine reflected in wet streets, fiberoptic bio-cabling braided into public infrastructure, drowned markets, floating courts, and AR veils over water.**

### Dry Season Hydropolis

During dry seasons, Hydropolis withdraws into:

- exposed crystal flats
- underground vaults
- dry mirror canals
- rail tunnels
- server catacombs
- sealed redoubt districts
- black-glass substrate districts
- archive bunkers
- Lividisapien wire markets under broken pylons

Dry-season visual read:

**Elevator shafts descending into mirror vaults, black-glass archives under crystal crust, exposed cable roots, rail bones, sealed districts, and server catacombs humming below dry flats.**

## Contact Frame Port

Hydropolis is the safest known matter-side port for true Àæonos-origin conjugate-life visitors who require Conjugate Contact Frames / Mirror-Shells in matter environments.

This does not mean ordinary Antisapiens require Contact Frames.

The distinction is important:

- **Antisapiens** are Meridian Prime-produced, matter-compatible áæbsconditian phenotype people.
- **True Àæonos-origin conjugate-life visitors** require Contact Frames / Mirror-Shells to visit matter environments.
- **Hydropolis** has infrastructure capable of receiving, maintaining, translating, and protecting Contact Frame visitors.

Hydropolis may contain:

- Mirror-Shell docks
- Contact Frame maintenance sanctums
- null-contact medical rooms
- sealed diplomatic corridors
- radiation / breach alarm streets
- translation courts
- frame-entry chapels
- visitor quarantine theaters
- field-coil inspection halls

Canon line:

**In Hydropolis, Antisapiens can stand beside the impossible relatives and argue over what kinship means.**

## Relationship to Conjugate Chimeras

Hydropolis may have medical, political, legal, and cultural stakes around Conjugate Chimeras / Mirrorborn survivors.

Do not make every Antisapien a Conjugate Chimera.

Do not make Hydropolis a factory for Conjugate Chimeras unless later canon explicitly develops that dark history.

Canon-safe possibilities:

- Hydropolis has some of the best diagnostic knowledge of the difference between ordinary Antisapiens, Contact Frame visitors, and Conjugate Chimeras.
- Some Hydropolis factions protect Mirrorborn survivors.
- Some factions fear them.
- Some factions classify them as evidence of World Works / WWISSaRD violation.
- Lividisapien communities may be more willing to hide or listen to them.
- Vaultsapien archives may hold sealed records of early bi-phase viability cases.

## Relationship to Humans / Meridians

Meridians may visit Hydropolis, but they do not own it.

Meridian visitors may experience Hydropolis as:

- beautiful
- unreadable
- bureaucratically hostile
- technologically overwhelming
- politically dangerous
- commercially tempting
- wet, reflective, and disorienting
- full of laws they do not understand until after they break them

Human settlement culture is centered on the crystal desert escarpment continent, dieselpunk infrastructure, Traverse logistics, Prime Ops, World Works, and settlement survival.

Hydropolis is not that world.

It is the signal redoubt that humans often misunderstand, fear, exploit, romanticize, or misname.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Do not make Hydropolis just another ordinary continent.
- Do not make Hydropolis fully underground and static.
- Do not make Hydropolis fully migratory and rootless.
- Do not put Hydropolis inside the Anticlinal Craton.
- Do not make humans, Prime Ops, or World Works the owners of Hydropolis.
- Do not make all Antisapiens require Contact Frames.
- Do not make all Antisapiens Conjugate Chimeras.
- Do not make Hydropolis a generic cyberpunk rain city.
- Do not flatten the signal-band districts into simple class neighborhoods.
- Do not overdefine first-contact mechanics too early.
- Do not use Hydropolis to solve the Core or Book One mysteries.
- Do not erase the wet/dry seasonal transformation.
- Do not forget that Hydropolis is both city and redoubt.
