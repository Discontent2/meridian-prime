# Anticlinal Craton

> Canon documentation for the Anticlinal Craton as a major Meridian Prime world-expansion location and Aeonolacertian sovereign homeland / refuge zone.

**Canon Status:** MAIN CANON, EXPANDABLE

## Core Canon Status

The **Anticlinal Craton** is a major Meridian Prime location.

It is canonized as:

- a culturally known but officially distorted Meridian Prime location
- a massive partially exposed geode island-continent
- a sovereign Aeonolacertian homeland on Meridian Prime
- a refuge / re-rooting site after separation from Aeonos
- a sacred geography and political stronghold
- a major multimedia expansion zone
- not the original Aeonolacertian home world
- not a Book One Core explanation
- not directly tied to Tenet Nocsid III's MITE II novella arc unless later canon explicitly develops that connection

Important distinction:

**Aeonos** remains the Aeonolacertian-named home world.

The Anticlinal Craton is the Aeonolacertian homeland / refuge / cultural cradle **on Meridian Prime**, not the species' original planetary origin.

The Anticlinal Craton began as a Development Department side-story concept and is now promoted into main canon in a protected, expandable form.

---

## Known But Officially Distorted

The Anticlinal Craton is culturally known but officially distorted.

World Works / Meridian official framing may describe it as:

- a protected geologic reserve
- a restricted autonomous cultural territory
- a legacy industrial access dispute
- a sensitive ecological / geological zone
- an unusual hydrocrystal basin with limited access protocols

Aeonolacertian framing is different:

World Works breached the Craton shell, found Aeonolacertian warriors at their last stronghold, and abandoned the project because direct conquest would have been too costly.

Canon tone:

**Official Meridian records describe a managed access dispute. Aeonolacertian memory describes a breach, a stand, and a retreat.**

---

## Sovereignty

Aeonolacertians are politically sovereign inside the Anticlinal Craton.

They currently control the Craton's four tunnel-borer gates.

Meridians may visit the Craton, but they are guests.

Traverse Ops is tolerated for trade purposes only.

World Works desires influence over the Craton, especially because of its resources, research value, political leverage, spectacle potential, and the Thunder Egg / Stone Heart, but direct seizure is not currently viable because it would risk mutually assured destruction.

Canon note:

**The Craton is not lost territory. It is held territory.**

---

## Geography

The Anticlinal Craton is a partially exposed geode island-continent.

It should visually evoke a Richat-structure-style domed anticline, but reimagined through Meridian Prime's crystal geology, pressure-world aesthetics, and geode-vault formation.

The Craton is enormous. Traversing around its outer perimeter takes at least four months.

---

## Endorheic Shard Sea

The **Endorheic Shard Sea** is the geography surrounding the Anticlinal Craton.

Access changes seasonally:

- During the two-year dry season, Traverse routes connect the Craton to the main Meridian Prime escarpment continent.
- During the two-year wet season, the routes become water-navigable, and the Craton functions more like an island-continent.

The wet/dry cycle creates major trade, political, military, religious, and storytelling consequences.

---

## Geode Aquifer Theory

The geode-aquifer concept is canonized in a restrained form.

Not all Meridian Prime aquifers are canonized as geodes.

Instead:

Some Meridian Prime aquifer systems, especially in deep-pressure crystal regions, may be immature geode-vaults, hydrocrystal basins, or forming geode systems.

This remains scientifically disputed, regionally variable, and politically sensitive.

Possible interpretations:

- Meridian geology: unusual aquifer / crystal-basin formation
- WWISSaRD science: hydrocrystal geode-vault formation
- Aeonolacertian tradition: the world forming shells, eggs, mouths, and hearts
- World Works resource logic: exploitable crystal-water systems

Canon guardrail:

**Do not let geode-aquifer theory explain all Meridian Prime geology too early.**

---

## Four Tunnel-Borer Gates

There are four known entrances into the Anticlinal Craton, roughly aligned with cardinal directions.

Each entrance was created by a supermassive World Works tunnel-boring machine.

World Works built the tunnel-borers and attempted to breach the Craton.

When the machines broke through the Craton wall, World Works encountered Aeonolacertian warriors at their last stronghold.

The project was abandoned.

The tunnel-boring machines remain in place and now function as:

- monumental industrial gates
- abandoned breach machines
- sovereign threshold markers
- sacred or contested entrances
- infrastructure ruins
- visual reminders of World Works failure
- trade-control points
- pilgrimage / memory sites

Aeonolacertians currently control the gates.

Canon tone:

**World Works made the holes. Aeonolacertians kept the thresholds.**

Open future question:

**What are the names, conditions, and political meanings of each of the four gates?**

## World Maw

The main translated name logic for the Anticlinal Craton is canonized.

Official Meridian translation:

**World Maw**

Common Meridian field name:

**the Maw**

Aeonolacertian meaning:

Closer to **"the mouth of the world that shelters what it could devour."**

Important nuance:

To many Meridians, **World Maw** sounds threatening, hungry, and geologically ominous.

To Aeonolacertians, the meaning is more layered: shelter, danger, birth, memory, consumption, and protection can coexist in the same sacred geography.

Do not invent the original Aeonolacertian word yet unless specifically instructed later.

## Interior Climate and Structure

The Craton interior is lush, hot, wet, and violently alive.

Outsiders may call it a tropical paradise, but this should be treated as an outsider simplification.

Better canon tone:

**The Craton interior is not gentle. It is alive under pressure.**

Inside the Craton are:

- exposed geode walls
- shell-like landforms
- lush wet interior zones
- crystal basins
- primordial wetlands
- old Aeonolacertian stronghold architecture
- trade settlements
- sacred civic spaces
- collapsed geode basins
- Geodeums
- preserved Craton fauna
- the central Inner Anticline
- the Thunder Egg / Stone Heart

## Inner Anticline

The central half-exposed geode inside the Craton is:

**the Inner Anticline**

This was formerly referred to as "the Anticline" in development notes. Use **Inner Anticline** for clarity.

The Inner Anticline is roughly one-eighth the size of the larger Anticlinal Craton.

Its collapsed top opens into a cave-like domed basin with high walls.

Inside the Inner Anticline is a primordial swamp.

At the center of the Inner Anticline stands the Thunder Egg / Stone Heart.

---

## Thunder Egg / Stone Heart

The central freestanding node is known by multiple names and classifications.

World Works / WWISSaRD classification:

**Thunder Egg**

Common Meridian translation:

**Stone Heart**

Aeonolacertian sacred sense:

**"the heart that remembers pressure."**

The Thunder Egg / Stone Heart is a freestanding node the size of a mountain.

It is visually, culturally, politically, and scientifically important.

Different groups interpret it differently:

- Aeonolacertian sacred geography
- Meridian geological anomaly
- World Works resource interest
- WWISSaRD research target
- old mythic landmark
- political leverage object
- spectacle magnet
- possible planetary pressure node
- local mystery

Current canon restraint:

The Thunder Egg / Stone Heart affects belief, politics, ritual, science, and fear.

Do not canonize any proven supernatural, Core, gateway, machine-control, or metaphysical function yet.

The Thunder Egg may matter enormously without being fully explained.

Canon guardrail:

**Everyone acts as if the Stone Heart matters. Nobody outside protected mystery canon can prove exactly how.**

## Geodeums

**Geodeum** is canon terminology.

Definition:

A **Geodeum** is a collapsed or partially opened large-scale geode space repurposed into a civic, ritual, theatrical, athletic, political, or combat venue.

Plural:

**Geodeums**

Optional formal / ceremonial plural:

**Geodea**

Use **Geodeums** in ordinary narration. Reserve **Geodea** for ceremonial, academic, Aeonolacertian, or elevated usage if desired later.

A Geodeum is not just a venue. Its crystal composition affects what kind of gathering belongs there.

Canon line:

**A Geodeum is architecture made from collapse, memory, pressure, and use.**

## Geodeum Types

Geodeums may function as:

- **Civic Geodeums:** theater, ceremony, debate, public mourning, performance
- **Pressure Geodeums:** racing, climbing, endurance contests, route-skill games
- **Blood Geodeums:** historical gladiatorial combat spaces
- **Machine Geodeums:** modern mechanized combat arenas
- **Ritual Geodeums:** seasonal performances tied to wet/dry cycles, ancestry, thunder, pressure, and crystal growth

Some Geodeums may combine these functions.

## Mechanized Gladiatorial Combat

**Mechanized Gladiatorial Combat** is a contested modern Craton spectacle / sport / ritual / exploitation system.

Its nature varies by jurisdiction.

It may be:

- Aeonolacertian cultural evolution in some contexts
- Meridian commercial exploitation in others
- World Works-backed spectacle where permitted
- underground circuit in illegal zones
- contested hybrid in places like the Red Glass Geodeum
- modern pressure rite for some younger pilots
- desecration to some traditionalists
- survival labor to indebted pilots
- entertainment to visitors
- data, leverage, and soft-power to corporate interests

Core rule:

Any and all machines physically operated by a being may be eligible, depending on local rules, jurisdiction, arena class, and contest type.

Examples may include:

- modified construction equipment
- industrial loaders
- armored rigs
- ex-Traverse equipment
- walking industrial mechs
- mining machines
- crane rigs
- tracked haulers
- custom arena machines
- illegal hybrids
- ceremonial machines

Important guardrail:

Keep pilots physically inside or directly operating the machines. This is not primarily a remote drone sport. The danger should remain embodied.

Canon tone:

**Mechanized Gladiatorial Combat is not one thing. It is sport, debt, rite, spectacle, labor, exploitation, fame, and argument.**

## Craton Fauna

Craton fauna are mostly preserved ecological relics, isolated evolutionary survivors, or living descendants of the Craton's protected interior ecology.

Arena creature releases are controversial.

They may be:

- legal in some jurisdictions
- ceremonial in some contexts
- illegal in others
- replaced or intensified by exotic off-Craton creatures when spectacle overrides tradition
- politically contested by Aeonolacertian factions, Meridian visitors, World Works interests, and arena operators

Do not make Craton fauna generic monster bait.

Craton fauna should feel tied to ecology, politics, ritual, spectacle, and survival.

World Works wants influence over the Craton for multiple reasons:

- entertainment / spectacle value
- access to the Thunder Egg / Stone Heart
- control of trade routes
- symbolic victory over an old failure

However, World Works currently cannot take direct control without risking mutually assured destruction.

The Craton is therefore a site of:

- soft power
- trade pressure
- research diplomacy
- entertainment deals
- information distortion
- proxy influence

- cultural exploitation
- covert attempts at leverage

## Visual and Art Direction Notes

The Craton should not look like a generic jungle crater.

It should feel like a continent inside a broken jewel.

Visual motifs:

- concentric crystal land rings
- shell-like escarpment walls
- giant exposed geode faces
- wet-season waterlines etched into crystal
- dry-season salt / crystal flats
- tunnel-borer gates as dead industrial gods
- tropical interior heat against colder exterior world
- steam, pollen, mist, and crystal dust
- hematoid quartz walls glowing red-black like old blood under glass
- Aeonolacertian civic spaces grown from collapse rather than built flat
- arenas that feel ceremonial before they feel commercial
- modified construction rigs with scars, sponsor markings, sacred tags, and illegal mods
- the Thunder Egg / Stone Heart first seen indirectly through reflection, mist, pressure thunder, arena framing, or cultural behavior rather than full explanation

The Red Glass Geodeum should feel like:

**a cathedral, stadium, execution ground, and machine shop fused inside a pressure world.**
