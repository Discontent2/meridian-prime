# Dry Port

> Protected canon for World Works' suspected search for an independent escarpment-side Àæonos contact port.

**Canon Status:** MAIN CANON, PROTECTED MYSTERY / EXPANDABLE

## Core Canon Status

The **Dry Port** is a field name, protected mystery, and geopolitical motive surrounding World Works' search for an independent matter-side contact site outside Hydropolis.

It is canonized as:

- a suspected or theorized escarpment-side contact port
- a possible alternative to Hydropolis-controlled Àæonos visitation
- a major World Works strategic objective
- a protected mystery connected to survey secrecy, route-proofing, and corporate concealment
- not yet proven to exist in public canon
- not yet confirmed as the same thing as the Book One hidden formation
- not a replacement for Hydropolis
- not a full explanation for the Core or gateway mechanics

Core concept:

**Hydropolis controls the only known legitimate, stable, matter-side port for true Àæonos-origin visitation. World Works cannot seize it. So World Works is hunting for a second port.**

## Protected Mystery: Does the Dry Port Exist?

The Dry Port should not be treated as publicly proven canon yet.

Protected canon:

World Works believes there may be an independent escarpment-side conjugate contact site, docking scar, receiving platform, alignment structure, or dead port hidden somewhere outside Hydropolis control.

World Works may be wrong.

World Works may be partly right.

World Works may have found evidence of something older, damaged, misread, or not designed for them.

Reader-facing uncertainty should remain active.

Possible interpretations:

- an ancient failed contact site
- a buried docking scar
- a conjugate alignment structure
- a dead Contact Frame receiving platform
- a dry-season exposure of old port infrastructure
- a false positive World Works is gambling on
- a structure Horus or World Works believes could become an independent Àæonos port
- a non-port phenomenon being misread through corporate desire

Canon guardrail:

**Belief drives action. Proof comes later.**

## Relationship to the Escarpment

The suspected Dry Port is associated with the human-settled crystal desert escarpment sphere rather than Hydropolis or the Anticlinal Craton.

It may be buried under crystal flats, pressure ridges, salt-bright route scars, Claw-adjacent terrain, old infrastructure, or seasonal exposure zones.

This makes it strategically important because World Works already has stronger logistics, route-control, and survey influence across the human escarpment continent than it has inside Hydropolis.

Do not map the Dry Port precisely yet unless later canon demands it.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Do not confirm the Dry Port too early.
- Do not make the Dry Port replace Hydropolis.
- Do not make Hydropolis weak because World Works wants another port.
- Do not turn the Dry Port into a generic alien portal.
- Do not use it to explain the entire Core mystery.
- Do not make Contact Frames optional around true antimatter / conjugate-life visitors.
- Do not make World Works cartoonishly evil; its motive should be control, leverage, fear, profit, and strategic independence.
- Do not make Horus's interest purely commercial.
- Do not reveal the full Dry Port hypothesis to Tenet too early unless the novella structure is deliberately revised.
- Do not make the Claw formation definitely the Dry Port until later canon explicitly decides that.
- Do not make Hydropolis unaware or passive; Antisapian factions should have stakes in World Works' search.
