# The Blackstep Province

> Main canon file for the volcanic basalt apron and wet-season threshold system surrounding or partially surrounding the Anticlinal Craton.

**Canon Status:** MAIN CANON, EXPANDABLE

## Core Canon

**The Blackstep Province** is an ancient volcanic basalt apron, partial ring, or broken Large Igneous Province associated with the Anticlinal Craton.

It gives the Craton believable shore logic during wet season.

During dry season, it reads as black stepped land.

During wet season, it becomes the Craton's docking reef, harbor wall, sacred border, and wet-season threshold system.

Core line:

**The Craton rises from geode heart, but it meets the wet season on black volcanic steps.**

## Seasonal Forms

### Dry Season

During dry season, the Blackstep Province is exposed.

It appears as:

- black basalt steps
- lava benches
- cracked volcanic flats
- columnar basalt walls
- old flow shelves
- obsidian / black-glass ridges
- dry docking terraces
- pressure-marker roads
- no-anchor stones standing in air
- old wet-season landing marks visible as scars

Dry-season function:

- land approach control
- threshold roads
- pressure-marker avenues
- dry inspection terraces
- Craton gate-apron outer ring
- place where wet-season docks are repaired and ritually prepared

Map label:

**Blackstep Province: Exposed Basalt Terraces / Dry Threshold Roads**

### Cataract Season

During cataract season, water begins cutting between the basalt steps.

It appears as:

- flooding channels
- black-water cuts
- newly dangerous ledges
- waterfall roar in basalt slots
- routes becoming piers
- docking terraces waking early

Cataract-season function:

- approach closure management
- ritual transition from road protocol to water protocol
- preparation of wet gates
- rescue / evacuation complexity

Map label:

**Blackstep Province: Wet Gates Waking / Channels Opening**

### Haboon Season

During haboon season, dust, flood, and basalt terrain combine into a violent access maze.

It appears as:

- black cliffs in dustwater haze
- flashwater over basalt shelves
- waves breaking across lava benches
- pressure resonance in stepped channels
- ship or convoy confusion near half-drowned terraces

Map label:

**Blackstep Province: Storm Shelf / Approach Not Advised**

### Wet Season

During wet season, the Blackstep Province becomes the Craton's maritime border system.

It appears as:

- docking islands
- basalt reefs
- columnar sea cliffs
- hidden shoals
- tidal channels
- blackstone harbor pockets
- wet-season causeways
- sacred water gates
- no-anchor zones
- ritual waiting waters

Wet-season function:

- controlled docking
- sacred landing protocol
- maritime threshold enforcement
- Craton island diplomacy
- anti-piracy defense
- smuggler hazard zone

Map label:

**Blackstep Province: Docking Reefs / Sacred Wet Gates**

## Wet-Season Docking Logic

The Craton should not have open, easy beaches.

Wet-season approach happens through controlled basalt landings.

Possible docking structures:

- basalt terrace docks
- columnar basalt piers
- sheltered tidal cuts
- blackstone harbor pockets
- rope-and-chain mooring fields
- cliff elevators or pressure lifts
- sacred landing gates
- no-anchor zones
- ritual waiting waters
- gate-drums or pressure warnings

Craton principle:

**Water approach is still threshold.**

## Three-Ring Craton Map Model

Future maps may show the Craton with three related rings or zones:

1. **Geode Core / Mountain Body**
   - sacred Craton mass
   - Geodeums
   - World Maw relationship
   - pressure interior

2. **Blackstep Province / Basalt Apron**
   - volcanic terrace and docking ring
   - dry-season threshold roads
   - wet-season reefs and sacred gates

3. **Seasonal Water Boundary**
   - dry road access in dry season
   - rising channels in cataract season
   - storm shelf in haboon season
   - wet docking access in wet season

This prevents the Craton from feeling like a mountain that magically becomes an island without shore logic.

## Political Consequences

### Craton / Aeonolacertian Authority

The Craton controls the true docks.

A ship does not simply arrive.

It must be received.

The Blackstep Province becomes the Craton's wet-season threshold architecture.

Possible Craton phrase:

**The mountain bled black so strangers would learn where to stop.**

Treat this as religious / mythic language, not literal geology unless later approved.

### Hydropolis

Hydropolis has wet-season navigation and signal expertise, but cannot treat the Blackstep docks like ordinary ports.

Hydropolis maps may show:

- signal shadows along basalt cliffs
- reflection chaos
- sealed docking silence
- forbidden approach channels
- identity-check delays at sacred wet gates

### World Works

World Works will likely want to survey or access the Blackstep Province.

Corporate term:

**Igneous Access Terrace Zone**

Translation:

**We want a port we can control.**

### Prime Ops

Prime Ops may need emergency or diplomatic wet-season access but lacks natural authority there.

This creates dependence on:

- Craton permission
- Hydropolis navigation
- route timing
- docking protocol
- possibly Free Scab smuggler knowledge, if desperation gets ugly

### Free Scabs / Eschar Slough

The Free Scabs may know illegal approaches through outer basalt shoals.

They may prey on ships heading toward Craton wet gates.

Free Scab saying:

**Every sacred dock casts a smuggler's shadow.**
