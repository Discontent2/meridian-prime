# Prime Plateau

> Main canon file for the mid-elevation staging shelf and contested logistics hinge between Prime Ops, the Great Cut, and the lower Dry Circuit / Mirror Basin routes.

**Canon Status:** MAIN CANON, EXPANDABLE

## Core Canon

The **Prime Plateau** is a major mid-elevation plateau / shelf system on the Meridian Prime Escarpment Continent.

It sits below high Prime Ops / escarpment territory and above the lower basin routes leading toward the Mirror Basin, Hydropolis, Craton approaches, and Dry Circuit lowlands.

Working route structure:

```text
Prime Ops
  ↓
major canyon system / The Great Cut
  ↓
The Prime Plateau
  ↓
lower descent gates and canyon roads
  ↓
Mirror Basin / Dry Circuit
```

Core line:

**Prime Ops owns the launch. The Prime Plateau owns the decision. The canyon owns everyone who gets arrogant.**

---

## Function

The Prime Plateau exists to make Traverse logistics, seasonal hydrology, and cartography believable.

Without a mid-elevation staging shelf, the descent from Prime Ops to basin routes would be too direct, too cliff-like, and too operationally brittle for regular Traverse use.

The Prime Plateau gives the descent a hinge.

Convoys use it to:

- stop after the first descent leg
- cool brakes and engines
- refuel
- repair track strain
- inspect cargo lashings
- shelter during haboon conditions
- wait for basin clearance
- wait for Hydropolis signal permission
- stage rescue equipment
- transfer cargo to descent-rated vehicles
- decide whether to continue, turn back, split, or camp

Canon line:

**The Dry Circuit begins at Prime Ops, but operationally begins at the Prime Plateau.**

## Seasonal States

### Dry Season

The Prime Plateau is active.

Functions:

- major staging shelf
- descent checkpoint
- refuel and repair zone
- end-of-shift camp cluster
- cargo redistribution point
- route decision node
- evacuation reserve station

Map status:

**Prime Plateau: Active Mid-Elevation Staging Shelf**

### Cataract Season

The Prime Plateau becomes an evacuation and hazard-management zone.

Functions:

- last safe high shelf before descent routes break
- cataract observation zone
- emergency shelter cluster
- controlled retreat point
- rescue staging area

Map status:

**Prime Plateau: Cataract Watch / Route Breakage Likely**

### Haboon Season

The Prime Plateau becomes a storm refuge and wind / flood danger zone.

Functions:

- storm shelter
- convoy holdfast
- rescue choke point
- dust-wall observation point
- flash-flood warning station

Map status:

**Prime Plateau: Storm Hold / Descent Not Advised**

### Wet Season

The Prime Plateau may remain partly accessible from the high escarpment while lower descent routes are closed, snow-locked, iced, or cut off.

Functions:

- snowfield edge support
- upper descent closure point
- winterized logistics shelf
- highland isolation refuge
- observatory of drowned lower routes

Map status:

**Prime Plateau: Snowline Support / Lower Routes Closed**

## Political Stakes

Whoever controls the Prime Plateau controls practical access to the Dry Circuit.

Stakeholders include:

- Prime Ops, which claims operational authority
- World Works, which claims infrastructure and survey rights
- NCI and other contractors, who hold old route knowledge
- local plateau settlements, which defend survival rights
- the Bought Nation, which treats the plateau as home, work zone, and political wound
- Hydropolis, which may seek monitoring or witness rights during dry / cataract transitions
- Craton observers, who may watch for pressure disturbances if canyon systems align with deep geology or old breach scars

World Works may call parts of the plateau a conservation zone when restriction is useful and an access corridor when extraction is useful.

Canon line:

**The Prime Plateau is where protected land, leased land, route land, and lived land all argue in the fog.**
