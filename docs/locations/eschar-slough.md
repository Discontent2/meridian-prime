# The Eschar Slough

> Main canon file for the Eschar Slough, the hidden seasonal archipelago / slough homeland of the Free Scabs.

**Canon Status:** MAIN CANON, EXPANDABLE / PROTECTED LOCATION

## Core Canon

**The Eschar Slough** is a brutal basin archipelago / slough territory that appears as dead scabland during dry season and becomes a hostile island-and-channel system during wet season.

It is the hidden homeland and base-system of the Free Scabs.

In dry season, outsiders see:

- cracked flats
- black crust
- salt scars
- dead flood terrain
- exposed stone ridges
- hydrocrystal scabfields
- miserable mesas
- abandoned flood towers

In wet season, the water rises around the ridges and reveals their true strategic value:

**The Eschar Slough becomes pirate country.**

Core line:

**The Free Scabs are dry-season bandits and wet-season pirates, living in an archipelago everyone mistakes for dead scabland until the water rises around it.**

---

## Name Meaning

### Eschar

An eschar is dead, dark, hardened tissue over a wound or burn.

In Meridian Prime usage, the word evokes:

- black crust
- old burns
- dead surface over living damage
- wound-memory
- hard scab over unresolved injury

### Slough

Slough carries multiple meanings:

- wetland
- backwater
- mud-channel
- skin or dead tissue shedding off
- rot and renewal
- dangerous softness under a hard surface

Together, **The Eschar Slough** means:

**A wetland made from a wound that never healed right.**

Pronunciation and usage:

- officials may say **slough** as *sloo*, emphasizing wetland geography
- Traverse crews may say **slough** as *sluff*, emphasizing dead skin and scab-shedding
- Free Scabs may use both, depending on whether they mean the water or the wound

Possible Free Scab saying:

**Say slough when you sail it. Say sluff when it takes skin.**

## Seasonal Identity

### Dry Season: Scabland Bandit Country

During dry season, the Eschar Slough is mostly exposed.

It appears as:

- scabland ridges
- salt flats
- dead flood channels
- crackled basin crust
- dry reef structures
- sinkfield margins
- miserable mesas
- ruined mooring remains

Free Scab functions:

- convoy ambushes
- salvage runs
- black-market exchange
- dry-channel travel
- hidden cistern control
- buried boat maintenance
- vehicle approaches to the Scab Cathedral
- route intelligence trading

Dry-season line:

**When the water leaves, the Scabs grow wheels.**

### Cataract Season: The Locking Season

During cataract season, land approaches begin closing.

The Free Scabs move goods upward and boats outward.

The Slough becomes:

- rising channels
- cut-off ridges
- unstable evacuation routes
- flood gates without gates
- rescue / ransom territory
- water and road confusion

Cataract-season line:

**The Slough locks one road at a time.**

### Haboon Season: Storm Raider Country

During haboon season, dust walls collide with flood sheets.

The Eschar Slough becomes a chaos machine.

Free Scab functions:

- storm raiding
- false-beacon wrecking
- rescue rackets
- blackwater smuggling
- zero-visibility movement
- hidden cargo transfer
- stormfront ambushes

Haboon-season line:

**The map cannot see them because the weather is helping.**

### Wet Season: Pirate Archipelago

During wet season, the Eschar Slough becomes a real island-and-channel system.

The Free Scabs turn to piracy.

Wet-season operations:

- raid barges
- hijack floating cargo
- prey on maritime routes
- attack weak convoy flotillas
- smuggle people between islands
- run black harbors
- loot submerged route caches
- sell passage through dangerous waters
- interfere with Hydropolis wet-season traffic
- hide in channels official ships avoid

Wet-season line:

**When the roads drown, the Scabs grow sails.**

The **Bone Channels** are the dry-season approach maze through the Eschar Slough.

- dry cuts
- scab ridges
- salt-white channel beds

## Why Authorities Fail to Control It

### Meridian / Prime Ops

Reads the Slough as low-value, high-risk seasonal terrain. Dry-season patrols can enter only at cost. Wet-season patrols need boats, which shift jurisdiction and logistics.

### World Works

Sees possible strategic value but struggles to justify open investment in terrain officially classified as unstable, contaminated, or unusable. May conduct black surveys.

### Hydropolis

Sees wet-season piracy as a direct signal and maritime threat. Signal mapping is complicated by mineral scatter, dead channels, stolen beacons, blackwater reflections, false identity packets, and storm interference.

### Craton

May consider the Slough guestless, wounded, ritually wrong, or outside proper threshold law. Craton exiles inside the Free Scabs make intervention spiritually complicated.

### Bought Nation

Knows too many of its runaways may have vanished into the Slough. It may hate the Free Scabs and still secretly hope some lost children survived there.
