# Sandbox Promotion Rules

> Canon-governance rules for reviewing sandbox material before promotion into Meridian Prime main canon.

**Canon Status:** MAIN CANON GOVERNANCE, EXPANDABLE

## Purpose

Repair sandbox scale turbulence by requiring every sandbox idea to declare its canon weight before promotion review.

Core problem:

The sandbox creates excellent material, but planetary laws, major civilization spaces, regions, factions, organisms, substances, sports, market items, jokes, and side-story flavor cannot all enter canon through the same gate.

Canon principle:

**Not every good idea weighs the same.**

---

## Core Review Philosophy

The sandbox should remain wild.

Promotion should be disciplined.

The sandbox may generate:

- apocrypha
- alternate traditions
- visual experiments
- side-story concepts
- local lore
- candidate canon
- new systems
- contradictory myths
- speculative science
- jokes, slogans, customs, and texture

Main canon should only absorb what has the right weight, dependencies, and guardrails.

Core principle:

**A sandbox idea becomes dangerous when no one knows how much it weighs.**

## Canon Weight Ladder

### Tier 1: Planetary Law

Rules affecting geology, seasons, physics, ecology, maps, first contact, or all regions.

Examples:

- geode-aquifer theory as a planet-wide claim
- matter / antimatter contact rules
- full first-contact mechanics
- global seasonal mechanics
- all-region map logic

Promotion rule:

Requires full canon review. Never promote from a side-story file alone.

Promotion risk:

**Planetary Risk / Physics-Cosmology Risk / Protected Mystery Risk**

---

### Tier 2: Major Civilization Space

A major people-place system.

Examples:

- Hydropolis
- Anticlinal Craton
- human Escarpment settlement sphere
- any future Aeonos or Àæonos civilization-space file

Promotion rule:

Requires topology, jurisdiction, culture, and protected-mystery review.

Promotion risk:

**High Risk / Protected Mystery Risk / Canon Bloat Risk**

---

### Tier 3: Region / Route System

A major geography, route ecology, or access system.

Examples:

- Dry Circuit
- Mirror Basin
- Prime Plateau
- Blackstep Province
- Eschar Slough
- The Great Cut / Cataract Ladder

Promotion rule:

Requires seasonal access matrix and adjacency review.

Promotion risk:

**Moderate to High Risk / Jurisdiction Risk / Scale Risk**

---

### Tier 4: Settlement

A city, town, hidden base, port, yard, or focused settlement.

Examples:

- Charterhold
- Ledger Falls
- Scab Cathedral
- future Prime Ops focused file
- future Mirror Basin settlements

Promotion rule:

Requires economy, route role, jurisdiction, and faction relationship.

Promotion risk:

**Moderate Risk / Canon Bloat Risk**

---

### Tier 5: Faction

A political, social, outlaw, civic, religious, corporate, labor, or subcultural group.

Examples:

- Free Scabs
- Reclaimers
- Null Court
- Veilknives
- framewright guilds
- Craton gate authorities

Promotion rule:

Requires relationship map, authority limit, and **not secretly responsible for everything** guardrail.

Promotion risk:

**Moderate Risk / Book One Risk / Jurisdiction Risk**

---

### Tier 6: Institution

A formal organization with operational authority or technical scope.

Examples:

- World Works
- Prime Ops
- NCI
- WWISSaRD
- framewright guilds
- Hydropolis licensing authorities

Promotion rule:

Requires authority boundaries, operational limits, and failure logic.

Promotion risk:

**High Risk if it touches Book One or protected mysteries. Moderate Risk otherwise.**

---

### Tier 7: Local Custom

A repeated ritual, habit, custom, law, holiday, social practice, hospitality code, or local tradition.

Examples:

- signal surrender
- receipt holidays
- threshold rituals
- no-anchor ceremonies
- route-family repair customs

Promotion rule:

Can stay sandbox until repeated use proves value.

Promotion risk:

**Low to Moderate Risk**

---

### Tier 8: Organism

A creature, plant, fungus, food species, ecological hazard, sacred fauna, or route-impacting lifeform.

Examples:

- Corpse Lantern Brides
- megafauna
- mega-fungi
- Signal Kelp
- Pressure Cedars

Promotion rule:

Must answer seasonal route effect, settlement effect, and political ecology.

Required questions:

- What does it do when the roads drown?
- Who maps it?
- Who eats, fears, worships, taxes, smuggles, or protects it?
- What route, law, settlement, or faction does it change?

Promotion risk:

**Low to Moderate Risk, unless it affects planetary ecology.**

---

### Tier 9: Substance

A fictional medicine, fuel, intoxicant, poison, distillate, contraband material, ritual compound, industrial material, or body-derived product.

Examples:

- Chrono-Floquet Distillate
- Ghost Fuel
- fictional medicines
- route-prohibited stimulants
- fauna pharmacopoeia materials

Promotion rule:

Must avoid real-world procedure and define story consequence.

Do not include:

- real preparation methods
- dosage instructions
- extraction procedures
- real-world drug or poison analogues presented as instruction
- actionable trafficking or manufacturing detail

Promotion risk:

**Safety / Substance Risk / Book One Risk if connected to Rob's death-chain.**

---

### Tier 10: Market Item

A trade object, charm, sticker, route token, black-market good, souvenir, small commodity, tool, or status item.

Examples:

- stickers
- charms
- route tokens
- false permits
- black-market goods
- ritual tags

Promotion rule:

Usually sandbox flavor unless it becomes plot-relevant or iconic.

Promotion risk:

**Low Risk / Canon Bloat Risk**

---

### Tier 11: Side-Story Flavor

A slogan, joke, chant, visual motif, local phrase, one-off color, name variant, minor rumor, or scene texture.

Examples:

- field jokes
- local insults
- one-off chants
- side-story slogans
- non-recurring visual details

Promotion rule:

Default sandbox. Promote only if it becomes an iconic recurring signal.

Promotion risk:

**Low Risk / Canon Bloat Risk**

---

## Required Sandbox Promotion Header

Future sandbox files proposed for canon review should include:

```text
Canon Weight:
Sandbox Status:
Promotion Risk:
Primary Canon Dependencies:
Protected Mystery Contact:
Route / Jurisdiction Impact:
Seasonal Impact:
Story Use:
Recommended Review Path:
```

### Header Guidance

**Canon Weight** should use the ladder above.

**Sandbox Status** should identify whether the file is Development Apocrypha, Candidate Canon, Side-Story Sandbox, Visual Development, Game Development, Mythic Development, or Do Not Promote.

**Promotion Risk** should use the labels below.

**Primary Canon Dependencies** should name files or systems that must be respected.

**Protected Mystery Contact** should state whether the idea touches the Core, Dry Port, Book One, Rob's death-chain, gateway mechanics, Aeonos / Àæonos, the Thunder Egg / Stone Heart, or other protected material.

**Route / Jurisdiction Impact** should state whether the idea changes who can move where and who controls the crossing.

**Seasonal Impact** should state what changes across dry, cataract, haboon, wet, freeze, and drawdown conditions.

**Story Use** should state whether the idea is a main arc pillar, side-story engine, local texture, game mechanic, visual-development aid, or one-off flavor.

**Recommended Review Path** should state whether to hold, revise, promote to candidate canon, promote to main canon, or mark do-not-promote.

## Promotion Risk Labels

Use one or more labels:

- **Low Risk**: local flavor or narrow addition.
- **Moderate Risk**: affects a known region, faction, or repeated practice.
- **High Risk**: affects major story, faction balance, route topology, or civilization-space logic.
- **Planetary Risk**: changes planet-wide geology, ecology, physics, seasonality, cartography, or first-contact consequences.
- **Protected Mystery Risk**: touches the Core, Dry Port, Rob's death-chain, gateway mechanics, first contact, Thunder Egg / Stone Heart, or other protected mysteries.
- **Book One Risk**: changes Tenet / MITE II / Rob / Horus / Chapter 1-9 reveal structure.
- **Physics / Cosmology Risk**: affects matter / antimatter rules, Conjugate Contact Frames, Chimeras, Aeonos, Àæonos, or other hard-cosmology logic.
- **Safety / Substance Risk**: involves fictional medicines, intoxicants, poisons, distillates, body-derived materials, or contraband pharmacopoeia.
- **Canon Bloat Risk**: adds names, items, customs, creatures, factions, or jargon without enough structural use.

---

## Contradiction Labels

Contradictions should be labeled before review.

### Productive Ambiguity

A deliberate tension that deepens the world and should remain unresolved for now.

### Protected Mystery

A contradiction or gap intentionally preserved because it touches a core mystery.

### Accidental Inconsistency

A contradiction likely created by development drift and needing cleanup.

### Scale Problem

A local idea accidentally affects the planet, or a planetary idea is treated as local flavor.

### Jurisdiction Problem

An idea changes authority, entry, law, permission, gates, route access, or ownership without addressing who controls the threshold.

### Timeline Problem

An idea conflicts with when events, settlements, technologies, species, or institutions exist.

### Naming Problem

Multiple terms blur or conflict without in-world reason.

### Canon-Weight Problem

An idea is strong but entering the wrong promotion tier.

---

## Promotion Review Questions

Before promoting a sandbox idea, ask:

1. How much does this idea weigh?
2. What canon files does it depend on?
3. What route, threshold, gate, map, or jurisdiction does it affect?
4. What season changes it?
5. Does it touch a protected mystery?
6. Does it distort Book One?
7. Does it make Meridian Prime smaller or larger?
8. Does it solve a problem or merely add decoration?
9. Does it duplicate an existing idea under a new name?
10. Does it need to be canon now, or can it remain useful sandbox material?

---

## Default Promotion Paths

### Hold in Sandbox

Use for beautiful but non-urgent material.

### Revise Before Review

Use when the idea is promising but unclear in weight, jurisdiction, seasonality, or dependency.

### Candidate Canon

Use for strong ideas that deserve main-canon review but are not yet approved.

Candidate Canon should be rare.

### Main Canon Promotion

Use when the idea has passed weight, dependency, jurisdiction, seasonal, and protected-mystery review.

### Do Not Promote

Use when the idea is useful only as experiment, alternate continuity, or creative compost.

Do Not Promote is not failure. Sometimes compost feeds better worlds.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Do not promote every good name.
- Do not promote every side-story concept.
- Do not promote planetary laws from local scene needs.
- Do not make every sandbox idea relevant to Book One.
- Do not let a cool organism become planet-wide ecology by accident.
- Do not make every faction secretly responsible for everything.
- Do not let protected mysteries leak through promotion notes.
- Do not let the sandbox become tame.
- Do not let canon become shapeless.
- Do not let review become so strict that invention dies in paperwork.
