## Orbital Locations

### The Lodestar

**Canon Status:** CONFIRMED CANON

**Location Type:** generation ship, orbital city, corporate headquarters, archive-citadel.

**Primary Faction:** World Works Corporation.

**Public Description:** The Lodestar is the great generation ship that carried humanity to Meridian Prime over the 200-year Traverse. From the surface, it appears as a large moving artificial star with red blinking lights.

**Hidden Truth:** The Lodestar is not merely a relic or headquarters. It is the surviving command body of a compromised mission, the site where the Auto-Coup converted mission authority into controlled corporate continuity.

**Visual Aesthetic:** solarpunk glass, orbital sunlight, cultivated gardens, polished corridors, corporate wellness imagery, and old mission bones hidden beneath clean surfaces.

**Primary Function:** World Works headquarters, orbital command center, archive repository, shuttle destination, and visible symbol of continuity.

**Story Uses:** seat of power, forbidden archive, luxury prison, orbital pilgrimage, corporate cathedral, final reveal site.

**Hazards / Taboos:** unauthorized travel is heavily restricted; certain Traverse-era sections may be sealed; public visitors see only curated zones.

**Connected Locations:** World Works Executive Arcology, The Traverse Archives, The Solar Gardens, The Red Dot Observatory, Shuttle Docking Spine, Prime Ops Space Port.

**Open Questions:** Which original Lodestar decks remain untouched by World Works? What sealed systems still obey pre-corporate mission authority?

#### World Works Executive Arcology

The corporate command habitat inside The Lodestar. It projects calm, green, solarpunk order while functioning as the decision-center for planetary governance, security, continuity doctrine, and archive control.

#### The Traverse Archives

The archival heart of The Lodestar. Public archives are curated. Restricted archives may contain records of the Poisoned Contact, the False Parley, the Surface Insurgency, and the Auto-Coup.

#### The Solar Gardens

Highly controlled gardens aboard The Lodestar. They serve as food source, oxygen system, status symbol, diplomatic theater, and proof that World Works can make life appear harmonious.

#### The Red Dot Observatory

An orbital observation and monitoring site within The Lodestar. Publicly, it is a viewing chamber where approved personnel observe Meridian Prime below. Hidden uses may include tracking surface unrest, shuttle movement, anomaly data, dissident signals, and memory-corruption blooms.

#### Shuttle Docking Spine

The major orbital docking structure where authorized shuttles connect to The Lodestar. It is heavily monitored and may be the only place most surface travelers ever touch the ship.

## Surface Human Locations

### Prime Ops Space Port

**Canon Status:** CONFIRMED CANON

**Location Type:** surface-orbit transit hub, security choke point, controlled infrastructure zone.

**Primary Faction:** Prime Ops Authority under World Works influence.

**Public Description:** Prime Ops Space Port is the primary authorized launch and landing facility connecting Meridian Prime’s surface to The Lodestar.

**Hidden Truth:** Prime Ops was deliberately built in a non-anomalous location. The site was chosen to be as far from Aeonolacertian and áæbsconditian activity as possible, avoiding entanglement instability and early native-world conflict so human infrastructure could grow unimpeded.

**Visual Aesthetic:** dieselpunk launchworks, fortified transit halls, old shuttle gantries, security gates, pressure doors, soot-stained service tunnels, corporate-clean executive corridors.

**Primary Function:** control movement between surface and orbit while preserving operational stability and continuity control.

**Story Uses:** border crossing, interrogation site, shuttle departure, smuggling route, disappearance point, first view of The Lodestar from a launch window.

**Hazards / Taboos:** unauthorized access, forged transit documents, native artifacts, memory-corrupted cargo, unregistered passengers, dissident signal devices.

**Connected Locations:** Restricted Shuttle Yards, The Lodestar, Shuttle Docking Spine, Customs and Continuity Processing, Executive Transit Wing.

**Open Questions:** Who chose the site? Did World Works already know enough about anomalies to avoid them before official settlement?

#### Customs and Continuity Processing

A bureaucratic-security complex inside Prime Ops. It checks identity, cargo, travel authorization, psychological stability, and continuity-risk classification.

#### Executive Transit Wing

A polished, separate transit path for World Works executives, high-value personnel, and carefully managed diplomatic guests.

#### Cargo Quarantine Vaults

Secure holding chambers for suspect cargo, native materials, damaged archive devices, and items exposed to anomalous regions.

### Restricted Shuttle Yards

**Canon Status:** CONFIRMED CANON

**Location Type:** militarized shuttle maintenance yard, restricted heritage machinery site.

**Primary Faction:** Prime Ops Authority and World Works security.

**Public Description:** The yards maintain the original well-built Lodestar shuttles that still travel between Prime Ops and orbit.

**Hidden Truth:** The shuttles are more than transport. They are original mission hardware, engineered for impossible reliability and wrapped in ritual, procedure, and corporate secrecy. Their maintenance records may reveal discrepancies between official mission history and the hidden A-30 crisis chain.

**Visual Aesthetic:** old heat-shield plating, gantry cranes, oil-black maintenance pits, brass warning plates, corporate lockouts, armed catwalks, shrine-like tool walls.

**Primary Function:** preserve, repair, guard, and deploy the original shuttles.

**Story Uses:** heist, sabotage, desperate launch, mechanic informant, hidden compartment discovery, chase through engine scaffolds.

**Hazards / Taboos:** unauthorized entry can mean disappearance; shuttle interiors may contain sealed Traverse-era systems; old hardware may know more than current operators.

**Connected Locations:** Prime Ops Space Port, Shuttle Docking Spine, Cargo Quarantine Vaults.

**Open Questions:** Are all original shuttles accounted for? Did any shuttle make unauthorized contact runs before A0?

### Meridian Capital City / Name TBD

The central human surface city of Meridian Prime. Its name remains open. It likely grew outward from the stable Prime Ops zone, becoming a dieselpunk civic machine of administration, labor, education, rationing, propaganda, and ordinary Meridian life.

### Diesel Districts

Industrial human districts marked by pressure systems, smoke, rail, engines, oil, soot, machine shops, repair guilds, crowded housing, and stubborn everyday life.

### The Commissary District

**Canon Status:** PROVISIONAL CANON

**Location Type:** surface market, ration hub, worker district, civic supply quarter.

**Primary Faction:** Meridians, with World Works oversight and Continuity Dissident presence.

**Public Description:** The Commissary District is where ordinary Meridians obtain approved goods, food allocations, machine parts, work meals, official supplies, and household necessities.

**Hidden Truth:** It is also a rumor-exchange, black-market pressure valve, dissident contact point, and memory reservoir. Recipes, repair tricks, family sayings, and contraband objects may carry fragments of unofficial history.

**Visual Aesthetic:** steam vents, ration counters, iron shutters, food stalls, grease lamps, public notice boards, repair kiosks, hand-painted signs, security drones overhead, families moving through soot and warmth.

**Primary Function:** feed and supply the surface population while allowing World Works to monitor civilian needs, scarcity, and unrest.

**Story Uses:** street-level introduction, first clue, dissident recruitment, ordinary family life, black-market exchange, public crackdown.

**Hazards / Taboos:** unlicensed trade, forbidden archive fragments, unregistered native artifacts, anti-continuity pamphlets, suspicious memory episodes in crowds.

**Connected Locations:** Diesel Districts, Continuity Education Halls, Dissident Signal Dens, Prime Ops Space Port.

**Open Questions:** Which vendor family knows the oldest unofficial history? What item appears repeatedly in Commissary contraband seizures?

### Continuity Education Halls

Schools, civic indoctrination centers, and public-history institutions where Meridians learn approved mission history, public doctrine, and the comforting meaning of Control over Continuity.

### Dissident Signal Dens

Hidden rooms, basement transmitters, roofline relay nests, and illegal broadcast points used by Continuity Dissidents to circulate forbidden records, contradict official history, and gather memory-corruption testimony.

## Aeonos Native Locations

### Aeonolacertian Hidden Enclaves

Hidden survivor settlements, sanctuaries, or fortress-communities of the Aeonolacertians. They may be concealed by terrain, ancient technology, memory distortion, or World Works suppression.

### Steam-Oracle Foundries

**Canon Status:** PROVISIONAL CANON

**Location Type:** Aeonolacertian technology sanctum, foundry-temple, continuity workshop.

**Primary Faction:** The Steam-Oracles.

**Public Description:** Unknown to most Meridians, or misidentified as ruins, volcanic machinery, or primitive native temples.

**Hidden Truth:** The foundries operate advanced Aeonolacertian systems hidden inside brass, stone, pressure tanks, pistons, valves, clockwork, lenses, carved machinery, and ritual instruments.

**Visual Aesthetic:** brass ribs, stone boilers, pressure gauges, steam plumes, bone resonators, scale-etched control wheels, living alloys, geothermal glow.

**Primary Function:** maintain continuity machinery, repair memory-pressure systems, craft or awaken ancient devices.

**Story Uses:** revelation that primitive-looking technology is advanced, ritual-science scenes, memory repair, siege defense, machine awakening.

**Hazards / Taboos:** touching unvented pressure systems; misreading ritual commands as superstition; entering without heat discipline; speaking during calibration.

**Siege / Soundscape Notes:** In crisis, foundry alarms may sound through tuned steam whistles in rhythmic **Xis-tl-Xis-tl** patterns. Commands can be transmitted through clicks, hisses, and glottal stops that cut through battle noise.

**Connected Locations:** The Aeolipile Pressure Chambers, Pressure-Memory Wells, Central Boiler Sanctums, Ruins of the Sundering of Scales.

**Open Questions:** Which foundry still contains a machine that remembers the False Parley?

### The Aeolipile Pressure Chambers

**Canon Status:** PROVISIONAL CANON

**Location Type:** sect chamber, pressure-memory engine, acoustic-continuity site.

**Primary Faction:** The Aeolipile, within the Steam-Oracles.

**Public Description:** If seen by humans, the chambers are often mistaken for boiler-priest sanctuaries or engine monasteries.

**Hidden Truth:** The chambers are advanced continuity instruments operated through steam, breath, scale vibration, rotating chambers, pressure vessels, acoustic resonance, and the Bellows Rite.

**Visual Aesthetic:** rotating brass spheres, venting arms, throat-resonance alcoves, heated stone floors, condensation curtains, copper lung-engines, pressure glyphs.

**Primary Function:** stabilize memory, diagnose continuity fractures, anchor damaged records, and set stored memory back into motion.

**Story Uses:** memory-restoration ritual, non-human science lesson, dangerous calibration scene, cross-species trust test.

**Hazards / Taboos:** broken rhythm can rupture a stored memory; cold bodies can disrupt heat grids; unauthorized recording is forbidden.

**Siege / Soundscape Notes:** The Aeolipile may use commands such as **K’atli-tlax tzk’!** meaning “Hold the line and maintain body heat,” literally “Lock scales for warmth.” In defensive huddles, armor plates click together in **tl-tl-tl** patterns to create conductive heat grids.

**Connected Locations:** Steam-Oracle Foundries, Central Boiler Sanctums, Pressure-Memory Wells.

**Open Questions:** Can the Aeolipile repair planetary memory, or only reveal how badly it was broken?

### Pressure-Memory Wells

Ancient Aeonolacertian sites where memory is stored or stabilized through pressure, geothermal resonance, bio-mechanical systems, and analog-looking machinery.

### Central Boiler Sanctums

**Canon Status:** PROVISIONAL CANON

**Location Type:** civic engine-heart, inner sanctum, siege objective.

**Primary Faction:** Aeonolacertians, especially Steam-Oracles and defensive class-lines.

**Public Description:** Unknown or misunderstood by humans as primitive power rooms or religious boiler shrines.

**Hidden Truth:** A Central Boiler Sanctum may function as a city’s Mother-Heart: power source, thermal commons, continuity stabilizer, memory organ, and civic sacred center.

**Visual Aesthetic:** enormous vertical boiler-hearts, brass arteries, heat pools, scale-marked walls, steam-lit defensive bridges, armored hatchways, glowing pressure cores.

**Primary Function:** provide heat, power, memory pressure, and symbolic unity for Aeonolacertian cities.

**Story Uses:** siege climax, defense of the city-heart, moral dilemma, last stand, awakening of ancestral systems.

**Hazards / Taboos:** cooling the Mother-Heart may kill more than infrastructure; enemies who breach the sanctum may collapse civic memory; defenders may be oath-bound to die before retreat.

**Siege / Soundscape Notes:** A desperate rally cry here is **Q’at-atli na-q’at!**, translated as “Protect the Central Boiler at all costs,” literally “Blood for the Mother-Heart!” Artillery officers may command **Xisssss-tlatl q’at-xis!**, meaning “Vent the steam-guns! / Fire!” literally “Hiss-release the pressure-weapons!”

**Connected Locations:** Steam-Oracle Foundries, The Aeolipile Pressure Chambers, Ruins of the Sundering of Scales.

**Open Questions:** Which Central Boiler Sanctum fell during the Catastrophic Interaction, and which one still runs?

### Ruins of the Sundering of Scales

**Canon Status:** PROVISIONAL CANON

**Location Type:** ruined Aeonolacertian battle/catastrophe site, memory wound, forbidden city remains.

**Primary Faction:** Aeonolacertians; contested by World Works, áæbsconditian agents, and truth-seekers.

**Public Description:** If known to humans, the site is misclassified as a geological scar, archaeological exclusion zone, or unstable ruin field.

**Hidden Truth:** The ruins preserve evidence of the Catastrophic Interaction from the Aeonolacertian perspective and may contain proof that human interference shaped the disaster.

**Visual Aesthetic:** cracked brass infrastructure, fossilized steam lines, collapsed scale-stone towers, vitrified pressure chambers, giant broken valves, silent whistle towers.

**Primary Function:** memorial, warning, evidence site, and possible battlefield record.

**Story Uses:** revelation site, haunted ruin, first encounter with Aeonolacertian survivor memory, pursuit across unstable machinery.

**Hazards / Taboos:** memory echoes, pressure ghosts, unstable machinery, territorial guardians, records that can overwrite false recollection.

**Siege / Soundscape Notes:** The ruins may still contain dead alarm-whistles that occasionally vent fragments of the old **Xis-tl-Xis-tl** warning pattern. Brass armor fragments may click in wind, imitating battle huddle signals.

**Connected Locations:** Central Boiler Sanctums, Steam-Oracle Foundries, The Thousandfold Rupture Zone.

**Open Questions:** Was this where the Aeonolacertians first realized the crisis had been manipulated?

## àæonos / áæbsconditian Locations

### The Null Court

The ruling or elite strategic seat of áæbsconditian power. It may exist physically on àæonos, through anti-matter architecture, or through projected influence into Aeonos-adjacent perception.

### Veilknife Transit Zones

Hidden routes used by Veilknives for stealth movement, phase transit, memory masking, and covert operations. These zones may be physical, resonant, or perceptual rather than conventional roads.

### The Amaranth Schola

**Canon Status:** PROVISIONAL CANON

**Location Type:** áæbsconditian archive-laboratory-priesthood, resonance academy, memory-signal institution.

**Primary Faction:** The Amaranth Schola.

**Public Description:** Unknown to most humans and possibly known only in distorted form to Aeonolacertians.

**Hidden Truth:** The Schola studies resonance, signal, memory-patterns, dreams, and cross-world perception. Its Madrigalists act as field-practitioners who hear fractures before others can see them.

**Visual Aesthetic:** black glass, amaranth light, teal machine optics, quiet halls, neural lattice choirs, suspended signal chambers, reflective void surfaces.

**Primary Function:** research and manipulate reality through frequency, perception, and record.

**Story Uses:** dream-contact, memory intrusion, forbidden education, anti-matter archive, uneasy alliance with Livid Æ Revisionists.

**Hazards / Taboos:** being perceived by the Schola may alter the perceiver; translated signals may not remain translated; some rooms may exist only while remembered.

**Connected Locations:** Madrigalist Signal Chambers, Livid Æ Revisionist Archives, Veilknife Transit Zones.

**Open Questions:** What song, signal, or record are the Madrigalists trying to recover?

### Madrigalist Signal Chambers

Specialized resonance chambers used by Madrigalists to manipulate encrypted signals, dreams, memory patterns, and cross-world perception through harmonic cybernetic systems.

### Last Thousand Identity Vaults

Protected áæbsconditian vaults preserving names, genealogies, identity records, continuity ledgers, and survival data from the post-catastrophe population.

### Livid Æ Revisionist Archives

Forbidden historical archives maintained by dissident áæbsconditian truth-seekers who suspect the official catastrophe narrative is false or incomplete.

## Anomalous and Catastrophe Sites

### The Thousandfold Rupture Zone

**Canon Status:** PROVISIONAL CANON

**Location Type:** matter/anti-matter catastrophe scar, memory-corrupted region, forbidden evidence field.

**Primary Faction:** contested; World Works, Aeonolacertians, áæbsconditians, and Continuity Dissidents all have reasons to seek or suppress it.

**Public Description:** Official maps may mark the region as hazardous geology, military exclusion, atmospheric instability, or non-civic terrain.

**Hidden Truth:** The zone is one of the central scars of the Catastrophic Interaction. It may preserve overlapping evidence from Aeonos, àæonos, the Surface Insurgency, and the Poisoned Contact.

**Visual Aesthetic:** impossible shadows, pressure-burned stone, anti-light glimmers, brass wreckage, glassed soil, mismatched echoes, memory blooms, areas that seem to remember different versions of the same event.

**Primary Function:** wound, proof, hazard, and convergence point.

**Story Uses:** major revelation, expedition, faction clash, memory trial, final-act descent into the original lie.

**Hazards / Taboos:** unstable memory, false recollection, matter/anti-matter resonance, predatory archive phenomena, World Works quarantine forces.

**Connected Locations:** Ruins of the Sundering of Scales, The First Contact Scar, Entanglement Faults, Memory-Corrupted Regions.

**Open Questions:** Does the zone reveal what happened, or does it make every witness remember a different truth?

### The False Parley Site

The hidden location, signal-node, or contact chamber where human intermediaries corrupted communication between Aeonos and àæonos. It may not be a single physical place.

### The First Contact Scar

A site where the first covert human contact with one or both native civilizations left detectable evidence. It may be physical, archival, or memory-resonant.

### Entanglement Faults

Regions where Aeonos and àæonos overlap imperfectly through resonance, memory bleed, signal distortion, or continuity instability.

### Memory-Corrupted Regions

Areas where records, personal memory, geography, and public history become unstable. These are not safe transit zones and are avoided by World Works infrastructure when possible.
